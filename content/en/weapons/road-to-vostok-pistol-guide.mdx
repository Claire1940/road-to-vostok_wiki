## Core Weapon Handling Mechanics

Road to Vostok prioritizes realism over arcade-style shooting. Weapons have physical weight, fire selectors, and adjustable tactical elements. When using a pistol, you must be aware that it is not just a "backup" but a sophisticated tool with its own set of rules.

Unlike many shooters where weapons are static on your screen, here they interact with the environment. If you stand too close to a wall, your character will pull the weapon back. Understanding the "physicality" of your sidearm is the first step toward mastery.

### Fire Selectors and Inspection
Most pistols in the game are semi-automatic, but some specialized machine pistols offer full-auto capabilities. You can inspect your weapon to check the chamber and the fire selector's position. 

| Action | Control (Default) | Tactical Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inspect Weapon** | Press **I** | Check fire mode and ammo status visually. |
| **Switch Fire Mode** | Press **V** | Toggle between Single and Full-Auto (if supported). |
| **Rotate View** | Mouse Scroll (During Inspect) | See the opposite side of the firearm for selector switches. |
| **Manual Chambering** | Press **R** (Hold/Tap) | Essential for clearing jams or loading a round manually. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Eastern-style firearms often have fire selectors on the right side. Use the scroll wheel during inspection to verify your current mode before engaging enemies.

## Managing Arm Fatigue and Stances

One of the most unique features of Road to Vostok is the arm fatigue system. Holding a pistol in a ready position or aiming down sights (ADS) consumes stamina. If your fatigue meter bottoms out, your aim will sway uncontrollably, making it nearly impossible to land a headshot on a bandit.

### High Stance vs. Low Stance
You can toggle your weapon stance using the mouse scroll wheel while not in ADS mode. This is a critical tactical decision you must make constantly.

*   **High Stance:** Your weapon is up and ready at the shoulder (or compressed ready for pistols). This allows for the fastest possible transition to ADS or point-firing. However, this stance **drains arm fatigue over time**. Use this only when entering a room or rounding a corner where you expect immediate contact.
*   **Low Stance:** Your weapon is lowered. This does not drain arm fatigue and allows your character to move slightly faster. This should be your default state when traveling across the massive maps.

| Stance | Fatigue Drain | Readiness | Recommended Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Stance** | **Yes** | Maximum | Room clearing, CQC, expected contact. |
| **Low Stance** | **No** | Medium | Long-distance travel, looting, resting. |

## Finding the Weapon Specialist

To get the most out of any **road to vostok pistol guide**, you need to know where to find the best hardware. The Weapon Specialist is the third trader you will likely encounter, and he is the only one who consistently stocks high-tier pistols, "meta" weapon attachments, and body armor.

### Location Guide: The Outpost Bunker
The Weapon Specialist is located in the **Outpost** map, tucked away in a fortified bunker. 

1.  **Transit to School:** From your starting cabin, follow the waterline to the yellow tape and cut left to enter the School zone.
2.  **Reach the Outpost:** Stay on the main highway through the School zone, passing the Doctor's location, until you find the transit point to the Outpost.
3.  **Locate the Bunker:** Once in the Outpost, head left toward the tent shelter (the second shelter in the game). 
4.  **Find the Tank:** Continue past the shelter toward the edge of the map. Look for a decommissioned tank sitting in front of a rock formation.
5.  **Enter the Bunker:** The entrance is directly behind the tank. Inside, you will find the Weapon Specialist.

> **💡 Tip:** If the Weapon Specialist's inventory is empty or lacks the armor plates you need, wait for the in-game shop reset. Armor is vital in 2026 to prevent being "one-tapped" by bandits with high-caliber pistols.

## Tactical Pistol Attachments

Even a basic sidearm can become a powerhouse with the right attachments. In Road to Vostok, attachments are physical and affect weapon handling.

*   **Lasers:** These are invaluable for pistols. When using a laser, you can utilize the "canted stance" or simply point-fire with high accuracy without draining as much fatigue as full ADS.
*   **Suppressors:** While they increase the length of the weapon (making it more likely to "collide" with walls), they are essential for stealthy players.
*   **Flashlights (ARVA/Polaris):** If you don't have night vision goggles yet, a weapon-mounted light or a pocket torch like the ARVA is mandatory for nighttime raids.

## Survival Tips for Sidearm Users

Using a pistol in Road to Vostok requires a different mindset than using a rifle. Here are the top tactical tips for 2026:

1.  **Manual Racking:** Remember that after reloading a completely empty pistol, you must manually rack the slide. You can practice this in the tutorial area to build muscle memory.
2.  **Canted Aiming:** If you have a laser attached, use the canted stance for close-quarters battles. This provides a better field of view than traditional iron sights.
3.  **Leg Meta:** If you encounter an enemy with heavy body armor and you only have a low-caliber pistol, aim for the legs. Armor plates only protect the torso.
4.  **Duct Tape and Sticks:** Always carry materials to craft splints. A broken leg while carrying a heavy primary and a sidearm will make you a sitting duck for bandits.

## FAQ

**Q: Does every pistol require manual racking in Road to Vostok?**

A: If you reload a pistol that still has a round in the chamber, you do not need to rack the slide. However, if the slide is locked back on an empty magazine, you must release the slide or rack it after inserting a fresh mag.

**Q: Where can I buy more ammo for my pistol?**

A: Basic ammunition can be found with the Generalist trader, but for armor-piercing rounds and specialized pistol calibers, you should visit the Weapon Specialist in the Outpost bunker.

**Q: How do I stop my pistol from swaying so much?**

A: Excessive sway is usually caused by high arm fatigue. Drop your weapon into a "Low Stance" using the scroll wheel to let your arms rest, or crouch to stabilize your aim. Following this **road to vostok pistol guide** and managing your stances is key to accuracy.

**Q: Can I find suppressors for my starting pistol?**

A: Yes, suppressors can be found as rare world loot in military areas or purchased directly from the Weapon Specialist if your reputation and funds are sufficient.
