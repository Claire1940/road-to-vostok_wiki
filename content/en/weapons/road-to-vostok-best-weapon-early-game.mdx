> **Tip:** When using the PM5, focus on short, controlled bursts. Even though it is an SMG, the 2026 physics engine rewards trigger discipline over "spray and pray" tactics.

## Weapons to Avoid in the Early Game

While loot is scarce, some weapons are more of a liability than an asset. The SA85 is the primary offender in this category. During range testing, the SA85 scored a dismal 8. The impact points were described as "peppering the wall," indicating a lack of consistency that can lead to missed shots even at close range.

Using an unreliable weapon increases your "Time to Kill" (TTK), giving enemies more opportunities to return fire. In a hardcore survival game like Road to Vostok, you should prioritize a weapon with a lower fire rate but higher accuracy over a high-caliber rifle that you cannot control.

### Ranking the "Avoid" List:
*   **SA85:** Extreme horizontal spread; unpredictable recoil.
*   **Worn Scavenger Rifles:** High jam probability; degraded accuracy stats.
*   **Short-Barreled Shotguns (without slugs):** Extremely limited range in open forest tiles.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute road to vostok best weapon early game for a new player?**

**Q: Where can I find the RK62 in Road to Vostok?**

A: The RK62 can often be found in military checkpoints or as rare loot in guard towers. It is also occasionally carried by higher-tier AI scavengers near the border crossing.

**Q: Is the M4A1 worth using if I can't find an RK62?**

A: Yes, the M4A1 is a solid mid-tier weapon. While it scored lower (16) in accuracy tests than the RK62 or K416, it is highly moddable. Adding a foregrip and a compensator can help close the performance gap.

**Q: Why is the SA85 considered bad for the early game?**
