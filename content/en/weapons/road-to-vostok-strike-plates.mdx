<Section icon={Swords} title="Types of Strike Plates and Their Tactical Use">

Road to Vostok features a variety of strike plates, each with distinct properties that cater to different combat scenarios and playstyles. Selecting the right plate for your mission can drastically alter your chances of success. From lightweight options for rapid movement to heavy-duty plates for fortified positions, understanding each type is crucial.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ceramic Plates">
    - **Excellent ballistic protection** against high-velocity rounds.
    - Lighter than steel, allowing for better mobility.
    - More susceptible to cracking and shattering from multiple impacts.
    - Ideal for aggressive pushes or defending against precision fire.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Steel Plates">
    - **High durability** and resistance to multiple hits.
    - Heavier, which can impact stamina and movement speed.
    - May cause spalling (fragments of bullet/plate material) that can injure exposed areas.
    - Suited for static defensive roles or when expecting heavy, sustained fire.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Composite Plates">
    - **Balanced protection and weight**, often using layers of different materials.
    - Offers a good compromise between ceramic's protection and steel's durability.
    - Can be more expensive or harder to acquire.
    - Versatile choice for most engagements, adaptable to various tactics.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Soft Armor Inserts">
    - **Lightweight and flexible**, primarily for shrapnel and low-velocity threats.
    - Provides minimal protection against direct ballistic impacts.
    - Often used as a secondary layer or for non-combat roles.
    - Best for light scouting or when mobility is paramount over direct combat.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Spalling Hazard">
Be aware that **steel plates** can cause spalling. While they protect your torso, fragments might hit unprotected limbs or face, causing additional damage or bleeding.
</Callout>

Each plate type also comes with different trade-offs in terms of weight, cost, and availability. A heavy plate might offer superior protection but will slow you down, making flanking maneuvers difficult. Conversely, a lighter plate allows for agility but might not withstand a direct hit from a powerful rifle.

</Section>

| Scenario | Recommended Plate Type | Justification |
