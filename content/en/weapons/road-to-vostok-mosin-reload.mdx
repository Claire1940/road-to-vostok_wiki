<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering the Road to Vostok Mosin Reload Mechanics">

Reloading the Mosin-Nagant in Road to Vostok is a multi-stage process that emphasizes realism. Unlike games where a "magazine" is a generic resource, this game tracks every individual round. If you have three loose rounds in your pocket and two in the rifle, you have exactly five shots. There is no magical pool of ammunition.

To perform a successful **road to vostok mosin reload**, you must follow a specific sequence. The game uses a physics-based inventory system, meaning the physical location of your ammo matters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Cycle the Bolt">
    After firing, your character must manually cycle the bolt to eject the spent casing and chamber a new round. If the rifle is empty, the bolt will remain open, signaling it is time to load.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access Loose Rounds">
    Open your inventory or use your quick-binds to select individual 7.62x54R rounds. In the 2026 build, keeping ammo in your tactical vest provides the fastest access.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manual Insertion">
    Your character will insert rounds one by one into the internal magazine. You can interrupt this process at any time by sprinting or switching weapons, which is vital if a bandit pushes your position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Close the Action">
    Once the desired number of rounds (up to 5) are inserted, the bolt must be pushed forward and locked. The rifle is now ready to fire.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Reloading Vulnerability">
During the manual insertion phase, you are completely defenseless. Because the Mosin reloads one round at a time, it is often better to load just two rounds and return to the fight rather than waiting for a full five-round reload while under fire.
</Callout>

</Section>
