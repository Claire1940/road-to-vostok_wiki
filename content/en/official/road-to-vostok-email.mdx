## Mastering the Shelter and Save Mechanics

In Road to Vostok, your shelter is your only sanctuary. It acts as a "time capsule" where the outside world does not exist until you step through the door. Understanding how the game handles saving and world persistence is crucial to avoiding progress loss.

*   **Saving Progress:** Your game only saves when you physically enter your shelter. 
*   **World Resets:** Every time you leave the shelter, the AI enemies and loot pools respawn at new, randomized locations.
*   **Loot Management:** Use the shelter's containers to store valuable items. You can customize the interior using the building system (F1 menu).

| Action | Keybind (Default) | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Enter/Exit Shelter** | **F** (Interact) | Saves game / Respawns world |
| **Open Build Menu** | **F1** | Allows furniture placement |
| **Place Item** | **G** | Confirms placement in shelter |
| **Rotate Item** | **Right Mouse** | Adjusts orientation of furniture |

## The Trader System: Taxes and Progression

The economy in Road to Vostok is governed by a dynamic tax system. When you first encounter a trader, you may notice that prices are significantly higher than the item's base value. This is because the "Border Tax" is applied to all transactions. If you find the pricing unfair, you can contact the community or check the **road to vostok email** updates for balance changes, but the intended way to lower these costs is through gameplay.

To reduce the tax percentage, you must complete tasks for the traders. These tasks range from retrieving specific items like fishing rods to clearing out bandit camps. As your reputation increases, the tax decreases, allowing you to purchase high-tier armor and weapons at a sustainable price.

> **💡 Tip:** Early in the game, focus on selling small, high-value items like battery cables and electronics to build up a cash reserve before attempting to buy expensive firearms.

## Weapon Handling: The Kar98 and Shotgun Mechanics

New players often struggle with specific weapon types like the Kar98 or pump-action shotguns. These weapons require manual cycling and loading that differs from standard automatic rifles. If you find your character is not shooting, it is likely because the chamber is empty or the manual reload sequence hasn't been completed.

1.  **Tap Control:** This removes your hand from the forend or grip.
2.  **Left Mouse Button:** While in the "manual" state, this allows you to feed individual shells or rounds into the weapon.
3.  **Tap Control Again:** Returns your hand to the firing position.
4.  **Press R:** Cycles the bolt or pump to chamber the round.

This level of realism is what sets Road to Vostok apart, but it can be frustrating if you aren't prepared for it during a firefight. Practice these movements inside your shelter where it is safe.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the official Road to Vostok email for bug reports?**

A: The most reliable way to report bugs is through the official Discord server or the Steam Discussion forums. However, for formal business or press inquiries, the **road to vostok email** can typically be found on the official website or the developer's social media "About" sections.

**Q: Does the game have permanent death (Permadeath)?**

A: Yes, Road to Vostok features a "permadeath" mechanic where dying in the Vostok zone results in the permanent loss of your character and all loot currently on your person. Items stored in your shelter remain safe, but the stakes are much higher than in traditional shooters.

**Q: Can I play Road to Vostok offline?**

A: Yes, the game is designed as a single-player experience. While you need an internet connection to download updates and potentially sync certain data, the core gameplay loop does not require a constant connection to a server.

**Q: How do I lower the high prices at the trader?**

A: Prices are dictated by the Border Tax. To lower this tax, you must complete tasks and missions for the trader. This represents your growing influence and trust within the local area, eventually leading to more favorable trade rates.
