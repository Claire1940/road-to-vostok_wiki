## Understanding the Hardcore Game Modes

Whether you eventually play the **road to vostok xbox** edition or the current PC build, choosing the right game mode is the first step toward survival. The game does not hold your hand, offering several distinct ways to experience the border zone.

### Standard Mode
This is the recommended starting point for new players. You begin in a shelter with a basic starting kit and neutral weather conditions. It allows you to learn the map transitions and loot pools without immediate, overwhelming pressure.

### Darkness Mode
For those seeking a more traditional "roguelike" survival start, Darkness mode spawns the player in a random location without a shelter or starting gear. The world simulation parameters are randomized, making every run unpredictable.

### Iron Man Mode
The ultimate challenge. In Iron Man mode, the entire game world becomes a permadeath zone. If you die anywhere on the map, your save file is deleted, and all progression is lost. This mode is intended for veteran players who have mastered the AI behaviors and map layouts.

| Mode | Starting Gear | Spawn Point | Death Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Basic Kit | Shelter | Gear Loss (Map Dependent) |
| **Darkness** | None | Random | Gear Loss |
| **Iron Man** | None | Random | **Permanent Save Deletion** |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always run the tutorial at least once. The weapon handling—specifically the need to manually load magazines and check calibers—is significantly more complex than standard shooters.

## Tactical Weapon Mechanics and Ballistics

The heart of the gameplay lies in its realistic treatment of firearms. Unlike arcade shooters, weapons in this world require constant maintenance and a deep understanding of compatibility.

1.  **Magazine Management**: You cannot simply press "R" to refill your total ammo count. You must find compatible magazines (e.g., AKM mags for 7.62x39mm weapons) and manually move loose rounds into them via the inventory screen.
2.  **Weapon Inspection**: Using the **X** key allows you to inspect your weapon, checking the remaining rounds in a magazine and identifying the fire mode.
3.  **Caliber Specifics**: Ammunition is divided by penetration levels. Higher-tier armor (like Level 4 plates) requires specific armor-piercing rounds to defeat effectively.
4.  **Attachments**: Scopes like the ACOG or suppressors like the PBS-1 can be found in the world. However, they must be compatible with the specific rail system of your firearm.

## The World Map: Zones and Transitions

The game world is divided into three distinct zones, each increasing in difficulty and reward. Navigating these requires managing your vitals and understanding the "Travel Cost" associated with moving between maps.

### Area 0-5: The Starting Zone
This area includes maps like the Village and the Highway. It is relatively low-risk, featuring bandits with lower-tier equipment. It is the best place to find basic medical supplies and food.

### The Border Zone: Medium Tier
As you push toward the center of the map, you encounter the School, Outpost, and the infamous Minefield. The AI here is better equipped, often appearing as "Guards" with automatic weapons and body armor.

### Vostok: The Permadeath Zone
Vostok is the final destination. It is a high-risk, high-reward area where the "Red Skull" icon appears on your HUD. **Death in Vostok results in the deletion of your save file, regardless of your game mode.** This is where the rarest loot and most dangerous military AI reside.

## Survival Essentials: Medical and Shelters

Survival isn't just about winning gunfights; it's about managing your character's physical condition over a long-term simulation.

### The Medical System
Medical conditions are displayed via icons on your HUD. If an icon turns red, the condition is active and will worsen if not treated.
*   **Bandages**: Used to stop bleeding.
*   **Splints**: Necessary for broken limbs, which severely impact movement and aim.
*   **Pills/Medkits**: Used to restore overall health and treat infections.

### Shelter Customization
Shelters serve as your safe havens. In the 2026 version of the game, players can unlock multiple shelters across the map. These areas allow you to:
*   **Save Progress**: Secure your loot and character state.
*   **Decor Mode**: Use the furniture catalog to customize your living space.
*   **Storage**: Keep extra weapons and ammo for future runs.

> **Warning:** AI can occasionally patrol near shelters. Ensure the area is clear before attempting to enter or exit, as you are most vulnerable during transitions.

## AI Factions and Combat Strategy

The AI in this world is divided into three distinct tiers, each with unique behaviors and equipment. Understanding who you are fighting is the key to choosing your engagements.

*   **Bandits**: Low-tier enemies often found in the starting zones. They typically use pistols or shotguns and lack significant armor. They are aggressive but poorly organized.
*   **Guards**: Professional security forces found in the Border Zone. They use tactical equipment, utilize cover effectively, and often travel in small squads.
*   **Military**: The elite forces of the Vostok zone. They have the highest accuracy, use high-penetration ammo, and wear heavy body armor. Engaging them without a plan is often fatal.
