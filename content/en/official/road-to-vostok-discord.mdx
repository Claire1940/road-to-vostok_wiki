## Survival Mechanics: Beyond Combat

Road to Vostok distinguishes itself through its "Medical and Mental" systems. Survival isn't just about winning gunfights; it's about managing your character's physiological state. In 2026, the game features a "Mental Stability" stat that fluctuates based on your environment and actions.

*   **Hydration and Energy:** Essential for maintaining stamina and focus.
*   **Mental Health:** Impacted by bad weather or poor food; mitigated by "comfort items" like cigarettes or snacks.
*   **Physical Health:** Managed through realistic medical supplies; there is no "auto-regeneration."

The looting system is entirely physics-based. This means that items do not simply "spawn" in a UI list; they exist as physical objects in the world. You might find a magazine tucked under a car seat or a medical kit hidden behind a crate. The "Tetris-style" inventory management requires you to prioritize items based on their barter value rather than a traditional currency system.

> **⚠️ Warning:** In the Vostok zone (Russia), death is final. If you die across the border, your entire save—including your shelters and stored loot—is wiped. Only enter this zone when you are fully prepared.

## The Developer's Roadmap and Transparency

Antti Lehtinen’s background as a former Finnish military officer informs the game's commitment to realism and professional development standards. By walking away from publisher deals to maintain 100% independence, the developer ensures that all revenue from the 2026 launch goes directly back into the "contractor pipeline."

This pipeline allows the project to scale quickly by activating a network of specialized contractors for art, sound, and coding without the overhead of a traditional studio. The in-game "Road to Vostok" button provides a live look at upcoming features, which currently includes doubling the map count, adding more complex trader tasks, and introducing seasonal weather transitions from summer to winter.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the official road to vostok discord link?**

A: The link is typically found on the official project website and the Steam store page. It is the primary place for developer updates and community competitions.

**Q: Is the game multiplayer or co-op?**

A: No. Road to Vostok is strictly a single-player experience. The developer has stated that the focus remains on creating a high-quality, atmospheric solo survival journey.

**Q: What happens if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: If you die in the Vostok zone, you lose everything. This includes your character, all gear, your shelters, and all stored items. The game essentially resets to the beginning.

**Q: How does the trading system work without money?**

A: The game uses a pure barter system. Items have relative values based on their utility. For example, medical supplies or tools might be worth several magazines or food items depending on the trader's needs.
