## Mastering the Decor Mode (F1)

To begin personalizing your space with a **road to vostok poster** or other furniture, you must first master the **Decor Mode**. Unlike the standard inventory management, Decor Mode provides a specialized interface for physical manipulation of the environment.

By pressing **F1** (which can be remapped in your settings), you activate a tactical overlay. In this mode, every piece of furniture—from heavy sofas to small wall frames—becomes interactable. You can grab objects, adjust their distance from your character, and rotate them to fit perfectly into corners.

### Decor Mode Control Scheme

| Action | Keybind (Default) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Toggle Decor Mode** | F1 | Switches between standard UI and furniture manipulation. |
| **Grab/Place Item** | Left Mouse Button | Picks up or drops the selected furniture/decor. |
| **Rotate Object** | Mouse Wheel / R | Precisely aligns the item's orientation. |
| **Grid Snapping** | G | Toggles alignment to help avoid "ugly gaps" between items. |
| **Furniture Catalog** | Tab (In Decor Mode) | Opens the Tetris-style storage for unplaced furniture. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Grid Snapping feature when placing multiple wall elements. It ensures that your collection of posters and paintings remains level and professional-looking.

## How to Acquire the Road to Vostok Poster and Furniture

In the 2026 exclusion zone, you cannot simply buy everything from a high-end catalog. Decor items must be scavenged, traded for, or crafted using raw materials found in the world. The **road to vostok poster** and similar wall-mounted items are often categorized as "Wall Elements," meaning they have specific placement logic that requires a vertical surface.

### 1. Trading with Specialized NPCs
Traders are your primary source for high-quality aesthetics. For example, the trader known as **Grandma** specializes in domestic goods. While generalists might carry basic tools, Grandma is the go-to source for carpets, fabric-related items, and decorative frames. 

### 2. The Furniture Crafting System
If you cannot find the specific item you want, you can craft it at a workbench within your shelter. Crafting furniture has a "Shelter Proximity" requirement, meaning you must be inside your base to finalize the build.

| Craftable Item | Required Materials | Utility |
|---|---|---|
| **Military Bed** | 2 Sleeping Pads, 2 Pillows, 1 Sleeping Bag | High-quality spawn point. |
| **Single Bed** | Lumber, Blanket, Mattress Roll, 1 Pillow | Standard recovery station. |
| **Wall Shelf** | 2 Lumber, 4 Nails | Adds vertical storage for loot. |
| **Decor Frame** | 1 Lumber, 1 Glass, 1 Paper | Used to display posters and maps. |

### 3. Scavenging and Looting
Keep an eye out for "Painting Related Furniture" in residential areas. While rare, these items can be added to your **Furniture Catalog** and transported back to your main base.

## The Furniture Catalog and Tetris Management

One of the most innovative features of the 2026 update is the **Furniture Catalog**. When you are redecorating a crowded shelter like the "Cabin," the space can become cluttered quickly. Instead of throwing items on the floor, you can "store" them in your catalog.

The catalog uses a Tetris-style inventory system. Large items like fridges and sofas take up significant blocks of space, while a **road to vostok poster** or a small stool takes up very little. This system allows you to transfer your entire interior design from one shelter to another. If you decide to move your primary operations from the starting Cabin to a more fortified location, you can pack your favorite decor into the catalog and redeploy it instantly at the new site.

## Item Grouping and Advanced Placement

A common frustration in survival games is having to move every individual item when you want to shift a table. Road to Vostok solves this through **Item Grouping**. 

When you place items—such as a lantern, an axe, or a small radio—on top of a piece of furniture, they become "grouped" with that object. If you enter Decor Mode and move the table, all the items resting on it will follow the table's new position. This applies to wall elements as well; if you have a shelf with a **road to vostok poster** hanging just above it, the system recognizes the spatial relationship, making it easier to maintain your aesthetic layout.

## Modding and Custom Furniture

For players who want to go beyond the standard assets, the developer has introduced the **Furniture Component** tool. This development tool allows the community to convert static 3D models into dynamic, interactable furniture. 

By using this component, modders can bring custom textures and models into the game. This means the variety of posters, including community-designed **road to vostok poster** variants, will likely expand exponentially as the modding tools are finalized. The tool automatically generates colliders and logic, ensuring that any custom object behaves exactly like the official assets.

> **Warning:** When using modded furniture, ensure your game version matches the mod requirements to avoid "invalid shader" errors during placement.

## Strategic Multi-Shelter Support

The 2026 update emphasizes the use of multiple shelters. You are no longer tethered to a single home. You might have a "Main Base" filled with comforts and a **road to vostok poster** on every wall, but you should also maintain "Bugout Shelters" along the game world.

These smaller outposts should be functional. Use the catalog to move basic beds and storage containers to these locations. While you might not spend as much time decorating a tent in the woods, having a familiar layout can help you navigate your storage quickly during a high-stress raid.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Road to Vostok poster in the Early Access?**

A: Posters and paintings are typically found as loot in residential buildings or purchased from specialized traders like Grandma. You can also craft frames to display paper-based items you find in the world.

**Q: Can I move furniture between different shelters?**

A: Yes. By using the Furniture Catalog in Decor Mode, you can "store" an item in one shelter and "place" it in another, provided you have the inventory space in your catalog.

**Q: Does furniture provide any gameplay buffs?**

A: While primarily aesthetic, certain furniture items like beds are required for setting spawn points and recovering health/stamina. Containers also provide organized storage for your loot.

**Q: How many furniture recipes are available?**

A: As of the 2026 Early Access update, there are over 20 different furniture crafting recipes, ranging from basic stools and shelves to military-grade beds and refrigerators.
