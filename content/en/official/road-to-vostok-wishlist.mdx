## Navigating the Final Demo Maps

In the current 2026 demo phase, players can explore three distinct maps, each offering unique challenges and environmental storytelling. These areas are far more expansive than previous iterations, rewarding those who take the time to scout before moving.

1.  **The Village:** The starting point for most survivors. It features an abandoned settlement occupied by bandits and serves as the home of the "Generalist" trader.
2.  **The Border Zone:** A high-tension area that acts as a transition point. It is more open and requires careful movement to avoid long-range engagements.
3.  **The Apartments:** A newly introduced urban environment that emphasizes verticality and close-quarters combat. This map is notorious for its lack of lighting, making flashlights or matches essential.

> **Warning:** The transition points between maps are not just loading screens; they are tactical choke points. Ensure you have cleared the immediate area before attempting to move to the next zone.

## Survival Mechanics and Player Wellness

Survival in Road to Vostok is a meticulous process. There are no experience points or skill trees to bail you out; your survival depends entirely on your knowledge of the mechanics and your ability to manage your physical state. The bottom-left of your UI tracks several critical wellness metrics that you must monitor constantly.

### Core Wellness Metrics

| Metric | Impact of Depletion | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hunger** | Reduced stamina and focus | Canned goods, cooked fish |
| **Thirst** | Rapid stamina drain | Clean water, sodas |
| **Health** | Death / Game Over | Medical kits, bandages |
| **Temperature** | Shaking / Accuracy loss | Shelter, fire, warm clothing |

The 2026 update introduced a robust fishing mechanic, allowing players to find sustainable food sources in the wild. This adds a layer of "quiet" gameplay, where you must weigh the benefit of gathering food against the risk of being spotted in the open by a roaming patrol.

## The Shelter System: Your Safe Haven

One of the most praised additions to the **road to vostok wishlist** experience is the player shelter. Unlike many survival games where your "base" is just a menu, Road to Vostok provides a physical cabin that you can fully furnish and arrange.

The shelter acts as your persistent hub. Here, you can:
*   **Edit Furniture:** Use a dedicated control mode to place tables, storage crates, and beds.
*   **Store Loot:** Stockpile weapons and provisions for future runs.
*   **Skip Time:** Use the bed to sleep through the dangerous night hours, though this requires you to have enough food and water to sustain the rest.
*   **Plan Transitions:** Use the map layouts within your cabin to plan your next route into the Border Zone.

## Trading and the Generalist NPC

Economy plays a vital role in your progression. Currently, the Generalist is the primary NPC you will interact with. He is located within the Village, though his location has been moved in the 2026 version to a more fortified building.

Interacting with the Generalist is not as simple as a standard RPG shop. There is a "Stranger Tax" applied to all new players, making items significantly more expensive. To reduce this tax, you must complete tasks—small missions that involve fetching specific items or clearing out localized threats. This reputation system ensures that you feel like an outsider earning your place in a hostile world.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't sell your only flashlight to the Generalist for quick cash. The darkness in the Apartments map is absolute, and you will regret the sale the moment the sun goes down.
