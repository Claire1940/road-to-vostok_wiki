## Getting Started: The Tutorial and Basic Mechanics

Before diving into the open world, every player should complete the integrated tutorial. It introduces the inventory system (Tab), weapon inspection (X), and fire mode toggles (F). Understanding your Carry Capacity (CC) is vital; you start with a limited capacity of 10, which can be significantly expanded by equipping vests and backpacks found in the world.

### Weapon Handling and Maintenance
Weaponry in Road to Vostok is realistic. You must manually load magazines by dragging compatible ammo types onto them in your inventory. 

| Action | Keybind | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Inspect Weapon** | X | Check ammo count and magazine status |
| **Fire Mode** | F | Toggle between Semi, Auto, or Safety |
| **Quick Equip** | Alt + Left Click | Instantly move items to active slots |
| **Lower Weapon** | Mouse Wheel Down | Lowers your profile and weapon stance |

> **💡 Tip:** Always perform an ammo check after a skirmish. Running into a building with an empty chamber is the fastest way to end your run in the Border Zone.

## Road to Vostok Main Quest Walkthrough: Trader Tasks

Progression in the current 2026 build is primarily driven by the "Generalist" trader. Completing his deliveries not only reduces the trader tax but also rewards you with essential survival tools that cannot be easily found elsewhere. Following this **road to vostok main quest walkthrough** for tasks will ensure you are battle-ready for the Vostok crossing.

### Phase 1: Basic Survival Needs
The first set of tasks focuses on hygiene and comfort. These are low-risk and can be completed by looting the initial houses in Area 05.

| Task Name | Required Items | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Hygiene First** | Toilet Paper, Lotion | Portable Light/Flashlight |
| **The Smoker** | 2x Smokes, Matches | **Alarm Clock** (Essential for time tracking) |
| **Back Pain** | 4x Painkillers | High-tier Medical Supplies |

### Phase 2: Technical Progression
Once you have the basics, the Generalist will ask for technical components. These items are heavier and often found in industrial crates or garages.

1.  **The Mechanic:** Deliver a Toolbox, Bucket, Tape, Nails, and Batteries. This unlocks the **Police Helmet**, providing vital protection against headshots from Bandits.
2.  **The Electrician:** Gather a Car Battery, Cables, Inverter, and a Jerry Can. This is one of the most important steps in the **road to vostok main quest walkthrough** as it rewards you with **Night Vision (Nods)**, allowing for safe travel during the dangerous night cycles.

## Advanced Quests: The Path to High-Tier Gear

To survive the military-grade AI in the Border Zone, you will need better firepower than the starting pistols or scavenged shotguns.

### The Fisherman's Reward
One of the unique quests involves the fishing mechanic. You must find a fishing rod (often found in cabins or near water) and catch a **Pike**. Delivering this specific fish to the trader rewards you with the **Mark 18 rifle**, a powerhouse for long-range engagements.

### The Collector
The Generalist is also looking for rare weapon parts. Finding a **KP31 and its associated Drum Magazine** is a difficult task but rewards the player with an **ACOG optic**. This sight is a game-changer for players who prefer to eliminate threats from a distance before entering a looted area.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Mark 18 and ACOG are extremely valuable. If you take these into the Vostok zone and die, they are lost forever along with your save file.

## Navigating AI Factions and Combat

As you progress through the **road to vostok main quest walkthrough**, you will encounter three distinct AI factions. Each has different behaviors and gear levels that dictate how you should approach a fight.

| Faction | Difficulty | Gear Level | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bandits** | Easy | Low (Pistols/Shotguns) | Aggressive but unorganized; poor accuracy. |
| **Guards** | Medium | Mid (SMGs/Rifles) | Defensive; usually found protecting specific loot points. |
| **Military** | Hard | High (Tactical Gear/NVGs) | Highly accurate; uses cover and flanking maneuvers. |

When engaging these factions, remember that the AI can spawn in groups of up to 10. Once an area is cleared, it remains peaceful for a duration, allowing you to loot safely. However, the sound of gunfire can attract nearby patrols, so suppressors are highly recommended for any main quest objectives.

## Survival and Medical Management

The medical system in Road to Vostok is condition-based. Instead of a simple health bar, you must manage specific symptoms. When a medical icon turns red on your HUD, you must use the corresponding item to cure it.

*   **Bleeding:** Requires bandages or a tourniquet.
*   **Pain:** Requires painkillers to stabilize aim and movement.
*   **Infection:** Requires antibiotics found in medical crates.
*   **Hydration/Hunger:** Managed through consumables like Pea Soup or Energy Powder.

## Crossing into Vostok: The Perma-Death Challenge

The ultimate goal of the **road to vostok main quest walkthrough** is the transition from the Border Zone into Vostok itself. This is a "Permadeath Zone." 

1.  **The Warning:** Before entering, a red skull icon will appear.
2.  **The Risk:** If you die within this zone, the game deletes your entire save file. All progress, shelter customizations, and hoarded loot are wiped.
3.  **The Reward:** Vostok contains the highest-tier loot in the game, including rare weapon variants and experimental equipment not found in Area 05.

Before attempting this crossing, ensure your shelter is stocked with backup gear. While the save file is deleted upon death in Vostok, having a plan for your next "life" can make the restart less punishing.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I save my progress in Road to Vostok?**

A: You must find and unlock Shelters. Shelters act as safe zones where you can save your simulation time, character progress, and store loot. Some shelters require specific keys obtained through trader tasks.

**Q: Where can I find the Alarm Clock for the trader task?**

A: While the Alarm Clock is a reward for the "Bad Habits" task (delivering smokes and matches), it can occasionally be found in residential nightstands or cabinets in the starting village.

**Q: What happens if I die in the starting zone versus the Vostok zone?**

A: In the starting or border zones, death typically results in losing the items on your person, but your save file remains. In the Vostok zone, death is permanent and results in the total deletion of your save file.

**Q: Is there a map in the game to help with the road to vostok main quest walkthrough?**

A: Currently, there is no traditional "mini-map." Players must rely on physical landmarks, compasses (if found), and the Alarm Clock to navigate and track time. Learning the layout of Area 05 is essential for efficient quest completion.
