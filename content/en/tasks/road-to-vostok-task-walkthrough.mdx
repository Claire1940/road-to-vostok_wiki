## Understanding the Task and Barter System

In the current 2026 build, tasks are primarily delivery-based and are essential for your survival. Completing these objectives lowers the "trader tax," making barters more favorable, and rewards you with keys to unlock new shelters. Since there is no physical currency, you must balance the value of your scavenged goods against the items you need.

| Trader | Location | Primary Goods | Common Task Items |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Generalist** | Village (Big Barn) | Food, Basic Tools, Freezers | Toilet Paper, Lotion, Matches |
| **Doctor** | School (3rd Floor) | Medkits, Splints, Lockers | Medical Supplies, Chemicals |
| **Weapon Specialist** | Outpost (Bunker) | Armor, Meta Weapons, Ammo | Weapon Parts, High-tier Loot |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the "Tasks" tab when speaking to a trader and add them to your notes. This allows you to track specific items while looting POIs (Points of Interest) without having to return to the trader constantly.

## Essential Medical Conditions and Treatments

Survival in the border zone is brutal. If you take damage, you must identify the specific symptom to apply the correct cure. Simply "healing" isn't enough; you need to address the underlying condition or your health will continue to tick down until you pass out.

| Icon | Condition | Cause | Required Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scratch** | Active Bleed | Gunshots, Melee | **Bandage** or **Tourniquet** |
| **Bone** | Fracture | Falls, Limb Damage | **Splint** |
| **Skull/Target** | Head Rupture | Headshot | **Medkit**, **IFAK**, or **AFAK** |
| **Fire** | Burn | Open Flames | **Balm** or **Lotion** |
| **Snowflake** | Hypothermia | Cold Weather/Water | **Heat Source** or **Winter Clothing** |

If you suffer a head rupture and lack a high-tier medkit, your best chance of survival is to sprint back to your nearest shelter. Drop your gear on the floor and wait to pass out; you will respawn at full health within the safety of your cabin, allowing you to recover your items and try again.

## Zone Transitions and Navigation

The world is divided into distinct zones, each with its own loot pool and difficulty tier. Moving between these areas consumes in-game time and impacts your character's hunger and thirst levels.

1.  **The Village (Area 0-5):** Your starting point. Focus on looting houses and cabinets for basic survival gear.
2.  **The School:** Home to the Doctor. Access this by following the yellow tape to the right of your initial shelter until you find a gap.
3.  **The Outpost:** A high-tier military zone containing the Weapon Specialist. This area requires better gear as the AI is more aggressive.
4.  **Vostok:** The final "Permadeath" zone. Entering this area triggers a red skull icon. If you die here, your entire save file is deleted.

To stay on track with your **road to vostok task walkthrough** goals, you must equip a physical map and a clock to monitor the simulation time. A 24-hour cycle in-game equates to roughly 2.4 hours in real life.

## Combat Strategy: Dealing with Bandits

Bandits in 2026 are highly accurate and often travel in small groups. Since you begin with no armor, a single burst from an AI can end your run. 

*   **Lean Peaking:** Rebind your lean keys to your mouse side buttons. Never expose your full body when clearing a room.
*   **Audio Cues:** Listen for mumbling or heavy footsteps. Bandits often give away their positions before they see you.
*   **The One-Tap Rule:** Most bandits do not wear helmets. Even a low-caliber pistol can take them down with a single well-placed headshot.
*   **Resetting the World:** If an area is too hot or you've picked it clean, entering and exiting your shelter will reset the AI and loot spawns.

## Managing Your Vitals and Carry Capacity

Your Carry Capacity (CC) is your most significant bottleneck. You can increase this by finding backpacks, fishing vests, or waist pouches. When your character becomes overweight (indicated by the kilogram symbol), you lose the ability to sprint and your stamina regeneration halts.

| Vital Stat | How to Replenish | Impact of Depletion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Energy** | Food, Energy Powders | Reduced Stamina, Passing Out |
| **Hydration** | Water, Juice, Canned Fruit | Health Loss over time |
| **Mental State** | Cigarettes, Specific Consumables | Aim Sway, Vision Blurring |
| **Temperature** | Clothing, Shelters | Rapid Health Drain |

> **Warning:** Always manually pack your magazines. Dragging ammo over a magazine in your inventory is the only way to reload. Pressing 'R' only works if you have a pre-packed magazine ready in your rig or pockets.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I save my progress in Road to Vostok?**

A: Progress is saved automatically whenever you enter a shelter. Shelters also act as your respawn point. You can unlock additional shelters across different zones by completing specific tasks for traders to receive keys.

**Q: Where can I find the best weapons?**

A: The highest-tier "meta" weapons and armor are sold by the Weapon Specialist in the Outpost bunker. However, you can find basic rifles and shotguns in wooden crates and on military corpses in the School and Border zones.

**Q: Does this road to vostok task walkthrough apply to the permadeath zone?**

A: Yes, but with extreme caution. The mechanics remain the same in Vostok, but the stakes are much higher. It is recommended to complete all available tasks for the Generalist and Doctor to secure high-end medical supplies before attempting to cross the border.

**Q: How do I customize my shelter?**

A: Press **F1** while inside a shelter to enter "Decor Mode." You can place furniture like lockers and freezers purchased from traders. Lockers are essential for storing excess gear that you don't want to lose if you die in the field.
