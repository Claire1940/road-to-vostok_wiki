## Combat and Stamina Management

Acquiring loot is impossible if you cannot survive an encounter. The game utilizes a dual-stamina system: Body Stamina and Arm Stamina. Understanding how these interact with your gear is crucial for maintaining accuracy during a firefight.

### Arm Stamina and Hip Firing
Hip firing in this game is a deliberate action that drains arm stamina. By scrolling up on the mouse wheel, you ready your weapon for point-shooting. However, holding this position—especially with heavy weapons—will cause your aim to sway.

| Action | Stamina Impact | Accuracy Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Semi-Auto Fire** | Low Drain | Best for medium range |
| **Full-Auto Fire** | **High Drain** | Causes massive aim sway quickly |
| **Weapon Swapping** | Recovery | Regains stamina while weapon is holstered |
| **Jogging** | Low Drain | Minimal impact on arm stability |

> **Warning:** If your Body Stamina reaches zero, your character becomes exhausted, causing your Arm Stamina to plummet instantly. Never enter a loot-heavy building with zero stamina.

## Advanced Item Interaction and Placement

One of the most unique features of the game is the item placement system. This allows players to organize their hideouts or create neat stacks of **road to vostok task items** for easier inventory management.

To interact with an item on the ground or in the world, use the **'G' key**. Once the item is selected, you can manipulate its position:
*   **Scroll Wheel:** Moves the item forward or backward in space.
*   **Right Mouse Button:** Rotates the item, allowing you to lay guns flat or stand cans upright.

This system is not just for aesthetics. Properly organizing your loot can help you visually track how many resources you have left without constantly opening a menu. It also prepares players for future "weapon rack" mechanics where specific placement will be required for storage.

## Self-Imposed Tasks and Challenges

Since the game is a hardcore survival experience, many players create their own "tasks" to test their skills. These challenges often revolve around specific **road to vostok task items** and extraction goals.

### The "Zero to Hero" Run
Start a raid with absolutely no equipment. Your goal is to locate a ground cache or a hidden weapon (like the Mosin found on specific rooftops) and then eliminate an AI patrol to secure an RK rifle. This forces you to learn the map's layout and the most likely spawn points for essential gear.

### The Inventory Fill Challenge
Pick a specific item, such as the rare red cans or pea soup, and attempt to fill every slot of your inventory before extracting. This requires multiple trips into high-risk areas and teaches you the rarity tiers of different loot types.

### Tarkov-Style Raiding
Enter the map with only a sidearm (Glock). Your task is to upgrade your kit entirely through scavenging and combat. If you die, you must clear your inventory, simulating the high-stakes loss of the Vostok border zone.

## Survival Tips for the Border Zone

*   **Step Height Matters:** When sprinting at Full Tilt, your character can clear larger gaps and higher obstacles. Use this to reach rooftops where high-tier weapons like the Mosin are hidden.
*   **Inertia Transfers:** You can transfer your running momentum into a crouch-slide to reach cover quickly.
*   **AI Blindspots:** The current AI has difficulty aiming downward. If you are on a height advantage or crouching at a different elevation, you can often avoid fire while you heal.
*   **Short Bursts:** When using automatic weapons, fire in 3-5 round bursts to preserve your arm stamina and maintain a tight shot group.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Mosin-Nagant in the demo?**

A: The Mosin can frequently be found on the roof of the main village building or inside randomly generated ground caches hidden in the shrubbery.

**Q: How do I heal in Road to Vostok?**

A: Currently, the primary way to heal is by consuming Pea Soup. Each can restores 10% of your total health. It is recommended to gather several cans before engaging in combat.

**Q: What happens if I die while carrying road to vostok task items?**

A: Depending on which side of the border you are on, the consequences vary. On the Area 5 side, you typically lose your inventory. On the Vostok side, the game features permadeath, which may result in the loss of your entire save file.

**Q: How do I rotate items when placing them?**

A: After picking up an item with the 'G' key, hold the Right Mouse Button to rotate the object. You can also use the scroll wheel to move the item closer or further away from your character.
