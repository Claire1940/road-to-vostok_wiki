## Overview of the Road to Vostok Task System

In Road to Vostok, the traditional questing system is replaced by "Tasks" provided by stationary traders located throughout the world. These tasks are primarily fetch-based, requiring you to find specific items in the environment and deliver them to a specific NPC. Unlike many other survival shooters, there is no digital currency; instead, the game utilizes a bartering system where every item has an inherent value used for trading.

Completing entries on the **road to vostok quest list** provides three primary benefits:
1.  **Tax Reduction:** Every completed task lowers the trader tax, making future barters more favorable for you.
2.  **Shelter Access:** Certain tasks reward you with keys that unlock new safe areas across the map.
3.  **Unique Rewards:** Some tasks provide high-tier items or crafting recipes that cannot be found easily through standard looting.

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Quest Type** | Primarily fetch quests and delivery tasks. |
| **Primary NPCs** | The Generalist (Village) and The Doctor (School). |
| **Reward Types** | Keys, lower taxes, medical supplies, and shelter unlocks. |
| **Item Delivery** | Items must be delivered all at once through the "Start Delivery" interface. |

> **💡 Tip:** Your starting cabin serves as a permanent stash. Use the containers there to hoard task items until you have the full amount required for a delivery.

## How to Manage Task Items and Stashing

Because inventory space is at a premium, you cannot carry every item on the **road to vostok quest list** at once. Effective questing requires a "stash and backtrack" strategy.

1.  **Identify the Task:** Interact with the trader and use the "Take Note" button to pin the requirements to your UI.
2.  **Loot Everything:** In the early game, pick up everything you find. Even items not on the quest list have barter value.
3.  **The Cabin Stash:** Return to your starting cabin frequently. Items stored in the cabin's containers are safe even if you die in the field.
4.  **Bulk Delivery:** You cannot turn in items one by one. You must have the entire required quantity in your inventory to click "Start Delivery" at the trader.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you die while carrying task items in your inventory, they are lost forever. Only items inside your cabin or unlocked shelters are preserved between deaths.

## Progression and Map Transitions

The game world is divided into three distinct zones. As you progress through the **road to vostok quest list**, you will move from the relatively "safe" starting zones into high-risk areas.

*   **Area 0-5 (Starting Zone):** Contains the Village and the Generalist. Loot is basic, and bandits are the primary threat.
*   **Border Zone (Medium Tier):** Contains the School and the Minefield. This is where you find The Doctor and better military-grade equipment.
*   **Vostok (Permadeath Zone):** The final high-reward area. Entering this zone triggers a red skull icon. If you die here, your entire save file is deleted.

## Survival Strategies for Questing

To successfully complete the **road to vostok quest list**, you must survive the journey between the Village and the School. Bandits are the most common obstacle, and they rely heavily on line of sight.

*   **Avoid the Open:** Bandits can spot you from significant distances. Use the forest line and ruins for cover.
*   **Looting Lockers:** In the School, lockers are searched in groups of three. This significantly speeds up your search for medical quest items.
*   **The Minefield:** Beyond the School lies a minefield. Mines are visible and can be disarmed by shooting them from a distance. Do not attempt to walk through the grass; stick to the worn paths.
*   **Weapon Maintenance:** Bandit weapons are almost always damaged. Focus on taking their backpacks and ammo rather than their rusty firearms unless you are desperate.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I turn in items for the road to vostok quest list?**

A: To complete a task, you must have all required items in your inventory. Interact with the trader, select the task, click "Start Delivery," and then click the items in your inventory to transfer them to the trader.

**Q: Where can I find the Doctor NPC?**

A: The Doctor is located on the third floor of the School building. He usually appears starting on the second day of your simulation.

**Q: What happens if I die while doing a task?**

A: Any items in your character's inventory are lost upon death. However, any tasks you have already completed remain finished, and your tax reductions or shelter unlocks are permanent (unless you are in the Vostok permadeath zone).

**Q: Can I complete quests in any order?**

A: While you can take notes on multiple tasks, it is generally recommended to complete the Generalist's "Attic Shelter" task first to give yourself a secondary safe point closer to the School.
