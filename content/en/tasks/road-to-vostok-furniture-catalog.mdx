## Entering Decor Mode and Basic Manipulation

To begin using your **road to vostok furniture catalog**, you must first understand how to interact with the environment. The game distinguishes between standard gameplay and "Decor Mode." While in a shelter, you can toggle this mode to unlock the ability to move heavy objects that are otherwise static.

By default, pressing **F1** (which can be remapped in the settings) activates Decor Mode. Once active, every piece of furniture—from bedside tables to industrial refrigerators—will display a visual indicator. 

### Furniture Manipulation Controls

| Action | Control | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Grab Object** | Left Click | Picks up the highlighted furniture item. |
| **Rotate** | Mouse Wheel | Rotates the object on its vertical axis. |
| **Adjust Distance** | Shift + Mouse Wheel | Moves the object closer or further from the player. |
| **Grid Snapping** | Automatic | Aligns objects to surfaces to prevent "ugly gaps." |
| **Surface Validation** | Shader Overlay | Green indicates a valid placement; Red indicates invalid. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the grid snapping feature to align shelves perfectly against walls. This maximizes your floor space, which is vital in smaller shelters like the Tent or the Attic.

## Mastering the Road to Vostok Furniture Catalog

The **road to vostok furniture catalog** isn't just a list of items; it functions as a specialized "Tetris-style" inventory for large-scale objects. When you are redecorating a room, the space can become crowded very quickly. The catalog allows you to "store" furniture temporarily in a virtual space, making it easy to clear a room before redesigning it.

This system is also the primary way to move furniture between different shelters. If you find a high-quality bed in a starting shelter, you can add it to your catalog and deploy it later in a more advanced forward operating base.

### Catalog Management Strategies

1.  **Clear the Floor:** When entering a new shelter, pick up all default furniture and send it to the catalog. This gives you a blank canvas to work with.
2.  **Organize by Function:** Use the Tetris interface to group similar items together within the catalog (e.g., all storage containers in one corner).
3.  **Multi-Shelter Logistics:** Remember that the catalog is global for your character. Items stored in the catalog while at the Village shelter can be accessed when you reach the border zones.

## Acquiring New Furniture: Crafting and Trading

You are not limited to the default items found in your initial bunker. Expanding your **road to vostok furniture catalog** requires a mix of scavenging, crafting, and bartering with the few remaining souls in the zone. In the 2026 version of the game, there are over 20 unique furniture recipes available for players to discover.

### Furniture Crafting Recipes

Crafting furniture has a "Shelter Proximity" requirement. You cannot build a bed in the middle of a forest; you must be within the bounds of a discovered shelter to access the furniture tab in your crafting menu.

| Item | Required Materials | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Military Bed** | 2x Sleeping Pad, 2x Pillow, 1x Sleeping Bag | High-quality save point and rest. |
| **Single Bed** | 1x Lumber, 1x Mattress Roll, 1x Blanket, 1x Pillow | Standard rest point. |
| **Wall Shelf** | 2x Lumber, 1x Nails, 1x Brackets | Vertical storage for small items. |
| **Wooden Stool** | 1x Lumber, 1x Glue | Basic seating. |

### Trading for Decor

Traders are another vital source for your **road to vostok furniture catalog**. While the Generalist trader carries a rotating stock of basic home goods, specialized traders offer unique items:

*   **The Generalist:** Sells fridges, nightstands, and basic lighting fixtures.
*   **Grandma:** A specialized trader focused on "soft" goods. She is the primary source for carpets, curtains, and fabric-based decorations.
*   **Scavenged Finds:** Some furniture can only be found as rare "world loot." If you see a unique painting or a sturdy locker in the world, check if it can be grabbed in Decor Mode.

## Advanced Features: Item Grouping and Lighting

A standout feature of the Road to Vostok customization system is **Item Grouping**. In many survival games, moving a shelf means you have to manually pick up every single can of food and box of ammo sitting on it. In Vostok, items placed on a surface become "parented" to that furniture.

When you move a table or a shelf in Decor Mode, every item currently sitting on that surface moves with it. This allows for rapid reorganization without the headache of micro-managing your entire inventory.

### Lighting and Atmosphere

Shelters are equipped with functional light switches. Players can manipulate the lighting in individual rooms to create a specific mood or to save power (depending on future survival mechanics).
*   **Mood Lighting:** Turn off overhead lights and use lamps or lanterns for a more immersive, "tactical" feel.
*   **Multiple Switches:** Larger shelters, like the Finnish civilian bunkers, often have multiple switches per room to control different light banks.

> **Warning:** Be careful when placing furniture near doorways. While the grid snapping helps, it is possible to block your own exit if you place large items like wardrobes too close to the frame.

## Modding and the Furniture Component

The developer has implemented a "Furniture Component" tool that streamlines the process for modders. This tool allows external 3D models to be converted into dynamic, interactable furniture with a single click. This ensures that the **road to vostok furniture catalog** will continue to grow as the community contributes new assets, from vintage Finnish decor to modern tactical gear racks.

The component automatically generates:
*   Collision boxes for item placement.
*   Detection logic for the Decor Mode cursor.
*   Target points for wall-mounted vs. floor-mounted logic.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I lose furniture if my shelter is raided?**

A: Currently, the shelter system acts as a safe zone for your items. However, the developer has teased optional "hardcore" features where survival involves more than just your own health, which may impact shelter security in future updates.

**Q: How do I transfer items from the Road to Vostok furniture catalog to a new shelter?**

A: Simply pick up the furniture in Decor Mode and "store" it in your catalog. When you travel to a new shelter location, open the catalog via the Decor Mode interface (F1 + Tab) and place the items in the new location.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many items I can put in the catalog?**

A: The catalog uses a Tetris-style grid. While it is quite large, you are limited by the physical dimensions of the items. You cannot store an infinite number of refrigerators, so prioritize the pieces you actually need.

**Q: Does furniture provide any gameplay buffs?**

A: Higher-quality beds allow for faster recovery or better save-point functionality. Additionally, containers (lockers, fridges, crates) are essential for organizing the hundreds of loot items you will collect throughout the game.
