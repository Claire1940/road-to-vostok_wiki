In the unforgiving exclusion zone of the Finnish-Russian border, survival is a matter of resource management and tactical positioning. Navigating the world effectively requires a deep understanding of the **road to vostok traders**, as these isolated NPCs are your only lifeline for high-tier medical supplies, protective armor, and advanced weaponry. Unlike traditional shooters, this hardcore survival experience eschews standard currency in favor of a realistic barter system, forcing you to weigh the value of every scavenged tin of food against the necessity of a new rifle magazine.

Finding and utilizing the **road to vostok traders** is essential for long-term progression. As you push deeper into dangerous zones like the School and the Outpost, the difficulty spikes significantly, making the gear provided by these merchants non-negotiable. This guide provides the exact coordinates for every current trader, explains the nuances of the barter economy, and offers strategies for surviving the bandit-heavy areas surrounding their hideouts in 2026.

## The Barter System and Trading Mechanics

Trading in Road to Vostok is built on a "value-for-value" mechanic rather than a cash-based economy. When you interact with a trader, you are presented with a scale meter in the center of the screen. Your goal is to balance the value of the items you want with the items you are willing to give up.

### How to Trade Effectively
To initiate a trade, select the items you desire from the trader's inventory and then begin moving items from your own inventory into the trade window. The meter will shift as values are calculated.

| Trade Status | Meter Position | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Underpaid** | Red (Left) | Add more items or higher-value loot to the window. |
| **Fair Trade** | Center (White/Grey) | Acceptable, though you may be leaving slight value on the table. |
| **Optimal** | **Green (Right)** | The trade is balanced in your favor or perfectly equal. |
| **Overpaid** | Deep Green/Max | You are losing value (e.g., trading a meta weapon for a juice box). Remove items. |

> **💡 Tip:** Trader inventories are not static. If you do not see the specific armor or weapon you need, check the timer at the top of the trade screen. Once it reaches zero, the inventory resets. You can also force a partial restock by purchasing several items, closing the menu, and reopening it.

## The Generalist: Your First Point of Contact

Located in the starting **Village** zone, the Generalist is the first of the **road to vostok traders** you will encounter. He operates out of a large, distinctive barn. While the interior of the barn is generally a safe zone where bandits will not enter, they are known to camp the exterior. Always clear the immediate perimeter before committing to a long trading session.

The Generalist focuses on essential survival goods and basic shelter upgrades. If you are looking to expand your storage early on, he is the primary source for basic containers.

*   **Primary Goods:** Food, water, basic tools, and "Freezer" furniture.
*   **Tasks:** Offers introductory scavenging tasks to help you learn the map layout.
*   **Location Strategy:** From your starting cabin, head toward the center of the Village. Look for the largest wooden structure with open bay doors. You will hear him mumbling to himself as you approach.

## The Doctor: Medical Supplies and Secure Storage

As you progress toward the **School** zone, your medical needs will shift from simple bandages to complex trauma care. The Doctor is the most critical NPC for surviving headshots and major ruptures. 

| Item Type | Utility | Recommended Stock |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Splints** | Repairs fractures (bone icon) | Always carry 1 |
| **Medkits/IFAKs** | Heals head ruptures and targets | Essential for School/Outpost |
| **Lockers** | Permanent storage for your shelter | Buy 2+ early game |
| **Tourniquets** | Stops heavy bleeding | Keep in quick-slot |

### Finding the Doctor
To reach the Doctor, you must first use the transit point at the edge of the Village. Look for the break in the yellow perimeter tape near the waterline. Once you transit to the School zone, follow the main highway until you see the large school building on your left. Enter the building and navigate to the **third floor**. The Doctor is located in the first room on the right.

## The Weapon Specialist: Meta Gear and Armor

For survivors looking to survive encounters with high-tier bandits, the Weapon Specialist is the most important of the **road to vostok traders**. He is the exclusive source for body armor and armor plates, which can prevent you from being "one-tapped" during a raid.

### Location Guide: The Outpost Bunker
The journey to the Weapon Specialist is dangerous and requires transiting through multiple zones.

1.  **Village to School:** Use the transit at the yellow tape boundary.
2.  **School to Outpost:** Stay on the highway in the School zone, passing the school itself, until you reach the far end of the map. Transit to the Outpost.
3.  **The Final Stretch:** In the Outpost, hug the left side of the map along the rock formations. You will pass a tent shelter (the second available save point).
4.  **The Landmark:** Look for a **broken-down tank** sitting directly in front of a bunker entrance built into the rocks. The Weapon Specialist is inside.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Outpost is heavily patrolled. Bandits in this area have better detection ranges. Use the "lean" mechanic (default Q/E) to check corners before approaching the bunker.

## Essential Strategies for Road to Vostok Traders

Interacting with traders is about more than just swapping items; it is about managing your character's physical state and long-term progression. Every time you transit between zones to visit a merchant, your character suffers "transit decay."

### Transit Costs and Penalties
Moving between maps consumes resources. You must plan your trading trips to ensure you don't arrive at a merchant in a state of collapse.

| Transit Route | Time Cost | Hunger/Thirst Penalty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Village → School** | ~2 Hours | Moderate |
| **School → Outpost** | ~2 Hours | Moderate |
| **Village → Outpost (Total)** | **~4 Hours** | **High** |

### Shelter Customization
Items purchased from traders, specifically furniture like lockers, rugs, and freezers, do not appear in your backpack. Instead, they are sent to your **Shelter Catalog**. 
1.  Return to your cabin or the Outpost tent.
2.  Press **F1** to enter Build Mode.
3.  Press **Tab** to open the catalog and place your purchased items.
4.  Use lockers to store high-value weapons you aren't ready to lose yet.

## Task Management and Progression
Each of the **road to vostok traders** offers unique tasks. These are not just for flavor; they are the primary method for "leveling up" your standing and accessing better gear. You can track these tasks in your notes. Note that you must have all required items in your inventory simultaneously to complete a turn-in; the traders will not accept partial deliveries.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find all Road to Vostok traders?**

A: There are currently three traders: the Generalist (Village Barn), the Doctor (School Building, 3rd Floor), and the Weapon Specialist (Outpost Bunker behind the tank). You must use the transit system to move between these zones.

**Q: Why is the trader's inventory empty?**

A: Trader inventories operate on a global timer. If they are out of stock of a specific item like body armor, you must wait for the reset timer (visible in the trade menu) to reach zero.

**Q: Can I sell items for cash?**

A: No, there is no cash currency in the game as of 2026. All transactions are handled via a barter scale. You must provide items of equal or greater value to receive the goods you want.

**Q: What happens if I die while traveling to a trader?**

A: Road to Vostok features a "permadeath" loot mechanic. If you die, you lose everything on your person. However, any furniture or items already stored in your shelter via the catalog or lockers will remain safe.
