## Road to Vostok: How to Complete All Tasks and Missions

The task system in Road to Vostok differs significantly from traditional RPGs. Tasks are provided by specific NPCs known as Traders, and completing them requires a combination of exploration, scavenging, and inventory management.

### The Barter and Task System
In the current 2026 build, there is no traditional currency. All commerce is handled through a barter system. When you accept a task from a trader, you are essentially agreeing to find specific items in exchange for reputation or high-tier equipment.

To manage your active missions, follow these steps:
1.  **Locate a Trader**: Visit one of the three main traders (Generalist, Doctor, or Weapon Specialist).
2.  **Open the Task Tab**: Within the trade menu, select the "Tasks" option to view available objectives.
3.  **Add to Notes**: Use the "Add to Notes" feature so you can track required items while out in the field.
4.  **Bulk Turn-In**: You must have all required items in your inventory simultaneously to complete a task. Partial turn-ins are not currently supported.

| Trader | Primary Location | Specialty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Generalist** | Village (Big Barn) | Basic supplies, food, freezers, and early-game tools. |
| **Doctor** | School (3rd Floor) | Medical supplies, splints, and storage lockers. |
| **Weapon Specialist** | Outpost (Bunker) | High-tier firearms, armor, and weapon attachments. |

## Finding and Reaching Every Trader

Knowing **road to vostok how to complete all tasks** requires knowing where the quest-givers reside. The world is divided into zones, and traveling between them consumes resources.

### The Generalist (Village Zone)
The Generalist is your starting point. He is located in the large barn within the central village area. While the interior of the barn is generally safe from bandit pathing, enemies can and will shoot at you through the open doors. Always clear the immediate perimeter before engaging in a lengthy trade session.

### The Doctor (School Zone)
To reach the Doctor, you must transit from the Village to the School.
*   **Transit Path**: Exit your starter shelter and head right until you reach the yellow boundary tape. Follow the tape left until you find a gap.
*   **Location**: Once in the School zone, follow the main highway until you see the large school building on your left. The Doctor is located on the third floor, first door on the right.

### The Weapon Specialist (Outpost Zone)
The Weapon Specialist holds the keys to the best gear in the game but is the most difficult to reach.
*   **Transit Path**: You must first travel to the School zone. Continue past the school building to the far end of the map to find the Outpost transit.
*   **Location**: Stay to the left near the water's edge until you see a broken-down tank. Behind the tank is a bunker built into the rock formation where the specialist resides.

> **💡 Tip:** Every time you transit between zones, your character will lose a portion of their hunger and thirst meters, and time will advance. Always pack a snack and water before a long journey.

## Essential Survival Mechanics for Tasking

You cannot complete tasks if you are dead. Road to Vostok features a "permadeath-lite" system where dying results in the loss of all gear currently on your person. To ensure you survive long enough to turn in your items, you must understand the status effect system.

### Managing Injury and Illness
The game tracks several vital signs. While hunger and thirst are straightforward, the injury system is complex. If you suffer a "Rupture" or a "Headshot" status, your health will drain at an alarming rate.

| Injury Type | Required Medical Item | Consequence of Neglect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bleed** | Bandage / Tourniquet | Slow health drain; eventual death. |
| **Fracture** | Splint | Inability to run; slow health drain. |
| **Burn** | Balm / Lotion | Persistent pain and health loss. |
| **Rupture/Headshot** | Medkit / IFAK / AFAK | Rapid health drain; near-instant death. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you suffer a head rupture and do not have an IFAK, your only hope is to sprint back to your shelter. If you can enter the shelter and drop your loot before passing out, you will respawn at full health and can reclaim your items.

## Combat Strategies for the Exclusion Zone

Bandits in Road to Vostok are notoriously accurate. They do not wear helmets, meaning a single well-placed shot to the head from any weapon can neutralize them. However, because you start with no armor, they can do the same to you.

1.  **Use the Lean Mechanic**: Never walk around a corner fully exposed. Use the Q and E keys (or rebind them to mouse side buttons) to peak.
2.  **Listen for Audio Cues**: Bandits often mumble to themselves. If you hear grumbling, stop moving and scan the area.
3.  **Break Line of Sight**: If a bandit spots you, they will "lock on." Duck behind cover immediately. The longer you stay in their vision, the more accurate their shots become.
4.  **Manual Magazine Packing**: Remember that pressing R only works if you have a loaded magazine in your rig. You must manually drag loose ammo onto empty mags in your inventory to "pack" them.

## Shelter Customization and Progression

Your shelter is your only safe haven. As you learn **road to vostok how to complete all tasks**, you will eventually earn enough barter value to purchase furniture.

*   **Build Mode**: Press **F1** inside your shelter to enter the customization menu.
*   **Storage Solutions**: Purchase lockers from the Doctor and freezers from the Generalist. These allow you to store weapons and food safely so they aren't lost when you die in the field.
*   **The Catalog**: Any furniture purchased from traders won't appear in your backpack. Instead, it is added to your shelter's internal catalog, accessible via the build menu.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the map and clock in Road to Vostok?**

A: These are not default UI elements. You must find a physical map and a clock item while looting houses or containers. Once equipped in your inventory, you can use them to check your location and the time of day.

**Q: What happens if I die while carrying task items?**

A: If you die in the world, you lose everything in your inventory. You will need to find all the required items again to complete the task. Only items stored in your shelter are safe from loss.

**Q: Can I complete tasks for different traders at the same time?**

A: Yes, you can track multiple tasks. However, because inventory space is limited and the weight of items affects your stamina, it is often safer to focus on one or two tasks at a time.

**Q: Why can't I run even though my stamina is full?**

A: Check the bottom right of your screen for a "Kilogram" symbol. If you are overweight, you cannot sprint. You will need to equip a better backpack or a fishing vest to increase your carry capacity and successfully complete **road to vostok how to complete all tasks** that require heavy items.
