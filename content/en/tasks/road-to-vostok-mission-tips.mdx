## Essential Controls and Interface Basics

Before stepping onto the road, you must master the interface. The game utilizes a diegetic UI where information is often found on the items themselves or through specific inspection animations. Understanding your carry capacity (CC) is the first step toward a successful mission.

| Action | Keybinding | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Inventory** | Tab | Access gear, medical status, and loot |
| **Inspect Weapon** | X | Check chamber, magazine, and fire mode |
| **Fire Mode** | F | Toggle between Single and Full Auto |
| **Fast Equip** | Alt + Click | Quickly move items to active slots |
| **Fast Transfer** | Ctrl + Click | Move items between containers |
| **Check Magazine** | B (Customizable) | Check remaining rounds and chamber status |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always check your chamber after reloading. If you do not manually rack the slide or bolt on certain weapons, the gun will not fire when you pull the trigger, leading to a quick death in a firefight.

## Combat Strategies: Outsmarting the AI

The AI in Road to Vostok is divided into three distinct factions: Bandits, Guards, and Military. Each faction possesses different levels of aggression and equipment. Unlike standard shooters, the AI here becomes more accurate the longer they have a direct line of sight on you.

### Breaking Line of Sight
The most effective way to survive an encounter is to break line of sight immediately after the AI spots you. Once you are behind cover, reposition and "re-peek" from a different angle. This resets the AI's accuracy timer, giving you a window to land a killing shot before they zero in on your position.

### Fire Selector Sound Cues
Since many Russian-made weapons in the game have fire selectors on the side opposite the camera, you must rely on audio cues. 
*   **High-Pitch Click:** Indicates the weapon is set to **Full Auto**.
*   **Low-Pitch/Dull Click:** Indicates the weapon is set to **Single Fire**.

## Looting and Inventory Management

Looting is the backbone of your progression. In Road to Vostok, "logical storage" is the rule—if an object looks like it should hold items (like a duffel bag, crate, or cabinet), it is likely interactable.

| Item Category | Priority | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Medical Supplies** | High | Keep bandages and splints in fast-access slots |
| **Quest Items** | High | Items like Inverters and Batteries should be extracted immediately |
| **Weapon Parts** | Medium | Only take attachments compatible with your current platform |
| **Food/Water** | Low/Medium | Consume on-site to save inventory space unless low on vitals |

### Armor and Protection
Armor levels are directly linked to the NIJ rating system. Higher-tier ammo can penetrate lower-tier armor with ease. Always prioritize finding a helmet, as headshots are often instantly fatal and prevent critical medical conditions from triggering.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Armor plates can be inserted into plate carriers. Ensure your carrier actually has plates installed; a vest without plates provides zero ballistic protection.

## The Economy: Traders and Task Completion

The trader system is unique because tasks are finite. Completing a delivery or scout mission for a trader provides rewards and, more importantly, lowers your "Trader Tax." 

1.  **Lowering Taxes:** Initially, trader prices are inflated by a high tax rate. Completing tasks like delivering "Toilet Paper" or "Lotion" reduces this tax, making high-tier gear more affordable.
2.  **Unlocking Shelters:** Keys are often rewarded for mission completion. These allow you to save your progress, store loot, and skip time safely.
3.  **One-Time Missions:** Remember that once a task is completed, it cannot be repeated on that save file. If you lose your gear in a permadeath zone, you cannot simply re-run old missions to get it back.

## Navigating the Zones

The game world is divided into three distinct difficulty zones. Understanding these is vital for applying your **road to vostok mission tips** effectively.

*   **Starting Zone (Area 0-5):** Low-tier loot, mostly Bandits. Use this area to farm basic supplies and complete initial trader tasks.
*   **Border Zone:** Medium-tier difficulty. Expect better-equipped Guards and more environmental hazards like minefields.
*   **Vostok (Permadeath Zone):** The ultimate challenge. Entering this zone triggers a red skull icon. If you die here, your entire save file is deleted. Only enter Vostok with top-tier armor (NIJ 4) and a suppressed primary weapon.

| Zone | AI Difficulty | Loot Quality | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Village** | Easy | Low | Minimal |
| **Apartments** | Medium/Hard | High | High |
| **Vostok** | Extreme | Legendary | Absolute (Permadeath) |

## Survival and Vitals Maintenance

Your character has several vitals to manage: Health, Hydration, Energy, and Body Temperature. In 2026, the simulation rate is approximately 2.4 hours of real-time for every 24 hours in-game.

*   **Medical Conditions:** When a medical icon turns red, you must use a specific item to cure the condition (e.g., a splint for a broken leg). 
*   **Mental Health:** Items like "Snus" or cigarettes can help manage your character's mental state, which can degrade during long, stressful raids.
*   **Weather and Temperature:** Winter environments require heavy clothing like windbreakers or combat boots to prevent hypothermia.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I die in the starting zone?**

A: In standard mode, you will lose the gear on your body but keep your shelter progress and stored items. However, if you are in the Vostok zone, your entire save is deleted upon death.

**Q: How do I find the Inverter for the trader mission?**

A: The Inverter is a rare RNG drop typically found in industrial areas or electronic crates. Following the **road to vostok mission tips** for farming, you should check the high-density loot buildings in the Apartments map.

**Q: Can I repair my weapons and armor?**

A: Yes, you can find repair kits or use traders to restore the condition of your gear. Using a weapon with low durability can lead to frequent jams and malfunctions.

**Q: Is there a way to see the time without an alarm clock?**

A: No, the game is designed to be immersive. You must find and equip an alarm clock or watch to see the current simulation time, which is crucial for planning your extraction before nightfall.
