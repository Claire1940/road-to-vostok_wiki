## Crossing the Minefield to the Apartments

The most difficult navigational challenge in the 2026 demo is the Minefield. This area sits between the relatively safe village and the high-value Apartment complex in Vostok. To cross it successfully, you need to follow a very specific path.

1.  **Preparation**: Bring a scoped rifle and a flashlight. The mines are small, green, and hidden in the grass.
2.  **The Entry Point**: Follow the main road from the village until you see the caution tape break.
3.  **The Tank Landmark**: Look for a derelict tank on the right side of the road. This is your "Safe Zone" marker.
4.  **The Sniper Tower**: From the tank, aim your scope toward the concrete towers in the distance. Bandits often spawn here.
5.  **The Zig-Zag Path**: Do not run straight. Use your scope to spot the physical mines (circular green objects) every 10–15 feet. Shoot them from a distance to clear a path.

| Landmark | Navigation Tip | Danger Level |

| **Concrete Slabs** | Pivot point to head toward the towers. | Low |
| **The Log Pile** | Marks the halfway point of the minefield. | Medium |
| **The Green Vehicle** | The exit door to the Apartments is right next to this. | **Extreme** |

## Survival Gear and Inventory Management

When you finally **road to vostok find map** transition points and reach new areas, your inventory capacity becomes your biggest bottleneck. You start with a very limited carry capacity (CC), which can only be increased by finding and equipping vests and backpacks.

### Essential Exploration Kit
*   **Flashlight**: Mandatory for nighttime travel. The journey to Vostok often takes 4+ hours of simulation time, meaning if you leave at dusk, you will arrive in pitch blackness.
*   **Optics**: A scope (like an ACOG or PSO) is required not just for combat, but for spotting mines and identifying AI factions from a distance.
*   **Medical Supplies**: Always carry at least one **Splint** and **Bandages**. A broken leg in the middle of a minefield is essentially a death sentence.
*   **Alarm Clock**: This item allows you to see the current simulation time. Use it to plan your departures so you arrive at your destination during daylight hours.

> **💡 Tip:** You can "duplicate" or stack ammo more efficiently by transferring partial stacks into containers that already hold the same caliber. This saves precious inventory slots for high-tier loot.

## Factions and AI Behavior

As you navigate the world, the AI you encounter will change based on how far you have traveled. The further you go toward Vostok, the more aggressive and well-equipped the enemies become.

*   **Bandits**: Low-tier enemies found in Area 0-5. They usually carry pistols or shotguns and wear no armor.
*   **Guards**: Mid-tier enemies often found patrolling the Border Zone. They use submachine guns and may have basic helmets.
*   **Military**: The deadliest faction. They carry high-caliber assault rifles (AK-12, RK-95) and wear Level 4 armor plates.

You can check the penetration level of your ammo by hovering over it in your inventory. To take down Military AI in Vostok, you generally need armor-piercing rounds or multiple well-placed headshots.

## Looting the Vostok Apartments

The Apartments are the current "end-game" of the Road to Vostok demo. Once you successfully navigate the minefield, you will find several multi-story buildings filled with high-end loot.

1.  **Check the Bathrooms**: Medical cabinets in the bathrooms are the best source for Medkits, Splints, and Cough Medicine.
2.  **Kitchens**: Look for canned juice and food to replenish the energy lost during the map transition.
3.  **Military Crates**: Usually found on the upper floors or rooftops. These contain armor plates (Strike Plates) and high-end weapon attachments.
4.  **The Red Skull**: Remember, while you are in the Apartments, the Perma-death rule is active. If you hear suppressed gunfire or see a laser sight, find cover immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find a map in Road to Vostok?**

A: Currently, there is no in-game map item or HUD overlay. You must navigate using landmarks like the train tracks, roads, and the prominent sniper towers near the border.

**Q: What happens if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: If you die in Vostok (indicated by a red skull on your HUD), your save file is permanently deleted. You will lose all progression, including items stored in your shelters.

**Q: How do I clear mines in the Minefield?**

A: Use a scoped weapon to spot the mines in the grass. You can shoot them to detonate them safely from a distance. Do not attempt to walk through the minefield without a long-range optic.

**Q: How do I save my progress?**

A: You can save your progress and simulation time by interacting with the bed or radio inside a Shelter. Shelters are safe zones where you can also store extra loot and customize your equipment.

**Q: Is there a way to travel faster between maps?**

A: No, transitions always cost simulation time and character vitals. The best strategy is to use an Alarm Clock to ensure you are leaving your shelter at a time that allows for a daylight arrival.
