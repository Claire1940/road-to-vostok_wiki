## Essential Controls and Recommended Keybinds

The default control scheme in Road to Vostok provides a solid foundation, but many veteran players find that certain actions are better suited for different inputs. To survive the punishing AI and the environmental hazards, you need a layout that minimizes finger travel and maximizes your ability to react.

One of the most significant improvements you can make is moving your leaning mechanics. By default, leaning is often tied to the Q and E keys, which can interfere with your ability to move left or right while peeking. Moving these to your mouse side buttons allows for much smoother room clearing. Additionally, changing the interaction key can significantly speed up your looting process.

| Action | Default Key | Recommended Key | Reason for Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lean Left** | Q | Mouse Button 4 | Allows for movement while leaning. |
| **Lean Right** | E | Mouse Button 5 | Facilitates smoother corner peeking. |
| **Interact/Search** | F (Variable) | **F** | Standardizes interaction for speed. |
| **Toggle Flashlight** | L | **T** | Easier to reach during dark encounters. |
| **Check Time** | N/A | **Equip Alarm Clock** | Vital for tracking daylight cycles. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself fumbling during combat, prioritize moving your tactical keys (like leaning and flashlights) to your mouse to keep your WASD fingers free for movement.

## Interaction and Looting Mechanics

Looting in Road to Vostok is not limited to searching boxes and crates. The game features a "physicalized" loot system where items frequently spawn directly on the ground, on tables, or hidden in corners of tents. Using your interaction key effectively is the "key" to finding rare medical supplies and ammunition.

When searching a house, always check the bathroom first. Medicine cabinets mounted on walls are the most reliable sources for bandages and painkillers. Since bleeding is a primary cause of death, knowing exactly where to look and having your interact key ready is vital.

| Loot Location | Item Type Frequency | Interaction Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Medicine Cabinets** | High (Meds/Bandages) | Check bathrooms in every residential building. |
| **Floor/Ground** | Medium (Ammo/Valuables) | Scan corners of tents and under doorways. |
| **Tables (Cabin)** | High (Weapon Storage) | Use as an overflow for weapons to save cabinet space. |
| **Red Barn** | High (Trading) | Safe zone for exchanging loot with the Trader. |

## Tactical Combat and Movement

The AI bandits in Road to Vostok are remarkably accurate and can spot players from a significant distance. To counter this, you must master the "keys" of tactical positioning. This involves using quick peeks and understanding the stamina system. Unlike many modern shooters that limit sprinting to short bursts, Road to Vostok allows for more realistic long-distance running, which you should use to reposition if an engagement turns sour.

Because early-game bandits typically do not wear body armor, your best strategy is to aim for the head. Ammunition is a scarce resource, so avoid "spraying and praying." Instead, use your lean keys to gather information on enemy positions and take controlled, single shots.

1.  **Use Quick Peeks:** Never stay exposed for more than a second. Lean out, identify the target, and return to cover.
2.  **Repack Magazines:** When in a safe spot, use your inventory keys to manually repack ammo into your magazines.
3.  **Manage Stamina:** Use your extended sprint capacity to cross open fields quickly, but always ensure you have enough breath left for steady aiming.

## The Trader and Task System

The Trader is located in the Red Barn near your starting cabin. Understanding his "keys" to business is essential because the game does not use a traditional currency system. Instead, everything is based on the perceived value of the items you offer in exchange for his goods.

| Mechanic | How It Works | Pro Tip |
|---|---|---|
| **Inventory Reset** | Resets every time you enter/exit your cabin. | Check back often for rare medical supplies. |
| **Barter System** | Balance the value meter with your own loot. | Trade low-value valuables for essential ammo. |
| **Task Delivery** | Requires items to be in your active inventory. | Manually click items to transfer them for tasks. |

To progress, you will need to accept retrieval tasks. These require you to venture into the town, find specific items, and return them. Ensure you have a backpack equipped, as this significantly increases your carry capacity, allowing you to bring back both task items and extra loot for trading. ## Survival and Cabin Management

Your cabin acts as your primary base of operations and a safe haven from the world. One of the "keys" to efficiency is how you manage your limited storage. While the cabinets are great for small items like ammo and meds, they fill up quickly.

> **Warning:** If you die in the world, you lose everything on your character. Always leave a "backup kit" in your cabin.

A clever way to store extra firearms is to place them directly on the table inside your cabin. This saves space in your containers for more critical, stackable items. If you ever run out of gear, you can perform a "zero-to-hero" run, where you head out with nothing and rely on your knowledge of loot spawns to rebuild your arsenal.

## Resetting the World for Infinite Loot

One of the most useful strategies for early-game progression is the "Town Reset." Every time you enter or exit your cabin, the loot and the enemies in the surrounding town are completely refreshed. If you find a reliable spawn for medicine or high-value ammo, you can:

1.  Exit the cabin and clear the town.
2.  Loot all high-value spots and medicine cabinets.
3.  Return to the cabin and stash your findings.
4.  Immediately exit again to find the town fully replenished.

This loop is the fastest way to snowball your progress and ensure you are fully equipped for the more difficult zones deeper in the Vostok territory.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I change my keybinds in Road to Vostok?**

A: You can access the keybinds menu through the Settings option in the main menu or by pressing Esc while in-game. It is highly recommended to follow this **road to vostok keys guide** and move your lean functions to your mouse for better tactical movement.

**Q: What is the most important key for survival?**

A: Aside from movement, the "Interact" key (typically F) is vital. You must use it to search medicine cabinets for bandages, as bleeding out is the most common way new players lose their gear.

**Q: Do I need a physical key to enter the Red Barn?**

A: No, the Red Barn is a safe zone that is open to the player from the start of the game. It contains the Trader and does not require any special items to enter.

**Q: How do I check the time in-game?**

A: You must find and equip an Alarm Clock. Once it is in your inventory or equipped to your character, you can observe the time of day, which is a key factor in deciding whether to stay out or return to your cabin before nightfall.
