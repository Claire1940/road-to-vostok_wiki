## Navigating the Road to Vostok Minefield

Once you reach the border zone, the gameplay shifts into a slow, methodical crawl. The mines are scattered across the main road, the grassy shoulders, and near the trench lines. In 2026, the visual fidelity of these traps has been refined, making them slightly easier to see against flat textures but harder to spot in shadows.

### How to Spot and Clear Mines
1.  **Scanning the Ground:** Move at a crouched pace. Mines appear as small, circular metallic discs slightly protruding from the dirt.
2.  **Using Light:** During the day, the sun can create a slight glint on the metal. At night, NVGs highlight the contrast between the mine and the soil.
3.  **The "Shooting" Method:** Once a mine is spotted, back away to a distance of at least 15-20 meters. Use a scoped rifle to fire directly at the center of the disc. The resulting explosion will clear a small radius, making the path safe.
4.  **Memorizing Paths:** The mine placements are semi-static. Successful players often establish a "safe lane" along the rock walls or specific pipe structures and stick to that route every time.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid running through tall grass at all costs. The vegetation completely obscures mines, making it impossible to clear them without stepping on one.

## Combat: The Russian Security Forces

The minefield is overseen by four massive watchtowers occupied by Russian Security Force personnel. These NPCs are equipped with RK95 rifles and will engage you from extreme distances. 

### Tower Sniper Tactics
There are usually 10 soldiers distributed across the towers and the ground-level defensive positions. They tend to gravitate toward the towers because of the superior line of sight. To clear them, follow this tactical sequence:

1.  **The Rock Line Cover:** Upon entering the zone, move to the elevated rock line on the left. This provides high-ground cover and a clear view of the towers.
2.  **Pick Off the Snipers:** Use your Mosin to eliminate the guards in the towers first. They are often "bloody wankers" who stand still once they lose sight of you, providing a perfect window for a headshot.
3.  **Listen for the Whiz:** If you hear a bullet whiz but no muzzle report, the shooter is over 300 meters away. Check the furthest towers using your scope.
4.  **The Helicopter Factor:** A patrol helicopter frequently passes over the minefield. If it spots you in the open, it may coordinate with ground troops or suppress your position. Stay under the large pipes or inside the trenches when the rotor noise gets loud.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I defuse the mines in the Road to Vostok minefield without shooting them?**

A: Currently, there is no manual "defuse" mechanic. The only way to safely remove a mine is to detonate it using a firearm or an explosive like a grenade. Walking over them, even while prone, will trigger the pressure plate.

**Q: How many soldiers spawn in the border zone?**

A: Typically, there are 10 soldiers guarding the minefield area. They are often concentrated in the four watchtowers. Eliminating a full wave may trigger a secondary spawn, so always keep your ears open for fresh muzzle reports.

**Q: What happens if I step on a mine with Level 4 armor?**

A: Level 4 plates and a ballistic helmet significantly increase your survival chances. Instead of an instant death, you will likely suffer heavy bleeding (lacerations) and a severe concussion. You must immediately use a medical kit to stop the bleeding and find cover, as the explosion will alert nearby snipers to your exact location.

**Q: Does the helicopter in the minefield attack the player?**

A: The helicopter primarily acts as a reconnaissance unit. While it doesn't always fire directly at the player in the current demo version, its presence usually means the ground troops have been alerted to your position, making them more aggressive and accurate.**
