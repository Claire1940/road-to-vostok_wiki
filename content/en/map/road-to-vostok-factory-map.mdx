## Understanding the Road to Vostok Factory Map Layout

The Factory map is an industrial zone characterized by tight corridors, multi-level warehouses, and hazardous open yards. Unlike the relatively open "Village" areas found in the early 0-5 zones, the factory environment forces players into close-quarters combat (CQC) scenarios. Understanding the verticality of this map is essential, as AI snipers and guards often patrol catwalks and rooftops.

The game world is divided into three distinct zones, each with its own risk profile and loot pool. The Factory typically sits within the Border Zone, acting as a gateway to the high-risk Vostok area. 

| Zone Type | Difficulty | Loot Quality | Perma-death Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Starting Zone (0-5)** | Low | Basic Supplies | None (Safe) |
| **Border Zone** | Medium | Military Grade | Standard |
| **Vostok Zone** | Extreme | Rare/Legendary | **Active (Save Deletion)** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** When transitioning toward the Vostok maps, look for the red skull icon in the upper corner of your HUD. This indicates that perma-death is active, and dying will result in the deletion of all your save files.

## AI Factions and Combat Tactics

On the **road to vostok factory map**, you aren't just fighting the environment. You will encounter three distinct AI factions, each with varying levels of equipment and tactical intelligence. Clearing a map instance of its maximum 10 AI spawns is the only way to ensure total peace while looting.

### AI Faction Breakdown

| Faction | Equipment Level | Aggression | Special Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bandits** | Low (Pistols/Shotguns) | High | Often travel in groups; easy to hear. |
| **Guards** | Medium (AKs/SMGs) | Tactical | Use cover effectively; better armor. |
| **Military** | High (Top-tier Gear) | Lethal | High accuracy; often guard high-value loot. |

When entering a factory building, always use the "X" key to inspect your weapon and check your magazine capacity. Sound travels realistically in these environments, so consider using the middle mouse wheel to lower your weapon and move more quietly. If you encounter a Faction Boss (available in later updates), expect a significant increase in difficulty and rare loot rewards.

## Essential Gear and Ammo Compatibility

Survival on the **road to vostok factory map** is heavily dependent on your equipment's "Carry Capacity" (CC). You start with a limited CC of 10, but finding backpacks and tactical vests can increase this to 58 or higher. 

One of the most complex systems in Road to Vostok is ammo and magazine compatibility. You cannot simply drag any bullet into any gun. You must match the caliber to the magazine and the magazine to the weapon.

### Common Calibers and Magazines

| Weapon Type | Compatible Caliber | Magazine Type |
|---|---|---|
| **AK-12 / AKM** | 7.62x39mm | AKM Magazine |
| **Pistol (G17)** | 9x19mm | G17 Magazine |
| **Shotgun** | 12x70 Buckshot | Internal/Tube |
| **SMG** | .45 ACP | .45 Stick Magazine |

Before heading into a factory raid, ensure you have used the tool-tip interface to verify your ammo. Loading the wrong caliber will result in a weapon jam at the worst possible moment. Additionally, equip armor plates (NIJ Level 4 is the highest) to protect your vitals from high-penetration military rounds.

## Medical Management and Vitals

The factory environment is full of hazards, from rusty walkways to high-velocity rounds. Your HUD will alert you to medical conditions using specific icons. When an icon turns red, the condition is active and requires immediate treatment.

*   **Bleeding:** Requires bandages or medical kits.
*   **Fractures:** Requires splints to restore movement speed.
*   **Infections:** Requires antibiotics (common in medical crates).

You can monitor your hydration and hunger through the inventory screen. While the 2026 demo currently lacks some eating animations, the stats are fully functional. Consuming items like canned goods or clean water is vital for maintaining your stamina during long sprints across the factory floor.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Alt" key for fast-equipping items and "Ctrl" for fast-transferring loot from containers to your backpack. This minimizes the time you spend vulnerable in the inventory screen.

## Looting Strategies for the Factory

To maximize your efficiency on the **road to vostok factory map**, focus on high-value-to-weight ratio items. Industrial shelves often contain tools, electronics, and weapon attachments like ACOG scopes or PBS silencers.

1.  **Check Every Crate:** All objects that logically look like storage (boxes, lockers, duffel bags) are interactable.
2.  **Prioritize Keys:** Keys unlocked through trader tasks or found in the world are the only way to access safe shelters.
3.  **Trader Deliveries:** Collect items like toilet paper and lotion. While they seem mundane, generalist traders often request these for tasks that lower your "Trader Tax."
4.  **Decor Mode:** If you find a shelter near the factory, you can use the "F1" menu and middle mouse button to place furniture and customize your safe zone.

## Extraction and Simulation Time

Time management is a core mechanic. In Road to Vostok, 2.4 hours of real-life time equals 24 hours in-game. Simulation time progresses when you sleep in shelters or transition between maps. Before leaving the factory, check your alarm clock item to ensure you aren't attempting a transition during a pitch-black night cycle unless you are equipped with night vision.

Transitions have a "travel cost" related to your character's vitals. If you are starving or severely injured, you may not be able to cross the border safely. Always heal and hydrate at a shelter before moving to a new map instance. ## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best loot on the road to vostok factory map?**

A: High-tier loot is typically found in the locked administrative offices and the main assembly hall. Look for green military crates which have a higher chance of spawning armor plates and weapon attachments.

**Q: How do I save my progress?**

A: Progress is saved at Shelters. These are safe areas where you can store loot and skip simulation time. You must find or earn keys to unlock most shelters found near the factory and border zones.

**Q: Can I play the factory map with friends?**

A: As of the 2026 version, Road to Vostok is strictly a single-player experience. The developer focuses on a polished solo survival atmosphere similar to the *Stalker* games.

**Q: What happens if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: If you die in a map marked with the red Vostok skull, the game triggers perma-death. This deletes your entire save file, including all items stored in your shelters. Always bring your best gear and move cautiously in these areas.
