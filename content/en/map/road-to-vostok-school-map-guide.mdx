## Navigating the School Map Layout

The School map is situated in a location that veterans may recognize from earlier development builds as the former minefield transit area. The geography has been significantly expanded in 2026 to include a more dense urban-rural mix, centered around the educational facility and a prominent residential structure known as the "Big House."

When you first spawn into the map, your primary objective should be orientation. The map boundaries are clearly defined, but the interior paths are winding. Approaching the main school building directly from the front is often a suicide mission due to the lack of hard cover. Instead, seasoned players suggest moving along the left-hand side of the map. This route offers natural concealment and allows you to use environmental objects like trees, rocks, and debris to break line-of-sight from roaming AI.

## Essential Gear and Tactical Preparation

Before stepping foot onto the School map, your loadout must be meticulously curated. Road to Vostok does not forgive the unprepared. One of the most critical mechanics to master is weight management. Unlike many arcade shooters, your backpack in this game does not necessarily provide more "slots" for items; instead, it increases the total weight capacity you can carry before your stamina and movement speed are severely penalized.

### Weapon Selection and Fire Rates

For the close-quarters combat (CQC) found inside the school hallways, an automatic weapon like the MP5 is highly recommended. However, trigger discipline is paramount. You can inspect your weapon at any time to verify your current fire mode.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always check your fire rate before entering a building. While full-auto is tempting, using single-fire for mid-range engagements outside the school will save precious ammunition and reduce recoil-driven misses.

### Survival Supplies

Because the School map can involve lengthy stalemates with AI, you must bring an abundance of food and water. The 2026 update has made metabolic rates more realistic, meaning a long raid without sustenance will lead to tremors and reduced stamina recovery. Additionally, ensure you have a "Blanket" item in your inventory, which allows you to utilize designated sleep spots to recover exhaustion levels during longer incursions.

## Key Locations and Looting Strategy

The heart of this **road to vostok school map guide** lies in the locked rooms of the school itself. Looting is not as simple as walking through doors; you will need to find specific keys that have a chance to spawn across the map.

### The Classroom and Gymnasium Keys

There are two primary locked areas within the school: the Classrooms and the Gymnasium. These areas contain the highest concentration of medical supplies and military crates.

1.  **Classroom Key:** Usually found in residential areas or on the bodies of defeated AI near the Big House. It unlocks rooms filled with bandages, medkits, and occasionally rare chemical injectors.
2.  **Gymnasium Key:** This key provides access to the large sports hall. Inside, you can find military crates that house weapon attachments and high-grade ammunition.

## Combat Tactics and AI Engagement

The AI on the School map is notably more aggressive than in the starting zones. They often spawn in groups of two or three, with common spawn points located near the Big House and the main entrance of the school.

### Sniping Positions

If you are equipped with a long-range optic, the left side of the map (near the boundary) offers several elevated positions. From here, you can safely pick off enemies patrolling the exterior of the Big House before moving in to loot. This "clear then enter" strategy is the safest way to handle the school grounds.

### Using Cover Effectively

When you are under fire, always prioritize "hard cover" (concrete walls, thick trees) over "soft cover" (bushes, wooden fences). The AI in Road to Vostok can and will shoot through thin materials. If you are spotted, relocate immediately. The AI tends to suppress your last known position, allowing you to flank them if you move quickly and quietly.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not linger in the open areas between the school and the camp. These are "kill zones" with very little cover, making you an easy target for snipers.

## Extraction and Transit Points

Knowing how to get out is just as important as knowing how to get in. The School map features a transit point that leads to the **Outpost**. This is located near the small camp area, typically characterized by a few tents and a campfire.

To successfully transit or extract:
*   Ensure the immediate area is clear of hostiles.
*   Check your weight; if you are over-encumbered, you may not be able to sprint to the extraction trigger if an ambush occurs.
*   Keep your weapon raised and check corners as you approach the transit zone.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to find the Classroom Key on the School map?**

A: While spawns are not 100% guaranteed, the Classroom Key is most frequently found inside the "Big House" or on AI scavengers that patrol the perimeter of the school building. Check jackets and drawers specifically.

**Q: How do I change my fire mode in Road to Vostok?**

A: You can check your fire mode by using the inspect weapon command. This will show if your weapon is set to single-fire or full-auto. Generally, pressing the fire mode toggle key (default 'B' on most layouts) will switch between them. This **road to vostok school map guide** recommends single-fire for most outdoor engagements.

**Q: Can I sleep anywhere on the School map?**

A: No, you can only sleep at designated camp areas or safe spots if you have a blanket in your inventory. There is a small camp located near the transit point to the Outpost that serves as a primary resting spot for players.

**Q: What should I do if I run out of food while at the School?**

A: The School's kitchenette area and the nearby Big House often have canned goods and water bottles. However, it is always safer to bring your own supplies, as loot spawns can be unpredictable in the 2026 version of the game.
