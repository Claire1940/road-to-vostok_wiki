## Understanding the World Map and Navigation

In Road to Vostok, navigation is an active gameplay mechanic rather than a passive UI element. You do not have a magical GPS; instead, you must find and utilize physical items to orient yourself. Tactical maps are rare but vital items that allow you to view the entire world map. Because the maps in this game are among the largest seen in the survival genre, getting lost is a genuine threat to your survival.

The world is divided into various zones connected by transit points. These transits act as the gateways between maps, often requiring specific conditions or simply finding the physical boundary to trigger the move. To move efficiently, you should familiarize yourself with the landmarks that define each zone.

## Detailed Guide to the School Map Location

The School is one of the most iconic and dangerous **road to vostok all map locations**. It is a multi-layered environment that rewards careful exploration but punishes reckless sprinting. Located near the minefield transit, the School serves as a hub for mid-tier loot and intense close-quarters combat.

### Entering the School
When approaching the School, it is generally safer to flank from the left side. This approach provides better natural cover and allows players equipped with long-range optics to scout the main building for hostiles. There are typically at least two to three enemy spawns in this area, often patrolling the perimeter or lurking inside the large house adjacent to the school.

### Key Rooms and Loot

## Tactical Movement Between Map Locations

Traveling between **road to vostok all map locations** involves managing your physical state as much as your ammunition. The game features a "Weapon Stance" mechanic that directly impacts your survival during transitions.

### Weapon Stances and Fatigue
Using the mouse scroll wheel, you can toggle between high and low weapon stances.
*   **High Stance:** Your weapon is up and ready. This is essential when entering rooms or turning corners in dense locations like the School or Outpost. However, this drains arm fatigue over time.
*   **Low Stance:** Your weapon is lowered, allowing your arm fatigue to recover. Use this while traversing open fields between major landmarks.

### Light Sources for Night Exploration
Many of the more rewarding locations are shrouded in darkness or feature deep interiors. You must manage your light sources to navigate these areas safely.

1.  **Arva:** A basic pocket torch. It provides minimal light but is better than total darkness.
2.  **Polaris:** A head-mounted torch that offers superior illumination while keeping your hands free for weapon handling.
3.  **Night Vision Goggles (NVGs):** Found in high-tier military locations. These are the gold standard for night-time navigation across the border.

## Weapon Handling for Different Terrains

The weapons in Road to Vostok are physical objects with complex interactions. Adjusting your weapon to suit the specific map location you are in is a vital skill.

*   **Scope Adjustment:** You can physically move scopes closer or further along the rail. A forward-set scope is better for target acquisition in close-quarters locations like the School, while a rear-set scope offers a better sight picture for sniping across the Outpost valleys.
*   **Fire Selectors:** Always inspect your weapon (default 'I') to check your fire mode. Western rifles often show the selector on the left, while Eastern rifles (AK variants) require you to rotate the inspection view to the right side to see the lever.
*   **Canted Stance:** If you have a laser attachment, you can use a canted stance. This allows you to use a long-range optic for distance while relying on the laser for hip-fire accuracy in tight corridors.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find my current position on the map?**

A: There is no "You Are Here" marker on the tactical map. You must orient yourself by looking for major landmarks like the School, the Outpost, or specific terrain features like ridgelines and roads. Finding **road to vostok all map locations** requires manual reconnaissance.

**Q: Are map transitions permanent?**

A: No, you can move back and forth between maps as long as you can reach the transit point. However, be aware that enemies may respawn or reposition when you re-enter a zone.

**Q: Where is the best place to find high-tier loot?**

A: Military areas and locked rooms within the School or Outpost offer the best rewards. Look for olive-drab military crates, as these often contain the best weapon attachments and NVGs.

**Q: How do I save my progress while exploring different locations?**

A: You need to find a place to sleep, which requires a blanket item. Some safe zones or secluded houses have areas where you can use a blanket to rest, which also serves as a way to pass time and save your current state.
