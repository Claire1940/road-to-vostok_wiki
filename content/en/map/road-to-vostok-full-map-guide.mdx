## Understanding the World Layout and Tactical Maps

The world of Road to Vostok is divided into several interconnected maps, each with its own atmosphere, loot tables, and threats. Unlike traditional shooters, you cannot simply pull up a digital GPS at any time. You must find physical tactical maps within the world to see the full scope of your surroundings. These maps are vital for planning your route from Point A to Point B, especially since some areas are separated by dangerous transit zones like minefields or military outposts.

| Map Feature | Description | Importance |
|---|---|---|
| **Tactical Maps** | Physical items found in the world that reveal the layout. | **High** - Essential for long-term navigation. |
| **Transit Points** | Specific locations that allow you to move between different maps. | **Critical** - These are your only path forward. |
| **Loot Zones** | Areas like the School or Outpost with high density of items. | **Medium** - High risk for high reward. |
| **Extraction** | Points where you can safely leave the zone with your gear. | **Critical** - Essential for permanent progression. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a tactical map in your quick-access slots if possible. The maps are some of the largest seen in the genre, and getting lost usually leads to starvation or an unexpected enemy encounter.

## The School Map: Navigation and Loot

The School is one of the most iconic and dangerous locations you will encounter early in your journey. Located near the minefield transit, it serves as a major loot hub but is heavily guarded by AI scavengers. When approaching the School, it is recommended to come from the left side. This approach offers better natural cover and allows you to scout the perimeter before committing to an entry.

### Key Locations in the School
The School is not just a single building but a complex. To fully explore it, you will need specific keys that do not always have a guaranteed spawn rate. You should be on the lookout for the **Classroom Key** and the **Gymnasium Key**. These unlock rooms containing high-tier medical supplies and military crates.

| Location | Key Required | Notable Loot |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Classroom** | Classroom Key | Medic supplies, food, ammo. |
| **Gymnasium** | Gymnasium Key | Military crates, weapon parts. |
| **The Big House** | None | Blankets (for sleeping), general loot. |
| **Campfire Area** | None | Cooking spot, temporary rest. |

### Tactical Approach to School
There are typically at least three to five enemies spawning around the School area. One usually patrols near the entrance, while others stay inside the main buildings. If you have a long-range setup, there is a sniping position near the map boundary that overlooks the "Big House," allowing you to pick off targets before they even know you're there.

## Survival Mechanics: Fatigue and Stances

In Road to Vostok, your physical state is just as important as your ammunition count. The game features a unique "Arm Fatigue" system. This bar drains whenever you are aiming down sights (ADS) or holding your weapon in a "High Stance." 

### Weapon Stances Explained
You can toggle between high and low weapon stances using your mouse scroll wheel while not in ADS. 
1. **High Stance:** Your weapon is up and ready. This allows for faster reaction times when entering a room or turning a corner. However, it constantly drains arm fatigue.
2. **Low Stance:** Your weapon is lowered. This preserves your stamina and fatigue, allowing you to travel longer distances without needing to rest your arms.

| Stance Type | Fatigue Drain | Readiness | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low Stance** | None | Low | Traveling, looting safe areas. |
| **High Stance** | **Moderate** | **High** | Clearing rooms, urban combat. |
| **ADS (Aiming)** | **High** | **Maximum** | Precision shooting, long-range. |

> **Warning:** Entering a combat situation with zero arm fatigue will result in massive weapon sway, making it nearly impossible to hit targets at medium range.

## Advanced Weapon Handling

Weapons in this game are physical objects with complex interactions. You cannot simply press "R" and expect an instant reload for every firearm. For example, shotguns and the Mosin-Nagant require you to manually open the chamber and load each individual round. Furthermore, you must manually rack the slide or bolt after reloading or firing to chamber the next round.

### Fire Selectors and Inspection
Knowing your fire mode is critical. You can inspect your weapon to see the physical position of the fire selector. 
*   **Western Rifles:** Usually have the selector on the left.
*   **Eastern Rifles (AK-style):** The selector is on the right. You may need to rotate your view using the scroll wheel during inspection to see it. 
*   **Pro Tip:** On Eastern rifles, the selector must be in the "Up" position for full-auto.

## Essential Gear for Exploration

A **road to vostok full map guide** wouldn't be complete without a breakdown of the gear needed to survive the night. Lighting is one of the most overlooked aspects of the game. When the sun goes down, the Finnish woods become pitch black.

1. **ARVA Pocket Torch:** A basic light source. It isn't very powerful, but it's better than nothing.
2. **Polaris Head Torch:** A much more effective light that stays on your head, keeping your hands free for your weapon.
3. **Night Vision Goggles (NVGs):** Rare military-grade gear found in high-security outposts. These are a game-changer for night raids.
4. **Canted Sights:** If you have a long-range scope and a laser attachment, you can use a canted stance. This allows you to use the laser as a secondary aiming point for close-quarters combat without removing your magnification.

### Medical Crafting
Injuries are common, and trips to the doctor are expensive and dangerous. You can craft basic medical supplies in the field using resources found in the environment.

| Crafted Item | Ingredients | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Splint** | Sticks + Rags + Duct Tape | Fixes broken limbs. |
| **Tourniquet** | Sticks + Rags | Stops heavy bleeding. |
| **Bandage** | Rags | Stops light bleeding. |

## Transit and Progression

Moving between maps is the core loop of the game. For instance, from the School, you can transit to the **Outpost**. These transit points are often located at the edges of the map and are marked by specific landmarks. Before you transit, ensure you have enough food and water, as you might spawn into a more hostile environment where resources are scarce.

Backpacks in Road to Vostok are unique; they do not give you more "slots" in a traditional grid sense. Instead, they increase the total weight capacity you can carry. This means you can bring more heavy gear, ammo, and loot back to your shelter, but you are still limited by the physical size of the items in your inventory.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the Classroom Key in the School map?**

A: The Classroom Key is a random spawn within the School area. Check the smaller residential buildings and the "Big House" nearby. It is not a guaranteed 100% spawn, so you may need to visit the area multiple times.

**Q: What should I do if my arm fatigue is constantly low?**

A: Stop using the "High Stance" (weapon up) when you are just walking. Use the scroll wheel to lower your weapon to the "Low Stance." This will allow your fatigue to regenerate while you move.

**Q: Is there a way to see the road to vostok full map guide in-game?**

A: There is no built-in "mini-map." You must find physical Tactical Maps or World Maps as loot items. Once found, you can inspect them to see your general location and the connections between different zones.

**Q: Why can't I fire my shotgun after reloading?**

A: Many weapons in Road to Vostok require manual cycling. After loading shells into a shotgun or a round into a bolt-action rifle, you must press the bolt/rack key to chamber the first round before the weapon is ready to fire.
