## Navigating the Road to Vostok Demo Map Zones

The world of Road to Vostok is not a single open-world expanse but a series of interconnected map instances divided into three distinct difficulty tiers. Each tier offers different loot pools, environmental hazards, and AI density. Understanding where you are on the map is vital for planning your equipment loadout.

| Zone Tier | Difficulty | Loot Quality | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starting Zone (Area 0-5)** | Low | Basic Supplies / Low-tier Firearms | Minimal |
| **Border Zone** | Medium | Military Grade / Specialized Tools | Moderate |
| **Vostok Zone** | Extreme | High-End Gear / Rare Artifacts | Permanent Death |

The demo specifically highlights the "Village" area within the 0-5 Starting Zone. This area acts as your introduction to the game’s mechanics, providing shelters to save your progress and traders to exchange scavenged goods for essential survival items like medical supplies and ammunition.

## Survival Mechanics and Vital Systems

Before venturing deep into the **road to vostok demo map**, you must familiarize yourself with the character's vitals and equipment limitations. Unlike more arcade-style shooters, every item in Road to Vostok has weight and logical storage requirements.

### Carry Capacity (CC) and Inventory
Your base carry capacity is strictly limited. To increase it, you must find and equip tactical vests, backpacks, and windbreakers. 
*   **Tactical Vests:** Provide quick-access slots for magazines and grenades.
*   **Backpacks:** Significant CC increase but may impact movement or visibility.
*   **Containers:** Items like duffel bags and crates found in the world are interactable loot containers.

### Medical Conditions
The HUD tracks your health through a series of icons. When an icon turns red, a specific condition is active (such as bleeding or infection) and requires a matching medical item to cure. Ignoring these symptoms will lead to rapid health depletion, especially during long treks between shelters.

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry at least one bandage and one antiseptic. In the early game, even a minor scrap with a bandit can turn fatal if you cannot stop the bleeding.

## Weaponry and Combat Tactics

The combat in Road to Vostok is grounded in realism. Weapons require specific magazines and compatible ammunition types. For instance, an AK-12 will not function with standard AKM magazines if the calibers differ. You must manually inspect weapons (X-key) to check chambered rounds and magazine status.

### AI Factions
The **road to vostok demo map** is populated by three distinct AI factions, each with varying levels of equipment and aggression.

| Faction | Equipment Tier | Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bandits** | Low (Pistols/Shotguns) | Aggressive, poor accuracy, often travel in small groups. |
| **Guards** | Medium (SMGs/ARs) | Defensive, better tactics, protect specific loot areas. |
| **Military** | High (Top-tier Gear) | Highly lethal, uses suppressive fire and flanking. |

Eliminating all AI in a specific map instance grants you temporary "peace," allowing you to loot the area without the constant threat of an ambush. However, be warned that faction bosses—while rare—can spawn with unique gear that far outclasses standard enemies.

## Exploring the Village: Key Locations

In the current demo, the Village serves as the primary playground for survivors. It is a dense area filled with residential houses, saunas, and police outposts.

1.  **The Shelters:** These are safe areas where you can save your progress and customize your living space using the "Decor Mode" (F1). Shelters are often locked and require specific keys obtained through trader tasks.
2.  **The Trader Generalist:** An essential NPC who provides tasks and sells survival basics. Completing his deliveries (such as finding lotion or toilet paper) lowers the "Trader Tax," making your future purchases significantly cheaper.
3.  **The Road:** The main asphalt path through the map. While it offers the fastest travel, it is also the most exposed area, making you an easy target for Bandits or Military patrols.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid staying in the open for too long. AI in Road to Vostok has sharp eyesight and can engage from significant distances if you aren't utilizing cover.

## The Vostok Zone: Understanding Permadeath

The most defining feature of the **road to vostok demo map** is the transition into the Vostok zone. This is a high-risk, high-reward area where the rules of the game change fundamentally. 

When you approach a transition point leading to Vostok, the game will provide a clear warning. Once you cross over, a red skull icon appears on your screen. In this zone, **death is permanent**. Dying in Vostok does not just reset your current run; it deletes your entire save file, including all progress made in the starting zones and shelters. This mechanic forces a level of tension rarely seen in single-player games, requiring players to weigh the value of rare loot against the risk of losing everything.

## Armor and Protection Systems

To survive the more dangerous sections of the map, you must invest in the armor system. Protection levels are directly linked to the penetration stats of the ammunition being fired at you.

| Armor Level | Protection Capability | Recommended Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1-2** | Stops low-caliber pistol rounds and buckshot. | Scavenging in Area 0-5. |
| **Level 3** | Reliable against common rifle rounds (5.45/5.56). | Border Zone exploration. |
| **Level 4+** | High-end protection against AP rounds. | Entering the Vostok Zone. |

Helmets are equally important as they prevent critical medical conditions resulting from headshots. You can also find individual armor plates to insert into compatible tactical vests, allowing for a modular approach to defense.

## Essential Tips for New Survivors

*   **Check Your Mags:** Always ensure your magazines are loaded with the correct ammo type (e.g., 9x18 vs 9x19) before leaving a shelter.
*   **Manage Simulation Time:** The game world operates on a 2.4-hour IRL cycle for a full 24-hour in-game day. Use alarm clocks to manage your sleep and time your excursions for daylight hours if you lack night-vision equipment.
*   **Loot Logically:** Focus on high-value, low-weight items like medical supplies and rare ammo. Bulky items like furniture should only be moved when you have cleared the immediate path of enemies.
*   **Use the Tutorial:** The interactive tutorial in the demo is a slideshow-style guide that explains complex inventory interactions. It is highly recommended for those struggling with the UI.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the road to vostok demo map?**

A: The demo map is accessible via the main menu after completing or skipping the tutorial. It currently features the "Village" instance within the Starting Zone (Area 0-5).

**Q: Does the demo include multiplayer?**

A: No, Road to Vostok is designed strictly as a single-player survival experience. The focus is on atmospheric immersion and challenging AI encounters.

**Q: What happens if I die in the starting village?**

A: If you die in the starting village (Area 0-5) or the Border Zone, you will lose the items currently on your person, but your save file and shelter progress remain intact. Only deaths in the Vostok zone result in a total save wipe.

**Q: How do I save my game in Road to Vostok?**

A: Saving is tied to Shelters. You must locate a shelter, ensure you have the required key (if applicable), and interact with the save point inside. Saving also advances the simulation time.
