## Locating the Road to Vostok Cave and Shelters

## Survival and Resource Management

Finding the cave is only half the battle; you must also manage your physical and mental state. The transcript from veteran explorers suggests that hydration is the most common cause of death during long-range runs to Vostok.

### Hydration and Nutrition
If you are planning a trip to the **road to vostok cave location**, pack at least three sources of liquid. 
*   **Juice Boxes:** Lightweight and provide a decent hydration boost.
*   **Water Bottles:** The gold standard for survival.
*   **Tomatoes:** A secondary source of hydration if you run out of liquids.

> **💡 Tip:** Avoid eating crackers or dry snacks if your hydration is below 40%. These items will drastically reduce your water levels and can lead to a quick death if you are far from a trader.

### Mental Health and Fishing
Road to Vostok introduces a "Mental" stat that fluctuates based on your actions. 
*   **Raw Fish Penalty:** Eating raw fish results in a **-80 Mental** penalty. 
*   **Fishing Spots:** Use a fishing rod at designated docks to catch food. Always cook your catch at a shelter's cooking station to maintain your mental health and maximize nutritional value.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the specific Road to Vostok cave location for the second shelter?**

A: The second major shelter, often referred to as the tent or rock shelter, is located in the Outpost map. After transitioning from the School, head to the far left ridge. It is situated near the "last gun" landmark and provides a safe spot to sleep and store items in a freezer.

**Q: Do I need a key to enter every Road to Vostok cave location?**

A: No. Natural rock shelters and tents are open to all players. However, the reinforced underground bunkers require a Bunker Key, which is a rare loot drop or a high-tier trade item.

**Q: How do I survive the Minefield transition to reach Vostok?**

A: Watch for green cylinders sticking out of the ground. These are the mines. You can trigger them by shooting from a distance, but be aware that the explosion will attract nearby AI. It is safer to slowly navigate around them while maintaining high hydration levels.

**Q: Can AI follow me into a cave or shelter?**

A: Yes, NPCs can open doors and enter shelters. It is recommended to close all doors behind you and listen for the "double footstep" sound glitch, which often indicates an NPC is moving nearby.
