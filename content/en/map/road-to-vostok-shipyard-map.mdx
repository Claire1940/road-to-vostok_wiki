## Overview of the Shipyard Zone

The Shipyard is characterized by its industrial atmosphere and heavy snow cover. Unlike the more rural Village area, this map is dense with large buildings, silos, and maritime infrastructure. The verticality of the map is a significant factor, with catwalks and rooftops providing vantage points that can be both a blessing and a curse.

When you first enter the **road to vostok shipyard map**, you will likely notice the immediate shift in scale. The buildings are massive, often containing multiple floors of lootable containers, office spaces, and industrial machinery. The presence of the large container ship at the docks serves as a primary landmark and a high-risk, high-reward looting destination.

## Looting Strategies and Hotspots

Looting on the **road to vostok shipyard map** is a systematic process. Because the map is so large, running aimlessly will quickly drain your energy and hydration. You should prioritize buildings based on your current needs. If you are low on medicine, the office sections of the industrial buildings are your best bet. If you need firepower, head toward the docks.

### The Container Ship
The ship is perhaps the most iconic part of the **road to vostok shipyard map**. Accessing it requires crossing open docks, which leaves you vulnerable. However, the containers on board often hold rare items like the Pro Optic, Holo sights, and even the Mosen bolt-action rifle. 

### Industrial Interiors
Inside the massive warehouses, you will find a variety of loot:
*   **Electronics:** Look for floppy disks and hard drives near computer clusters.
*   **Clothing:** Windbreakers and boots are essential for managing your temperature in the 2026 survival mechanics.
*   **Weapon Parts:** Tables and workbenches often spawn magazines and small attachments.

## Combat and AI Behavior

The AI in Road to Vostok is designed to be punishing. On the Shipyard map, they utilize the environment effectively. They are known to notice flashlights during night raids or in dark interiors, so light management is crucial. 

### Engagement Rules
1.  **Identify Before Shooting:** Ammo is scarce. Don't waste rounds on targets at extreme ranges unless you have a scoped rifle like the Mosen.
2.  **Use Cover:** The industrial machinery provides excellent hard cover. Avoid standing in the middle of the dock areas.
3.  **Listen for Footsteps:** The sound of boots on wood or metal catwalks is a dead giveaway for enemy movement.

## Survival and Medical Management

The **road to vostok shipyard map** is unforgiving to those who ignore their physical condition. Fractures are one of the most dangerous status effects in the game. A fall from a catwalk or a shipping container can result in a broken leg, which slows your movement to a crawl. Without a splint, a fracture is often a death sentence if you are far from an extraction point.

### Essential Medical Kit
*   **Splints:** Absolutely mandatory for the vertical gameplay of the Shipyard.
*   **Bandages/Medkits:** To stop bleeding and restore HP after firefights.
*   **Painkillers:** To manage the effects of injuries while heading to extraction.
*   **Food/Water:** Items like peaches, soup, and lemonade are vital for maintaining energy levels during long raids.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to "parkour" between shipping containers without a splint in your inventory. The fall damage calculation in the 2026 build is highly realistic and punishing.

## Environmental Hazards: Snow and Cold

Temperature management is a core pillar of the Road to Vostok experience. The Shipyard is a snowy zone, meaning your body temperature will drop over time if you aren't wearing appropriate gear. 

1.  **Find Shelter:** Use the interior fires or heaters found in some buildings to warm up.
2.  **Equip Proper Clothing:** Boots, hats, and windbreakers provide insulation stats. Check the item description for the "Insulation" value.
3.  **Keep Moving:** While stealth is important, staying stationary in the snow for too long will accelerate your temperature loss.

## Navigating the Catwalks

The catwalk system on the **road to vostok shipyard map** allows you to bypass many ground-level threats, but it makes you highly visible to anyone looking up. If you choose to use the elevated paths, do so quickly and use the metal railings for whatever slight cover they provide. This is the best place to use optics to scout the area before descending into the high-loot zones of the docks.

### Extraction Strategy
When your backpack is full of loot—perhaps a new Pro Optic or a stack of 7.62 rounds—you need to plan your exit. Most players choose to loop back toward the Village. Ensure you have cleared the path behind you, as AI can occasionally wander into areas you previously thought were safe.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the KP31 on the Road to Vostok Shipyard map?**

A: The KP31 SMG is most commonly found inside the industrial buildings or on the container ship. It often spawns in a low-durability state, so be sure to check its condition before relying on it in a fight.

**Q: How do I fix a fracture in Road to Vostok?**

A: You must use a splint or a high-tier medkit that includes fracture repair. If you don't have one, you will need to limp to the nearest trader or search medical crates in the office sections of the Shipyard.

**Q: Are there mines on the Shipyard map?**

A: There are rumors of mines in certain high-security areas of the **road to vostok shipyard map**. While not every raid features them, it is best to stay on paved paths or clear floors when entering restricted military-style zones.

**Q: Does the flashlight attract enemies?**

A: Yes, the AI is programmed to react to light sources. In the dark interiors of the Shipyard, using a flashlight can give away your position to nearby scavengers. Use it sparingly or only when you are certain the area is clear.
