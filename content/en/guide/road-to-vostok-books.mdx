## The Survivor's Handbook: Health and Nutrition

In *Road to Vostok*, health is a finite resource that disappears quickly. Players have a base of 100 health, but a mere four shots from an enemy AI can result in a permanent end to your journey. Understanding the "books" on field medicine and nutrition is the difference between extraction and death.

Currently, the most reliable way to restore health is through traditional Finnish pea soup. While future updates are expected to bring more complex medical items, mastering the basics of nutrition is vital for any long-term expedition.

| Item | Health Restored | Rarity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pea Soup** | 10% | Common | Found in kitchens and residential areas. |
| **Red Cans** | Variable | Rare | High-value loot used for inventory filling challenges. |
| **Medical Kits** | TBD | Very Rare | Expected in later 2026 updates for trauma. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry at least two cans of pea soup. Since each use only heals 10%, you need a significant stockpile to recover from a serious gunfight.

## Weapon Handling and Tactical Manuals

The combat in this game is governed by procedural weapon handling, which simulates the physical weight and sway of firearms. Unlike arcade shooters, you cannot simply hold down the trigger and expect accuracy. You must manage two distinct types of stamina: Body and Arm.

### Arm Stamina and Aiming
Holding your weapon in a high-ready position or aiming down sights (ADS) drains your arm stamina. As this bar depletes, your aim will begin to sway in "figure-eight" patterns, making long-range shots nearly impossible.

| Action | Arm Stamina Drain | Accuracy Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Hip Firing** | Moderate | High (Sway increases over time) |
| **Full Auto Fire** | Very High | Severe (30% drain per mag) |
| **Semi-Auto Fire** | Low | Minimal |
| **Weapon Swapping** | None | Resets posture; allows for stamina recovery |

## Navigating the Border Zone: Loot and Caches

While players often look for **road to vostok books** to find secret maps, the reality of the border zone is that loot is dynamic. Ground caches are one of the most important features for high-tier progression. These are bluish boxes hidden under shrubbery that contain rare weapons like the Mosin-Nagant.

### Essential Keybinds for Looting
To interact with the world effectively, you must master the physical item system. You don't just "click" items into an inventory; you can physically manipulate them in the game world.

*   **Middle Mouse Button:** Open containers (cabinets, crates, tents).
*   **G Key:** Grab a physical item.
*   **Mouse Wheel (While Grabbing):** Move the item forward or backward.
*   **Right Mouse (While Grabbing):** Spin the item for perfect placement on shelves.

## Advanced Movement Tactics

Knowledge found in the metaphorical **road to vostok books** of veteran players often includes movement exploits and "tech" that can save your life. 

1. **Inertia Transfer:** Running at full tilt and then crouching allows you to slide into cover quickly.
2. **Jump-Crouching:** Crouching mid-air helps you clear higher obstacles and land in a smaller profile.
3. **Short-Burst Sprinting:** By pulsing the sprint key, you can maintain high momentum while minimizing the drain on your arm stamina, keeping you "combat-ready" even while moving fast.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there actual road to vostok books I can read in-game?**

A: Currently, "books" primarily refer to the lore documents and survival guides found in the game's UI and community resources. However, the developer has hinted at more world-building items, including notes and manuals, being added to the loot pool in late 2026.

**Q: How do I find the hidden ground caches?**

A: Ground caches are bluish boxes tucked away in dense foliage. They spawn randomly, but they often appear near unique landmarks like the power array or specific rock formations. They are the best source for high-tier rifles in the demo.

**Q: What happens if I die on the Vostok side of the border?**

A: The Vostok side is the "permadeath" zone. If you die there, your entire character save file is deleted. On the Area 5 (Finland) side, you only lose the gear you are currently carrying.

**Q: How do I fix the "figure-eight" weapon sway?**

A: This is caused by low arm stamina. To fix it, lower your weapon (scroll down on the mouse wheel), stop sprinting, or find cover to let your character catch their breath. Switching to a secondary weapon can also provide a brief window of stability.
