<Section icon={Wrench} title="Shelter Customization and Item Placement">

One of the unique aspects of Road to Vostok is the degree of control you have over your shelter's interior. Customization isn't just cosmetic; it's a functional mechanic that helps you organize your loot and optimize your space.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter Decoration Mode">
    Once inside your shelter, activate "Decoration Mode." This allows you to interact with furniture and items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Catalog, Move, and Rotate Furniture">
    Every shelter starts with a default furniture layout. In decoration mode, you can select individual furniture pieces, move them to new positions, and rotate them to fit your preference. This helps create optimal pathways and storage areas.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Acquire New Furniture">
    Expand your customization options by acquiring new furniture. This can be done through crafting at a workbench (if available in the shelter) or by trading with specific vendors. Look for furniture blueprints or trade offers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize Free Item Placement">
    Beyond furniture, you can place individual items anywhere within your shelter. This is where the physics-based looting system shines. Items are not confined to inventory slots; you can literally drop them on shelves, tables, or the floor. Their positions save automatically when you exit the shelter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Maximize Storage Efficiency">
    Use furniture containers (like chests or lockers) for bulk storage of less frequently accessed items. For quick access, place essential gear or recent loot on accessible surfaces using free item placement. This helps maintain a clear inventory for your next run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
The **physics-based item placement** means you can truly organize your shelter visually. Items stay where you put them, adding a layer of immersion and personal touch to your safe house.
</Callout>

</Section>
