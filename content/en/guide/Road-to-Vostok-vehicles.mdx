<Section icon={Target} title="Acquisition and Survival Strategies for Vehicles">
Acquiring a vehicle in Road to Vostok will undoubtedly be a high-stakes endeavor. Given the game's emphasis on scarcity, vehicles are unlikely to be found easily or in pristine condition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate a Derelict Vehicle">
    Scour specific high-risk areas, such as abandoned military bases, industrial zones, or larger towns, where vehicles might be found. These locations will likely be heavily guarded or contain significant environmental threats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assess Vehicle Condition">
    Once found, inspect the vehicle for missing parts (tires, engine components, battery), fuel levels, and overall damage. A thorough assessment will determine the resources needed for repair.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gather Necessary Resources">
    Collect essential items like fuel cans, repair kits, spare tires, and specific engine parts. These resources will be rare and highly valuable, making resource runs inherently risky.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Perform Field Repairs">
    Repair the vehicle using collected items. This process might be time-consuming and expose the player to danger. Consider establishing a temporary safe zone around the vehicle during repairs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Secure and Maintain">
    Once operational, ensure the vehicle is always fueled and in good repair. Consider caching spare parts and fuel along frequently traveled routes. Parking in hidden, secure locations is crucial to prevent theft or damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Vehicle Security">
Securing your vehicle from other players (if multiplayer elements are introduced) or environmental damage will be paramount. Hidden stashes and fortified garages could become vital strategic assets.
</Callout>

Vehicles, once acquired, would become critical assets. Players would need to weigh the benefits of speed and cargo against the risks of increased noise, fuel consumption, and the potential for attracting unwanted attention.

</Section>
