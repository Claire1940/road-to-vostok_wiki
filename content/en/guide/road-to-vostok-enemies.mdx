While the darkness of the Finnish forests is terrifying, turning on a light source effectively places a target on your chest. Enemies may struggle to see in the dark, but they are hyper-sensitive to light sources, allowing them to spot you from over 200 meters away while you can only see two meters ahead.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Engagement and the 50m Rule">

Engaging **road to vostok enemies** requires a strict adherence to the "Golden Rule of 50 Meters." The AI is intentionally tuned to be absurdly lethal at close range. If you attempt to clear a building or a tight hallway using "run-and-gun" tactics, the AI's reaction time will almost always beat yours. At 10 meters, the bandit has the advantage; at 50 meters, you have the tactical upper hand.

Precision is further complicated by the Arm Stamina system. This is a hidden mechanic separate from your overall movement fatigue. Holding your weapon in the "ready" position or firing long bursts drains your arm strength. Once the arm icon turns red, your weapon sway becomes uncontrollable, making it impossible to land shots even on stationary targets.

| Engagement Zone | Threat Level | Recommended Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0m - 15m** | **Critical** | Avoid direct confrontation; use grenades or wait for them to move. |
| **15m - 50m** | **High** | Use hard cover; never peek the same angle twice. |
| **50m - 100m** | **Moderate** | Ideal engagement range for rifles; maintain distance. |
| **100m+** | **Low** | Sniper territory; AI detection is significantly reduced. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Managing Arm Stamina">
During a prolonged firefight, you must consciously lower your weapon (using the 'lower weapon' keybind) to recover arm stamina. It feels counter-intuitive to put your gun down while being shot at, but 2 seconds of rest is the difference between a headshot and a wasted magazine.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Physical Looting System">

One of the most frustrating moments for new players is killing a bandit and finding their inventory empty. In most survival games, the weapon is part of the loot UI. In Road to Vostok, the looting system is manual and physical. The weapon the enemy was using remains a physical object in the game world, often still clutched in the NPC's hands or lying on the ground next to the body.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Neutralize the Threat">
    Ensure all enemies in the immediate area are dealt with. Never attempt to loot while a second bandit is active, as map transitions can trigger respawns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Physical Weapon">
    Look at the NPC's hands or the ground within a 1-meter radius of the body. The rifle will be visible as a 3D model.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manual Interaction">
    Hover your reticle directly over the weapon model until the interaction prompt appears. Click specifically on the weapon to add it to your hands or backpack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check the Inventory UI">
    Only after grabbing the physical weapon should you open the inventory (Tab) to loot smaller items like ammo, bandages, or food.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Loot Respawns">
Road to Vostok uses a zone-based reset. Every time you transition between maps or return to your shelter, the loot—and more importantly, the bandits—will respawn. Never assume a previously cleared area is safe after a loading screen.
</Callout>

</Section>

Entering the Vostok zone is an irreversible tactical decision. If you die within the borders of Vostok, the game deletes your entire save profile. This includes your weapon stash at the shelter, your base builds, and every bit of progress you've made since starting the game. It is the ultimate "Hardcore" experience.
