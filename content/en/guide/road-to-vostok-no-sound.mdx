<Section icon={Wrench} title="Advanced Troubleshooting Steps for No Sound">
If basic checks don't resolve the "Road to Vostok no sound" problem, you'll need to delve deeper into your system and game files. These steps address common software conflicts, driver issues, and file corruption that can lead to audio malfunctions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update Audio Drivers">
    Outdated or corrupted audio drivers are a frequent cause of sound problems.
    - Open **Device Manager** (search in Windows).
    - Expand "Sound, video and game controllers."
    - Right-click your audio device (e.g., Realtek High Definition Audio, NVIDIA High Definition Audio) and select "Update driver."
    - Choose "Search automatically for updated driver software." If no new drivers are found, visit your motherboard manufacturer's or sound card manufacturer's website for the latest drivers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Verify Game Files via Steam">
    Corrupted game files can lead to various in-game issues, including missing audio.
    - Open your **Steam Library**.
    - Right-click on **Road to Vostok** and select "Properties."
    - Go to the "Installed Files" tab.
    - Click "Verify integrity of game files..." Steam will check and re-download any missing or corrupted files.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Run Game as Administrator">
    Sometimes, permission issues can prevent the game from accessing audio resources.
    - Navigate to your **Road to Vostok installation directory**.
    - Right-click the game's executable file (e.g., `RoadToVostok.exe`).
    - Select "Properties," then the "Compatibility" tab.
    - Check the box "Run this program as an administrator."
    - Click "Apply" and "OK."
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check DirectX/Vulkan Compatibility">
    Road to Vostok's official FAQ mentions Windows/Linux and DirectX/Vulkan support. Ensure your system's configuration is optimal.
    - Confirm your graphics drivers are up to date, as they often include DirectX/Vulkan runtime components.
    - If the game allows, try switching between DirectX and Vulkan renderers in the game's settings to see if one resolves the audio issue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Driver Rollback Caution">
If a recent driver update caused the "no sound" issue, you might consider rolling back your audio drivers in Device Manager. However, only do this if you're certain the update is the culprit, and always back up your system before making significant driver changes.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={LifeBuoy} title="Common Audio Problem Scenarios & Solutions">
Different scenarios can lead to audio problems. Understanding these specific contexts can help in pinpointing the exact cause and applying the right fix for your "Road to Vostok no sound" experience.

| Problem Scenario       | Potential Cause                       | Recommended Solution                                  |
| :--------------------- | :------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------- |
| **No sound at launch** | Incorrect default audio device        | Set your preferred output device as default in Windows Sound Settings. |
| **Sound cuts out**     | Overlapping audio applications        | Close all other applications that use audio (browsers, music players). |
| **Static/Distortion**  | Incorrect sample rate/bit depth       | Adjust your audio device's format to 48000 Hz, 16-bit in Sound Control Panel. |
| **Only music plays**   | SFX/Voice volume muted in-game        | Check individual volume sliders in Road to Vostok's audio options. |
| **No sound after alt-tab** | Game audio process suspended       | Try restarting the game. If persistent, update display drivers. |

<Callout type="success">
Many players have found success by simply restarting their PC after applying driver updates or verifying game files. A fresh boot can often resolve lingering software conflicts.
</Callout>
