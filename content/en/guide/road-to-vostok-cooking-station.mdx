<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Setting Up and Using the Cooking Station">
Once you've successfully gathered all the components, crafting the **Road to Vostok cooking station** is the next logical step. The process is intuitive, and using it is even simpler. The primary challenge often lies in acquiring the raw materials, especially water, which is a common ingredient for many recipes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Craft the Cooking Station">
    Navigate to your crafting menu. Select the cooking station recipe. Ensure you have all required materials, including metal scraps, wires, a fuel tank, and the inverter. Initiate the crafting process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Place the Station">
    Once crafted, the cooking station appears in your inventory. Simply select it and place it down in your desired location. It doesn't require any specific power source or complex setup; just place it in your vicinity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gather Ingredients">
    Before you can cook, you need ingredients. For recipes like pea soup, you'll need peas and, crucially, water. Water is often the most challenging resource to find, so prioritize scavenging for it in wells, water pumps, or abandoned buildings.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Start Cooking">
    Interact with the placed cooking station. A menu will appear showing available recipes based on the ingredients you possess. Select the recipe you wish to cook (e.g., pea soup) and confirm. The station will begin processing your ingredients.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Collect Cooked Food">
    Once the cooking process is complete, the cooked item will appear in the station's output slot or directly in your inventory. Collect it to enjoy its benefits. The station can typically be used repeatedly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
Always keep an eye out for water sources. Having a consistent supply of water is key to utilizing your **Road to Vostok cooking station** effectively for a wide range of recipes. Consider carrying empty bottles to fill when you find a source.
</Callout>

</Section>
