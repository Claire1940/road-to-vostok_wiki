In the unforgiving environment of *Road to Vostok*, managing your inventory and shelter space is critical for survival. ## Understanding the Attic Unlock Process

The attic serves as a superior shelter compared to the starting cabin, offering significantly more floor space for loot crates, weapon lockers, and storage solutions. Because the game’s inventory management can quickly become overwhelming, securing this space is a priority for mid-game progression.

To unlock the attic, you must navigate a sequence of quests provided by the local traders. The core of this progression relies on obtaining a specialized item—often referred to as a water lock—which is required to manufacture the final component needed to trade for the attic key.

### Essential Quest Requirements

| Quest Item | Source | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Saline (x3)** | Medical Cabinets/Bandits | Trade for Water Lock |
| **Board Game** | World Loot | Trade for Locker/Trolley |
| **Books** | World Loot | Trade for Storage Cabinet |
| **Sugar & Yeast** | General Loot | Crafting Moonshine/Key Item |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always check medical cabinets and defeated bandits in the village map. These locations are the most reliable sources for the saline bottles required to acquire the water lock.

## Crafting and Trading for the Attic Key

Once you have secured your saline bottles, you must visit the medic trader to exchange them for the water lock. This item is not a standalone reward but a key ingredient in a complex crafting recipe.

After obtaining the water lock, you will need to combine it with other resources to create the specific item requested by the general goods trader. This process effectively turns basic scavenging into a productive crafting loop.

### Crafting Recipe for the Attic Key Quest

1. **Gather Materials**: Collect your water lock, three bottles of water, sugar, and yeast.
2. **Access Crafting Menu**: Use the crafting station to initiate the production of the required quest item.
3. **Trade Completion**: Take the finished item to the general goods trader to complete the "bulkhead" or attic-related questline.
4. **Unlock**: Once the delivery is accepted, the attic key will be provided, granting you permanent access to the upper level of the building.

## Optimizing Your New Shelter Space

Upon unlocking the attic, you will notice it is essentially a blank canvas. Unlike the starting cabin, the attic does not come pre-furnished with storage. You will need to bring your own loot crates and lockers. 

Given the vast increase in space, it is recommended to prioritize heavy-duty storage items like the giant locker. These containers offer superior inventory capacity, making them ideal for storing bulky weapons and gear that previously cluttered your cabin floor.

### Storage Capacity Comparison

| Storage Unit | Space Efficiency | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Small Cabin Crates** | Low | Early game consumables |
| **Standard Cabinets** | Medium | Meds and ammo |
| **Giant Lockers** | High | Weapons and large gear |

## Strategic Survival Tips

> **⚠️ Warning:** The area surrounding the trader and the attic building can be dangerous. Be prepared for hostile encounters with NPCs who may camp the vicinity. Always carry splints and enough medical supplies, as a broken leg can easily end a looting run prematurely.

*   **Loot Management**: If your pockets are full, prioritize high-value items like keys and rare crafting components over low-durability weapons.
*   **Backtracking**: Don't be afraid to return to previously visited maps. Medical cabinets often respawn their contents over time, providing a consistent way to farm saline for future trades.
*   **Inventory Space**: If you are struggling with encumbrance, use the trolley obtained from the board game quest to transport heavy loads back to your shelter.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the saline needed for the water lock road to vostok quest?**

A: Saline is most commonly found in medical cabinets located in the village buildings. Defeating hostile NPCs in these areas also provides a secondary chance to loot the item.

**Q: Do I lose my cabin when I unlock the attic?**

A: No, you retain access to both the cabin and the attic. The attic is simply an additional, larger storage space that you can utilize as your base expands.

**Q: What do I do if I have too much loot and no space?**

A: Focus on completing the board game quest as early as possible to obtain the locker and trolley. These tools are essential for managing the overflow of items you will collect as you progress.

**Q: Is the water lock a permanent item?**

A: No, the water lock is a consumable crafting component used to create the final quest item. Once you turn it in to the trader, it is removed from your inventory.
