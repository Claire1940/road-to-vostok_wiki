## The Evolution of Shelter Customization

In the early development phases and initial demos, players were restricted to a static environment. While small lootable items could be placed on surfaces, larger objects like sofas, beds, and heavy shelving units were bolted to the floor. The 2026 version of the game has completely overhauled this. Now, almost every piece of **road to vostok furniture** is a dynamic object that can be moved, rotated, stored, or even crafted from scratch.

This shift serves two purposes: it allows for personalized base building and solves the long-standing community feedback regarding storage limitations. By placing more containers and shelves, you can effectively manage the hundreds of items you scavenge during your playthrough.

## Mastering Decor Mode

To begin customizing your living space, you must enter **Decor Mode**. By default, this is mapped to the **F1** key while inside any owned shelter. Once activated, the interface shifts, and all interactable furniture pieces are highlighted with visual indicators.

### Furniture Manipulation Mechanics

When you select a piece of furniture in Decor Mode, you have full control over its placement. The system uses a sophisticated "valid/invalid" shader effect to let you know if an object can be placed in a specific spot.

| Feature | Function | Tactical Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grid Snapping** | Aligns objects to a hidden grid on floors and walls. | Prevents "ugly gaps" and ensures clean, organized storage layouts. |
| **Rotation Control** | Allows 360-degree rotation of any object. | Fits large items like sofas or beds into tight corners or alcoves. |
| **Distance Adjustment** | Moves the object closer or further from the player character. | Enables precise placement without having to move your character constantly. |
| **Surface Detection** | Automatically identifies if an item is a wall-mount or floor-mount. | Ensures shelves and paintings stay on vertical surfaces correctly. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the grid snapping feature when placing multiple storage crates side-by-side. This maximizes floor space and makes it easier to navigate your shelter during night cycles.

## The Furniture Catalog and Item Grouping

One of the most innovative features of the **road to vostok furniture** system is the **Furniture Catalog**. When redecorating, your shelter can quickly become cluttered. The catalog acts as a temporary "pocket dimension" or storage interface for your furniture.

Instead of dragging a heavy sofa across the room, you can "pick up" furniture directly into the catalog. This opens a Tetris-style inventory screen where you can see all your owned furniture pieces. This makes moving between different shelters—such as transitioning from the starting Cabin to a more advanced Bunker—incredibly efficient.

### Item Grouping Logic
The "Item Grouping" feature is a massive quality-of-life improvement for 2026. If you have a shelf filled with medical supplies, ammo cans, and food, you no longer have to clear the shelf before moving it.
1. Place items on a table, shelf, or inside a drawer.
2. Enter Decor Mode (F1).
3. Grab the furniture piece.
4. All items located on or inside that piece of furniture will **automatically group** and move with it.

## How to Acquire New Furniture

You aren't limited to the default items found in your starting shelter. There are three primary ways to expand your collection of **road to vostok furniture** in 2026: crafting, trading, and world discovery.

### 1. Furniture Crafting
Crafting is the most reliable way to get high-quality interior items. Note that furniture crafting has a **Shelter Proximity Requirement**. You cannot build a bed in the middle of a forest; you must be within the bounds of your shelter to access the furniture section of the crafting menu.

| Furniture Item | Required Materials | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Military Bed** | **2x Pillows, 2x Sleeping Pads, 1x Blanket, 1x Sleeping Bag** | High-tier recovery item with tactical aesthetic. |
| **Single Bed** | **1x Lumber, 1x Blanket, 1x Mattress Roll, 1x Pillow** | Basic sleeping arrangement for early-game shelters. |
| **Wall Shelf** | **2x Lumber, 1x Nails (Scrap)** | Essential for vertical storage and item organization. |
| **Simple Stool** | **1x Lumber, 1x Glue/Fasteners** | Basic seating; takes up very little floor space. |

### 2. Trading with NPCs
Traders in the zone now carry furniture supplies. While the Generalist trader might have basic items like fridges or nightstands, specific NPCs have specialized inventories.
*   **The Generalist:** Sells a rotating stock of common household items and containers.
*   **Grandma (Specialized Trader):** Focuses on fabric-related furniture, such as carpets, curtains, and cushions.

### 3. World Discovery
As you explore maps like the Village or the Border crossing, you may find unique props. While not all world objects are lootable, the developer has implemented a "Furniture Component" tool that allows many static props to be converted into dynamic furniture in future updates and mods.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Furniture items are heavy. If you are planning to buy a fridge from a trader, ensure you have cleared enough inventory space or have a vehicle/mule setup to transport the item back to your shelter.

## Lighting and Atmosphere

Customization isn't just about where you put your bed; it's about the atmosphere. Shelters in Road to Vostok now feature functional **light switches**. Most rooms have multiple switches that control different light sources, allowing you to create a "moody" low-light environment for better immersion or a bright, high-visibility workspace for sorting loot.

The lighting system interacts dynamically with your furniture. Placing a tall wardrobe in front of a light source will cast realistic shadows, which can be used to hide certain containers from view if you're playing on a high-stakes difficulty where every advantage matters.

## Multi-Shelter Strategy

With the introduction of multi-shelter support in 2026, players can maintain several bases across the game world. Each shelter can be customized independently.

*   **Main Hub (e.g., The Village Bunker):** This should be your most furnished area, containing high-tier beds, extensive shelving for ammo and weapons, and decorative items to boost "shelter comfort."
*   **Bug-out Shelters (e.g., The Tent):** These should be minimalist. Focus on essential **road to vostok furniture** like a single bed, a cooking station, and one or two high-capacity crates for emergency supplies.

By utilizing the Furniture Catalog, you can easily transfer "surplus" furniture from your main hub to your smaller outposts, ensuring that no matter where you are in the zone, you have a functional place to rest.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I lose my furniture if my shelter is raided?**

A: Currently, the shelter acts as a safe zone for your items. However, some hardcore difficulty modes may introduce mechanics where certain "surprise features" require you to defend your perimeter to keep your belongings safe.

**Q: Is there a limit to how much road to vostok furniture I can place?**

A: While there is no hard "item cap," your shelter's physical dimensions are the primary limit. Using the Furniture Catalog helps manage crowded spaces by allowing you to swap items in and out of active use.

**Q: How do I get more crafting recipes for furniture?**

A: Recipes are often unlocked by finding technical manuals in the world or by increasing your reputation with specific traders like the Generalist or Grandma.

**Q: Can I move furniture while items are still on top of it?**

A: Yes! Thanks to the Item Grouping system introduced in 2026, any items placed on a surface will stay attached to that surface when you move or rotate the furniture in Decor Mode.
