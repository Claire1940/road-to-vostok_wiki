Airdrops are typically signaled by a distinct engine drone that can be heard across the entire map. Once the cargo is released, a colored smoke flare (usually red or orange) marks the landing site. This smoke remains active for approximately five to ten minutes, drawing in both players and hostile AI factions.

| Signal Type | Detection Range | Duration | Tactical Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine Sound** | Map-wide | 30-60 Seconds | Initial warning to check the sky |
| **Visual Flare** | ~500 Meters | 5-10 Minutes | Pinpoints the exact loot location |
| **Physical Crate** | Proximity | Until Looted | Provides physical cover during combat |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Guide: Securing the Loot">

Approaching **Road to Vostok airdrop locations** requires a disciplined tactical routine. Rushing directly to the smoke flare is the most common mistake made by new players. Instead, follow this professional extraction-style procedure to ensure you survive the encounter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Triangulate the Descent">
    As soon as you hear the plane, stop moving and look up. Observe the parachute's drift. Use your compass to mark the bearing of the landing site.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish an Overwatch Position">
    Do not run to the crate. Find a ridge, a building, or a dense treeline within 100 meters of the drop. Wait for 2-3 minutes to see if AI or other players are drawn to the flare.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The 'Safe' Approach">
    Approach the crate from the side with the most cover. If you are in a group (though the game is currently single-player focused), you would have one person looing while the other covers. Solo players must loot in "bursts"—check one slot, look around, check another.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Immediate Relocation">
    Once the high-value items are in your backpack, leave the area immediately. Do not stay to organize your inventory. Move at least 200 meters away into a safe "grey zone" before managing your gear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="AI Behavior">
Hostile AI in Build 1 is programmed to investigate airdrops. Even if you arrive first, expect a patrol to arrive within 60-90 seconds of the crate hitting the ground.
</Callout>

</Section>
