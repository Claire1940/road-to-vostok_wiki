<Section icon={Shield} title="Evasion and Avoidance Strategies">
The most effective way to deal with a BTR patrol is to avoid being detected in the first place. This requires keen observation, sound awareness, and strategic movement. Knowing their patrol patterns and using the environment to your advantage are key.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Listen for Sound Cues">
    BTRs are loud. Pay attention to the distinct sound of their engines and tracks. If you hear one approaching, immediately seek cover and observe its direction of travel. Use headphones for better directional audio.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Terrain and Cover">
    Dense forests, ruins, and elevated positions can provide excellent concealment. Stay out of open fields and roads where BTRs have clear lines of sight and fire. Use buildings, rocks, and thick vegetation to break visual contact.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Observe Patrol Routes">
    Over time, you may learn common patrol routes for BTRs in specific maps. Use this knowledge to plan your movements, especially when approaching critical objectives or extraction points. Some routes might involve mines or physical obstacles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Move Slowly and Stealthily">
    Avoid sprinting or making unnecessary noise when a BTR is nearby. Crouch or prone to reduce your visibility and sound footprint. Wait for the patrol to pass before resuming your movement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
When crossing the Border Zone, be extra vigilant. Officially, different crossing points can involve mines, physical obstacles, or waterways, and the area is guarded by armed forces with air support, increasing the complexity of BTR evasion.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Engaging BTR Patrols: When to Fight">
While avoidance is paramount, there might be situations where engagement is unavoidable or strategically necessary. This requires specialized gear and a tactical approach.

<Callout type="warning" title="High-Risk Engagement">
Only attempt to engage a **Road to Vostok BTR patrol** if you possess anti-armor capabilities and are prepared for a prolonged and dangerous fight. Without proper equipment, you are likely to be eliminated.
</Callout>

| Weapon Type        | Effectiveness Against BTR | Notes                                                                   |
| :----------------- | :------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Anti-Tank Launcher | **High**                  | Direct hits are crucial. Limited ammo, high weight.                     |
| Heavy Machine Guns | **Medium**                | Can suppress infantry, limited damage to BTR armor. Requires sustained fire. |
| Explosives         | **Medium**                | Grenades can damage tracks or expose crew. Requires precise placement.  |
| Precision Rifles   | **Low**                   | Only effective against exposed crew or unarmored infantry.              |
| Standard Rifles    | **Very Low**              | Almost no effect on BTR armor. Only useful for infantry.                |

**Tactical Considerations:**

- **Target Infantry First**: Eliminate the accompanying infantry to reduce incoming fire and allow for a clearer shot at the BTR.
- **Flank Maneuvers**: BTRs have blind spots. Try to get to their sides or rear to target weaker armor points or exposed components.
- **Use Environmental Cover**: Never engage in the open. Use buildings, large rocks, or other durable cover to protect yourself from heavy machine gun fire.
- **Prioritize Mobility**: Be ready to reposition quickly. BTRs can adjust their firing angles, and staying in one spot makes you an easy target.
