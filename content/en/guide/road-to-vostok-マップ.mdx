## Navigating the School Map Layout

The School area in Road to Vostok is a dense environment that replaces the older minefield transit zones found in earlier versions of the game. It features a mix of open fields, a central school building, and residential structures that offer various tactical opportunities. When entering the **road to vostok マップ**, your primary focus should be on the central school building, but approaching it directly is often a death sentence.

Most players will spawn on the outskirts. From there, the best approach is typically from the left side of the map. This route provides significantly more cover compared to the open fields on the right. By staying near environmental objects like trees, rocks, and derelict vehicles, you reduce your profile and make it harder for snipers in the "big house" to spot you.

| Location Type | Description | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **School Building** | The central structure with multiple floors and locked rooms. | High (Best loot, but highest risk) |
| **The Big House** | A residential building overlooking the school grounds. | Medium (Good for sniping/overwatch) |
| **Outpost Transit** | The extraction or movement point to the next zone. | Essential (Survival exit) |
| **Small Camp** | A minor campsite near the extraction point. | Low (Quick rest/minor loot) |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your fire rate before entering the school building. Inspecting your weapon allows you to see if you are in single-fire or full-auto. For the tight corridors of the school, full-auto is recommended, but single-fire is better for the approach.

## Key Loot and Locked Rooms

The school building isn't just a maze of classrooms; it is a repository for some of the best gear in the current **road to vostok マップ**. However, the most valuable items are locked behind specific doors that require keys. Currently, survivors should look for two primary keys: the **Classroom Key** and the **Gymnasium Key**.

Looting the school is a systematic process. The ground floor often contains basic supplies, but the locked rooms are where you will find military-grade crates and medical supplies.

| Key Name | Location of Use | Potential Loot |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Classroom Key** | Main hallway, first floor. | Medical supplies, food, and electronics. |
| **Gymnasium Key** | Rear entrance of the school. | Military crates, weapon parts, and ammo. |
| **Military Crates** | Scattered in locked zones. | High-tier weaponry and armor. |

Finding these keys is not guaranteed in every raid, as spawn rates can vary. If you manage to find them, prioritize securing the gymnasium first, as the military crates there offer the best defensive upgrades.

## Enemy Spawn Points and Combat Tactics

The AI in Road to Vostok is unforgiving. In the School section of the **road to vostok マップ**, enemies typically spawn in predictable clusters, but their patrol patterns can catch an unprepared player off guard. Usually, you can expect at least two enemies to spawn near the school entrance, with others patrolling the "big house" or the transit zones.

When engaging enemies, use the following tactical steps:

1.  **Identify the Sniper:** Check the upper windows of the big house before approaching the school.
2.  **Use the Left Flank:** Move through the foliage on the left side of the map to avoid the main road.
3.  **Clear the Perimeter:** Eliminate the two guards outside the school before attempting to enter.
4.  **Listen for Footsteps:** AI in the 2026 update is more vocal and makes distinct noise when moving over rubble.

> **Warning:** Do not stay in the open for more than a few seconds. The AI has high accuracy at medium range and will quickly pin you down if you are caught without cover.

## Survival Inventory Management

A successful run on the **road to vostok マップ** depends heavily on what you bring in and what you choose to carry out. Backpacks in Road to Vostok serve a specific purpose: they increase your weight capacity rather than just giving you more slots. This means you can carry heavier items, but you must still be mindful of your stamina.

| Item Type | Recommendation | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Food/Water** | Bring at least 2 portions. | Raids can take longer than expected if you get pinned. |
| **Blanket** | Essential for long raids. | Allows you to sleep and recover stamina in safe spots. |
| **Medkits** | At least 3 bandages/meds. | AI damage is punishing; bleeding must be stopped fast. |
| **Ammo** | 3-4 Spare Magazines. | The school can have up to 5-6 enemies patrolling. |

Remember that the map ends near the Outpost transit. If you are heavily encumbered, head directly for the extraction point near the small camp rather than trying to clear every room in the school.

## Advanced Sniping Positions

For players who prefer a long-range approach, the School map offers several vantage points. If you have a scoped rifle, you can effectively clear the school grounds from a distance before ever stepping foot inside.

The best sniping spot is located on the far left side of the map, near the boundary. From this elevated position, you have a clear line of sight into the big house and the school's front entrance. This allows you to pick off guards and enter a "free loot zone" once the threats are neutralized.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Classroom Key in the Road to Vostok マップ?**

A: The Classroom Key is a random spawn that can often be found in jackets or small containers within the residential houses surrounding the school. It is not a 100% guaranteed spawn in every raid.

**Q: How do I extraction from the School area?**

A: The extraction, or transit point, is located past the "big house" near a small campsite. It is labeled as the "Outpost" transit on your navigation UI.

**Q: Does the backpack give me more inventory slots?**

A: No, in the current version of the game, backpacks primarily increase the weight limit you can carry. You must still manage the physical slots available in your vest and rig.

**Q: How many enemies spawn at the School?**

A: Typically, you will encounter 3 to 5 enemies. Two usually spawn near the front of the school, while others may be positioned further back near the transit zone or inside the larger buildings.
