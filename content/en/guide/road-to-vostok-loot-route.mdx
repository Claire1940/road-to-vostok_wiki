## Pre-Run Preparation and Mechanics

Before stepping out of your cabin, you must ensure your gear and settings are optimized for a stealthy approach. Unlike traditional arcade shooters, this title emphasizes sound and positioning.

### Essential Keybinds for Looting
Effective movement is critical when navigating tight interiors. Many veteran players recommend remapping the "Lean" keys (default Q and E) to side mouse buttons. This allows you to maintain full movement control with your left hand while peeking around corners to check for bandits.

### Gear Management
For a standard Village run, you do not need to over-encumber yourself. A primary rifle (like the KA-74) and a secondary submachine gun (like the PM5) are sufficient. 
*   **Ammunition:** Carry at least two spare magazines per weapon.
*   **Medical:** Always keep an SOS kit or a high-quality medkit in your hotbar. Headshots are often fatal, but having a medkit can prevent "perma-death" scenarios if you survive the initial hit.
*   **Hydration/Energy:** While the Village is close to your home base, keeping your hydration above 80% ensures your stamina regenerates quickly during sprints.

## The Definitive Village Loot Route

The most efficient **road to vostok loot route** follows a counter-clockwise path starting from the right side of the map. This strategy allows you to clear high-visibility areas early and use the environment to shield your movements as the sun rises.

### Phase 1: The Tents and Docks
Start by heading right from your cabin toward the temporary tents. While loot here is often sparse, check the ground for loose items that don't always highlight clearly. 
1.  **Garbage Bags:** Move toward the large black garbage bags near the fence. These can contain anything from rags to rare keys.
2.  **The Dock:** Check the crates on the wooden pier. 
3.  **The Trader's Van:** Peek inside the white van near the generalist trader. This is a rare spawn point for crackers and energy drinks.

### Phase 2: The Barn and Hidden Crate
The large barn is a hotspot for both loot and danger. 
*   **Stealth Warning:** AI bandits often patrol the perimeter of the barn. Crouch-walk as you approach to avoid detection.
*   **The Hidden Crate:** Inside the barn, look for a pile of crates in the corner. There is a "secret" wooden bin hidden behind the static geometry that frequently spawns magazines and weapon attachments.

> **💡 Tip:** AI bandits react to sound. If you fire an unsuppressed weapon, stay mobile. Bandits will investigate the last location they heard a shot.

### Phase 3: Residential Sweeps
The houses in the Village are where you will find the most valuable "residential" loot, such as saline and sugar.
*   **Kitchens:** Always check the cupboards and stoves. Sugar and yeast are common here.
*   **Bathrooms:** Bandits have a tendency to hide in bathrooms. Always "slice the pie" when entering and check behind the doors.
*   **Saunas:** Medical supplies like antiseptics and bandages often spawn on the wooden benches of the saunas.

## Survival Tactics and AI Behavior

Executing a **road to vostok loot route** isn't just about clicking on boxes; it's about navigating the 10 bandits that spawn in the zone.

### Understanding the "Flinch"
In Road to Vostok, the aim punch (flinch) is significant. If a bandit hits you first, your reticle will kick violently, making it nearly impossible to return fire accurately. Always use cover—leaning from behind a tree or a doorframe is 100% safer than standing in the open "no man's land" between houses.

### The "Tape" Strategy
If you feel overwhelmed, move toward the map boundaries marked by yellow tape. This is the out-of-bounds area. By keeping the tape to your back or side, you effectively cut off 180 degrees of potential flanking maneuvers from the AI.

### Crafting the Kilju
One of the primary goals of your **road to vostok loot route** should be gathering materials for Kilju. This special item is used to trade for the Attic Key with the Generalist Trader.
*   **Ingredients:** 3x Saline, 3x Water, Sugar, and Yeast.
*   **Benefit:** Unlocking the Attic provides a safe, permanent storage room that persists across your runs, significantly increasing your long-term survival odds.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The BTR armored vehicle can spawn in the Outpost after day 18. If your route takes you near the transition zones, listen for the heavy engine sound and seek immediate cover indoors.

## Managing Inventory and Value
As your backpack fills up, you must prioritize value-per-slot. A common mistake is carrying too many low-value food cans.

1.  **Consumables:** If you find peaches or meatball cans, eat them immediately to top off your stats rather than wasting inventory space.
2.  **Durability:** Weapons found on bandits often have less than 30% durability. Unless it is a legendary weapon (like the M78), it is usually better to unload the ammo and drop the gun.
3.  **Stacking:** Keep an eye on your organization. Use the "Low Ready" stance (holding the weapon down) to move faster between looting points.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to find Saline for the Kilju quest?**

A: Saline is most commonly found in the medical cabinets of the larger residential houses and occasionally in the saunas. It is considered a rare spawn, so you may need to run your **road to vostok loot route** multiple times to find all three.

**Q: How many bandits spawn in the Village?**

A: Currently, there are typically 10 bandits spawned per map instance. They do not respawn during a single session, so once you have cleared a house or sector, it remains safe until you leave the map or sleep.

**Q: Can I sleep in the Village to skip the night?**

A: Yes, you can find pillows or mattresses inside the houses. Using these allows you to sleep and skip the darkness, which is helpful if you do not have a flashlight or night vision.

**Q: What should I do if I start bleeding after a gunshot?**

A: Immediately find cover and use a bandage. Bleeding in Road to Vostok will quickly drain your health to zero. If you have multiple injuries, a medkit or SOS kit is required to fully restore your HP after the bleeding has stopped.**
