## Understanding Movement and Stamina

The movement system in Road to Vostok is governed by two distinct stamina pools: Body Stamina and Arm Stamina. Understanding how these interact is the first step in learning **road to vostok how to escape patrols**. If you exhaust your body stamina, your character becomes winded, causing your arm stamina to plummet and making it nearly impossible to return fire accurately if a patrol spots you.

There are two primary states of movement:
1.  **The Jog:** A sustainable pace that drains body stamina slowly. It only affects arm stamina if your body stamina hits zero.
2.  **Full Tilt Run:** A maximum-speed sprint that drains both body and arm stamina simultaneously.

| Movement Type | Body Stamina Drain | Arm Stamina Drain | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Walking** | None | None | Stealth and recovery |
| **Jogging** | Low | None (unless exhausted) | Long-distance travel |
| **Full Tilt** | High | Low | Escaping immediate danger |
| **Crouching** | None | None | Low profile/Stealth |

> **💡 Tip:** If you need to cover a large distance quickly, put your weapon away. Running with a weapon equipped increases the drain on your arm stamina, which will penalize your accuracy if you are suddenly forced into a firefight.

## Road to Vostok How to Escape Patrols: Core Mechanics

When a patrol is nearby, your primary goal is to break line-of-sight and create distance. The AI in the 2026 builds follows specific logic patterns that can be predicted and avoided. If you are caught in the open, your best bet is to use "Inertia Transferring" to reach cover. By initiating a Full Tilt run and then hitting the crouch key, your character will slide or move quickly into a low profile, allowing you to duck behind low walls or foliage efficiently.

### Using Verticality and Cover
The AI currently struggles with significant height differences. If you can reach a high vantage point, such as the roof of a village house or the rocky outcroppings behind the power array, you gain a massive tactical advantage. Not only does this allow you to scout patrol paths from a distance, but it also forces the AI to navigate complex geometry to reach you, giving you ample time to escape.

### The "Crouch Invincibility" Exploit
A notable quirk in the current AI behavior involves their vertical aiming limits. If you are on relatively similar ground height as an enemy patrol and you enter a crouch, the AI often struggles to adjust its aim downward quickly. While this is not a guaranteed "invincibility" shield, it frequently causes the AI to miss shots, providing a vital window to either return fire or retreat into denser cover.

## Stealth Tactics and AI Avoidance

To successfully implement a strategy for **road to vostok how to escape patrols**, you must treat every sound as a potential death sentence. Sound travels significantly in the Finnish wilderness, and sprinting will alert nearby enemies long before they see you. 

### Breaking Line of Sight
Once a patrol has spotted you, they will converge on your last known position. To lose them, you must put a solid object (building, rock, or dense hill) between you and the enemy. Once the line of sight is broken, immediately change your direction of travel. If you continue running in a straight line, the AI's predictive pathing will likely intercept you.

### Tactical Stealth Comparison

| Action | Noise Level | Visibility | Best For... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prone Crawl** | Silent | Minimum | Crossing open fields |
| **Crouch Walk** | Very Low | Low | Moving through villages |
| **Jogging** | Medium | Medium | General navigation |
| **Sprinting** | High | High | Breaking contact |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be aware of your "Step Height." While sprinting, your character can clear larger gaps and step over higher obstacles. If you lose momentum, you might find yourself stuck on a small ledge while trying to flee, making you an easy target.

## Combat as a Last Resort

Sometimes, escaping a patrol isn't possible without firing a shot. In these instances, understanding the hip-fire and arm stamina mechanics is vital. In Road to Vostok, hip-firing is accessed by scrolling up on the mouse wheel. This raises the weapon to a ready position but begins draining arm stamina immediately.

*   **Arm Stamina & Sway:** As your arm stamina depletes, your weapon will begin to sway in a "figure eight" pattern. 
*   **Full Auto Penalty:** Firing in full auto drains arm stamina significantly faster than semi-auto. A full magazine dump from an HK416 can drain nearly 30% of your arm stamina.
*   **Weapon Swapping:** A useful trick to regain a moment of stability is to swap to your secondary weapon. This resets the arm position and can give you a few seconds of steadier aim to pick off a pursuing enemy.

## Survival Essentials: Healing and Loot

If you take damage while attempting to **road to vostok how to escape patrols**, you need to heal immediately. The game uses a 100-point health system, and most hits take away 25 HP.

**Pea Soup: The Ultimate Survival Tool**
In a nod to Finnish tradition, cans of pea soup are your primary healing item in the demo and early game. Each use restores 10% of your health. While it may seem meager, stacking these cans allows you to sustain yourself through multiple skirmishes.

### Finding Ground Caches
Scattered throughout the map are bluish boxes hidden under shrubbery. These ground caches contain high-tier loot, including the Mosin-Nagant and essential medical supplies. Finding these caches early in a run can provide the firepower needed to discourage patrols from following you.

| Item | Use | Rarity |

## Advanced Escape Techniques

For players looking for every advantage in 2026, there are several "momentum-based" techniques that can be used to outrun AI patrols.

1.  **Burst Sprinting:** By tapping the sprint button in a rhythmic fashion rather than holding it down, you can maintain a high-speed "glide" that uses momentum to preserve arm stamina. This allows you to move at near-maximum speed while keeping your aim steady.
2.  **Crouch Jumping:** Jumping and immediately crouching in mid-air allows you to clear higher obstacles and land in a low profile, which is perfect for hopping through windows to escape a pursuing squad.
3.  **Reload Skipping:** By swapping weapons at the right frame during a reload or bolt-action animation, you can occasionally bypass the delay, allowing for faster follow-up shots against aggressive AI.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I know if a patrol has spotted me?**

A: The AI will verbalize their status and immediately transition from a walking pace to a tactical crouch or sprint toward your position. You will also hear the distinct sound of weapons being readied.

**Q: What is the best weapon for dealing with patrols?**

A: For most players, the Mosin-Nagant is preferred for its ability to neutralize threats from a distance before they can close the gap. However, the RK-62 is more versatile if you are forced into a close-quarters escape.

**Q: Does rain or night affect my ability to escape patrols?**

A: Yes, environmental factors like heavy rain and darkness reduce the AI's detection range, making it significantly easier to practice **road to vostok how to escape patrols** without being detected from long distances.

**Q: Can I bribe or talk my way out of a patrol encounter?**

A: Currently, the AI is hostile on sight. There are no negotiation mechanics in the 2026 builds, so stealth or combat are your only options.
