## The Barter Economy and Survival Loot

Currency does not exist in the post-collapse world of Road to Vostok. To get the best weapons and survival gear, you must master the barter system. Traders do not want cash; they want high-value military components and electronics. 

When searching for supplies, including **water road to vostok** containers or medical kits, keep an eye out for "gold" items. These are pieces of equipment that traders value above all else. Because traders charge double what they offer, you need to arrive with a surplus of valuable goods to secure top-tier rifles like the MK-18 or SVD.

### Items to Stockpile for Trade:
*   **ACOG Sights:** Extremely high trade value and essential for long-range combat.
*   **Suppressors:** Vital for stealth; traders will offer significant gear in exchange.
*   **Electronics:** Found in industrial areas; light but highly valuable.
*   **Elite Medical Supplies:** Items like FX kits and high-end medkits.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never trade away your last source of water or medical supplies, regardless of how good the weapon offer looks. Survival always comes before firepower.

## Combat Tactics: Stealth vs. Aggression

A common mistake for new players is treating Road to Vostok like a standard tactical shooter. In the Vostok zone, uncontrolled aggression is a death sentence. The military units are coordinated; they use radios to call for reinforcements and armored vehicles to flush out attackers.

The golden rule of combat is: **Do not shoot unless you have no alternative.** Total stealth is your primary objective. Every bullet fired is a beacon for every enemy within several hundred meters.

### Weapon Duality Strategy
You should ideally carry two types of weapons to handle different environmental challenges:
1.  **Scoped Rifle (e.g., AKM with ACOG):** For identifying and engaging targets in open fields or long apartment corridors.
2.  **Shotgun:** The undisputed king of Close Quarters Battle (CQB). Use this inside tight buildings where angles are narrow.

## Navigating the Minefield

The minefield serves as a gatekeeper to the deeper zones of the game. Proximity mines are nearly invisible and can end a "perfect" run instantly. Learning to navigate this area is a rite of passage for any survivor.

*   **Visual Patterns:** Look for subtle disturbances in the ground or metallic glints.
*   **Ballistic Detonation:** If you spot a mine, do not walk around it. Shoot it from a safe distance to clear the path.
*   **Terrain Geometry:** Use high ground and rocky outcrops where mines cannot be buried.

> **💡 Tip:** The world resets every time you enter or leave the shelter. Use this to your advantage to farm the same safe areas repeatedly until you have the gear necessary for the minefield.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find more water in Road to Vostok?**

A: Water is most commonly found in residential kitchens, grocery stores, and medical tents. In 2026, players should focus on the starting village's houses to find plastic bottles and canned drinks. Always check the backpacks of fallen scavengers as well.

**Q: Does rain affect my character's health or thirst?**

A: While rain (the environmental **water road to vostok** factor) does not directly hydrate your character, it significantly impacts your survival by lowering the ambient temperature and reducing AI visibility. You still need to carry bottled water even during a storm.

**Q: What happens if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: Death in the Vostok zone results in a total save wipe. You lose all equipment, progress, and stored items. This is why the shelter is so important; you must store your most valuable gear in the shelter chest before attempting to cross the border.

**Q: Can I use a suppressor to kill everyone stealthily?**

A: Suppressors help, but they are not silent. The VSS Vintorez is the most effective suppressed weapon, but even then, the sound of a body hitting the floor or a missed shot hitting a wall can alert nearby patrols. Stealth should rely on movement, not just silenced weapons.
