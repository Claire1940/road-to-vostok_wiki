## Understanding the Save and Persistence Mechanics

In Road to Vostok, saving is not a manual action found in a pause menu. Instead, the game utilizes an "Auto-Save" and "Shelter Persistence" logic. Your progress is primarily tied to your Shelter (the cabin in Area 05). When you interact with your storage or enter your home base, the game tracks the state of your inventory and the world.

The world itself is dynamic. Every time you enter your cabin and return to the map, the loot spawns and AI positions refresh. This is a key tactic for "farming" specific items like the elusive inverter or medical supplies. However, your character's physical state—including injuries, hunger, and thirst—is constantly being updated. If you exit the game while in the middle of a field, you may find yourself in a precarious position upon reloading.

| Feature | Save Behavior |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manual Save** | Not available; uses automated state tracking. |
| **Shelter Storage** | **Permanent** persistence (unless permadeath is triggered). |
| **Inventory Save** | Saved upon map transitions or entering the Shelter. |
| **World State** | Resets/Refreshes when you enter or leave the Shelter. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always return to your Shelter and interact with your storage before closing the game to ensure all recently looted items are registered to your persistent save file.

## The Three Zones: Risk vs. Reward

The save system is inextricably linked to the map's geography. The game world is divided into three distinct difficulty tiers, each with its own rules regarding what happens when you "fail." Understanding these zones is crucial for managing your "Road to Vostok how to save game" strategy.

### Area 05 (The Starter Zone)
This is your safe haven. Area 05 contains your primary shelter. If you die here, you will lose the gear currently on your person, but everything stored in your cabin remains untouched. This allows for a "regain" loop where you can bounce back from a bad raid by dipping into your reserves.

### The Border Zone
The Border Zone acts as a transitionary area filled with higher-tier AI, landmines, and helicopter patrols. Like Area 05, dying here is punishing but not terminal. You will respawn at your shelter with your stored items intact, though your tactical gear will be lost to the zone.

### Vostok (The Permadeath Zone)
This is where the save mechanics change entirely. Vostok is the endgame area featuring the best loot, such as the Mark 18 and high-tier plate carriers. However, if you die within the Vostok maps, the game **deletes your entire save file**. You do not just lose your gear; you lose your quest progress, your shelter upgrades, and your stash.

| Zone Name | Death Penalty | Save File Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Area 05** | Lose equipped gear | **Safe** |
| **Border Zone** | Lose equipped gear | **Safe** |
| **Vostok** | **Total Wipe** | **Deleted** |

## How to Protect Your Save Progress

Since Vostok can delete your hard-earned progress, veteran players use a "staged" approach to saving. Before you ever set foot in the Border Zone or Vostok, you should have a "base" level of gear that you never take with you. This ensures that even if you lose a save, you have a mental blueprint of how to fast-track your next run.

### Questing and Tax Reductions
One of the most important things to "save" is your reputation with the trader. By completing tasks—such as finding toilet paper, lotion, or buckets—you lower the trader's tax rate. Initially, taxes can be as high as 100%, making items prohibitively expensive. As you complete quests, this tax drops, making your "new" save files much easier to manage if you have to start over.

### Farming the Refresh Loop
Because the world refreshes when you enter your cabin, you can "save" time by efficiently farming the nearby houses. 
1. Loot the two houses nearest to the Shelter.
2. Return to the Shelter to deposit items.
3. Exit the Shelter to refresh the loot spawns.
4. Repeat until you have the necessary components for questing.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Landmines in the Border Zone are static but lethal. If you are carrying high-value quest items, do not rush. A single misstep can send you back to the Shelter without your gear.

## Managing Equipment and Inventory

Your inventory management is a secondary form of saving. Since you can lose your body and gear, you should categorize your items into "Essential," "Trade," and "Quest" tiers.

| Item Category | Action | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inverters / Batteries** | Store in Shelter immediately. | **High** |
| **Weapon Magazines** | Keep 2 on person, store spares. | Medium |
| **Food / Soda** | Consume as needed, do not overstock. | Low |
| **Medical Supplies** | Always carry at least 2 bandages/meds. | **High** |

The game features realistic weapon handling, where you must manually rack rounds and check magazines (default 'B' key). If you die because you forgot to chamber a round, the save system will not pity you. Always ensure your weapon is "saved" in a ready state before leaving the Shelter area.

## Advanced Strategies for Vostok Runs

When you finally decide to enter the Vostok zone, you are putting your entire save file at risk. To mitigate this, preparation is the only "save" you have.

1. **Night Raids:** Use NVGs or flashlights. AI detection is slightly lower at night, giving you a better chance to break line of sight.
2. **Line of Sight (LoS):** If an AI spots you, their accuracy increases the longer they look at you. Break LoS immediately to reset their "aim" clock.
3. **The Sleeping Bag:** While it is a single-use item, the sleeping bag allows you to wait out dangerous weather or darkness. However, it takes up significant inventory space.
4. **The "Load Game" Salvage:** In the current demo build, if you die in a non-permadeath zone, you can sometimes "Load" the save to find yourself back at the shelter. However, any gear you had on your body at the time of death will be gone.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Road to Vostok have a permadeath mechanic?**

A: Yes, but only in the "Vostok" zone. If you die in Area 05 or the Border Zone, you only lose your carried equipment. If you die in Vostok, your entire save file is deleted.

**Q: How do I save my game before quitting?**

A: To ensure your progress is recorded, return to your Shelter (the cabin) and interact with your storage containers. The game automatically saves your state during map transitions and Shelter entries.

**Q: Can I get my gear back if I die in the Border Zone?**

A: No. Once you die, your body and the gear it held are effectively removed from that instance. You must rely on the items you previously "saved" in your Shelter storage to re-arm yourself.

**Q: How do I refresh loot in the world?**

A: Loot and AI spawns refresh every time you enter and then exit your Shelter. This is the most effective way to farm for rare items like the inverter or specific weapon magazines in the early game.

**Q: Is there a way to backup my save file to avoid permadeath?**

A: While you could technically find the save folder in your Windows AppData, the game is designed around the "hardcore" philosophy. Backing up saves is considered against the intended experience of the Vostok zone's high-stakes gameplay.
