## Essential Movement and Interaction Controls

Movement in Road to Vostok is governed by a realistic inertia system. Unlike traditional arcade shooters, your character has physical weight that affects how quickly you can stop, turn, or accelerate. There are two primary states of movement: the standard jog and the Full Tilt run. 

The standard jog is your baseline for exploration, allowing you to maintain awareness of your surroundings while slowly consuming body stamina. However, when you need to cross a dangerous clearing, the Full Tilt run provides a significant speed boost at the cost of both body and arm stamina.

| Action | Key / Input | Tactical Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Move Forward/Back/Left/Right** | W, A, S, D | Subject to character inertia and weight. |
| **Jog (Toggle/Hold)** | Left Shift | Drains body stamina slowly. |
| **Full Tilt Run** | Shift (Double Tap/Hold) | Drains both body and arm stamina; increases step height. |
| **Crouch** | C / Left Ctrl | Lowers profile; can be used to transfer inertia into a slide. |
| **Jump** | Space | Crouch mid-air to land in a crouch for immediate cover. |
| **Interact / Pick Up** | F / G | Use G for the advanced item placement system. |
| **Inventory** | Tab | Access gear, health, and survival items. |

> **💡 Tip:** When performing a Full Tilt run, your character's step height increases, allowing you to step over small gaps and up steeper inclines that would normally require a jump.

## Advanced Road to Vostok Controls and Combat Mechanics

The combat in Road to Vostok is built around "procedural weapon handling." This means that your accuracy is not just a matter of where you point the mouse, but also the physical state of your character's arms. The **road to vostok controls** for combat include a unique hip-firing mechanic that differs from most mainstream FPS titles.

To ready your weapon for hip-firing, you must scroll up on the mouse wheel. This raises the weapon to a ready position, which immediately begins to drain your arm stamina. If your arm stamina reaches zero, your weapon sway will become erratic, making it nearly impossible to land shots at medium range.

| Combat Action | Input | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Ready Weapon (Hip Fire)** | Mouse Wheel Up | Prepares weapon for firing; starts arm stamina drain. |
| **Aim Down Sights (ADS)** | Right Mouse Button | Maximum accuracy; high arm stamina drain. |
| **Fire Weapon** | Left Mouse Button | Recoil affects arm stamina; full-auto drains stamina faster. |
| **Switch Fire Mode** | B / V | Toggle between semi-auto and full-auto. |
| **Reload** | R | Standard reload; can be cancelled by weapon swapping. |
| **Check Magazine** | Alt + R | Check remaining rounds visually. |

### Managing Arm vs. Body Stamina

Understanding the duality of the stamina system is vital. Body stamina (the lower bar) dictates your ability to run and jump. Arm stamina (the upper bar) dictates your ability to hold a weapon steady. If you exhaust your body stamina by sprinting, your arm stamina will begin to plummet immediately. 

To maintain peak combat effectiveness, avoid "red-lining" your sprint. If you anticipate a firefight, stop running a few seconds early to let your character catch their breath. This ensures that when you raise your rifle, your aim remains steady.

## Survival and Healing Systems

In the current 2026 build, health management is straightforward but unforgiving. Your character typically has 100 Health Points (HP). Most high-caliber rounds from AI enemies will deal approximately 25 damage, meaning you are only four shots away from death at any given time.

Healing is primarily handled through consumable items found in the world. The most iconic of these is the Finnish Pea Soup. Beyond being a caloric necessity, these cans serve as your primary medical kit in the early stages of the game.

| Item | Health Gain | Weight | Rarity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pea Soup (Can)** | **+10 HP** | 0.5 kg | Common |
| **Bandage** | **+25 HP** | 0.1 kg | Uncommon |
| **Medical Kit** | **+50 HP** | 1.2 kg | Rare |

> **Warning:** Healing takes time. Do not attempt to use items while under direct fire, as the **road to vostok controls** do not currently allow for "one-handed" healing while shooting.

## Advanced Interaction: Item Placement

One of the most impressive features of the **road to vostok controls** is the item placement system. This allows players to organize their hideouts or create tactical barricades by physically manipulating objects in the 3D space.

1.  **Pick Up Object:** Press **G** while looking at a loose item (like a weapon or a can of soup).
2.  **Distance Adjustment:** Use the **Mouse Wheel** to move the item closer to or further away from your character.
3.  **Rotation:** Hold the **Right Mouse Button** while the item is held to rotate it on its axes.
4.  **Place Item:** Press **Left Mouse Button** to release the item in its new orientation.

This system is particularly useful for stacking supplies in your inventory or setting up a "sniper's nest" with weapons laid out horizontally for quick access. ## Pro Tips for Mastery

To truly excel, you should familiarize yourself with some of the more nuanced aspects of the **road to vostok controls** that aren't immediately obvious:

*   **Weapon Swapping for Stamina:** If your arm stamina is depleted, quickly swapping to your secondary and back can sometimes provide a momentary reset of your character's posture, allowing for a more stable follow-up shot.
*   **Momentum Transfer:** If you are at a Full Tilt run and hit the Crouch key, you will perform a brief slide. This is excellent for getting under low obstacles or reaching cover faster than a standard crouch would allow.
*   **The "Snake Man" Technique:** By pulsing the sprint key in a specific rhythm rather than holding it, you can maintain a high speed while preserving a significant portion of your arm stamina. This is technically a exploitation of the current inertia system but is highly effective for crossing large maps.
*   **Short Bursts:** When using full-auto weapons like the 416, firing in 3-5 round bursts minimizes the massive stamina drain associated with continuous fire.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I customize the road to vostok controls?**

A: You can access the keybindings menu through the Settings tab in the main menu or by pressing Escape in-game. Most keys can be remapped to suit your preference, though some procedural handling keys (like the mouse wheel for hip fire) are currently fixed in the demo builds.

**Q: Why does my character's aim sway so much even when I'm not moving?**

A: This is likely due to depleted arm stamina. Check the top bar on your HUD. If it is low, your character is physically exhausted. Stop aiming, lower your weapon (Mouse Wheel Down), and wait a few seconds for the bar to refill.

**Q: Is there a way to lean around corners in Road to Vostok?**

A: Yes, the standard Q and E keys allow your character to lean left and right. This is essential for clearing rooms and engaging AI while minimizing your exposure to return fire.

**Q: What happens to my items if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: Road to Vostok features a permadeath mechanic. While dying in Area 5 only results in the loss of your current inventory, dying on the Vostok side of the border will result in the permanent deletion of your save file, making mastery of the **road to vostok controls** a literal matter of life and death.
