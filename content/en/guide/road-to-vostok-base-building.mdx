## Mastering the Road to Vostok Base Building Mechanics

The core of the customization experience lies in the **Decor Mode**. Unlike the standard gameplay mode where you interact with loot, Decor Mode allows you to pick up, rotate, and snap large furniture pieces into place. By default, pressing **F1** while inside a shelter toggles this interface, revealing indicators on every manipulatable object in the room.

### The Decor Mode Interface

When you enter Decor Mode, the game world becomes a canvas. You can grab stools, tables, beds, and even wall-mounted shelves. The system uses a sophisticated grid-snapping mechanic to ensure that furniture aligns perfectly with walls and floors, preventing the "clutter" feel that plagued earlier versions of the game.

| Feature | Control/Keybind | Function in Decor Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Toggle Decor Mode** | **F1** | Switches between standard interaction and furniture manipulation. |
| **Grab Object** | **Left Mouse** | Picks up a highlighted piece of furniture or prop. |
| **Rotate Object** | **Mouse Wheel** | Adjusts the orientation of the held item before placement. |
| **Grid Snapping** | **Automatic** | Aligns items to surfaces for clean, gap-free placement. |
| **Furniture Catalog** | **Tab** | Opens a Tetris-style storage for all owned furniture. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the grid-snapping feature to place wall shelves at uniform heights. This maximizes vertical storage without wasting floor space in smaller shelters like the Cabin.

## Advanced Furniture Catalog & Storage

One of the most innovative aspects of **road to vostok base building** is the Furniture Catalog. In many survival games, moving a base is a logistical nightmare. In Road to Vostok, players can "store" their furniture into a specialized inventory. This catalog uses a Tetris-style grid, allowing you to pack away sofas, fridges, and beds to be deployed in a different shelter later.

### Item Grouping Logic

A major quality-of-life feature in the 2026 update is **Item Grouping**. If you place loot—such as ammo cans, lanterns, or tools—on top of a table or inside a shelf, those items become "grouped" with the furniture. When you move the table in Decor Mode, all the items on top move with it. This eliminates the need to clear off surfaces every time you want to redecorate your living space.

| Storage Type | Capacity | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Wall Shelves** | Low-Medium | Quick access to medical supplies and grenades. |
| **Nightstands** | Medium | Storing magazines and loose ammunition. |
| **Fridges** | High | Specialized storage for food and perishables. |
| **Crates/Safes** | Very High | Long-term storage for rare weapons and high-value loot. |

## Crafting and Acquiring Furniture

You are not limited to the default furniture found in your starting shelter. To truly master **road to vostok base building**, you must learn to craft and trade for new interior components. Crafting furniture requires a "Shelter Proximity" requirement, meaning you can only build these large items while you are physically inside one of your safe zones.

### Common Crafting Recipes

To craft furniture, you will need to gather raw materials like lumber, blankets, and metal parts. Once you have the necessary components, open the crafting menu while in your shelter to see the available options.

*   **Military Bed:** Requires 2x Sleeping Pads, 2x Pillows, and 1x Sleeping Bag. This provides a high-quality spawn point.
*   **Single Bed:** Requires Lumber, 1x Blanket, and 1x Mattress Roll. A cheaper alternative for secondary "bugout" shelters.
*   **Wall Elements:** Specialized items like paintings or clocks that can only be snapped to vertical surfaces.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some furniture items are heavy and will significantly impact your carry capacity if you try to move them between shelters without using the Catalog system.

## Trading for Base Upgrades

Not all base building components can be crafted. Traders like the **Generalist** and the specialized **"Grandma" trader** offer unique furniture items. The Grandma trader, in particular, is the primary source for fabric-related items like carpets and curtains, which can be used to overlap other furniture and give your shelter a more "lived-in" feel.

1.  **Complete Tasks:** Finishing deliveries for traders lowers their tax rate, making expensive furniture more affordable.
2.  **Check Loot Tables:** While rare, some furniture like fridges or nightstands can occasionally be found in the world as lootable objects.
3.  **Key Acquisition:** Some high-tier shelters are locked behind keys held by specific traders. Building a relationship with them is the only way to expand your base network.

## Multi-Shelter Support and Strategy

In Road to Vostok, you aren't limited to a single base. The 2026 gameplay loop encourages a "hub and spoke" model. You might have a **Main Shelter** (like the Cabin) where you store your primary gear and a series of **Bugout Shelters** (like Tents) positioned near transitions to higher-risk areas.

The **road to vostok base building** system is synchronized across all shelters you own. Any furniture stored in your Catalog can be withdrawn at any location. This allows you to quickly set up a functional outpost in a new zone without having to find new furniture from scratch.

| Shelter Type | Location | Strategic Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **The Cabin** | Starting Zone | Safe, large floor plan, plenty of default storage. |
| **The Tent** | Border Zone | Low profile, easy to hide, perfect for quick resets. |
| **The Attic** | Urban Zone | Vertical advantage, multiple exit points for quick escapes. |

## Customizing the Environment

Beyond just placing boxes, the 2026 update introduced interactive environment controls.
*   **Light Switches:** Most shelters now feature multiple light switches per room. You can toggle these to create "mood lighting" or to keep a low profile during night cycles.
*   **Dynamic Props:** Almost any static prop in the game can be converted into dynamic furniture via modding tools, but in the base game, look for items with the white "Decor" indicator.
*   **Wall Elements:** These are specialized furniture pieces that only snap to walls, allowing you to clear up floor space for better movement.

## FAQ

**Q: Does base building in Road to Vostok include building walls or structures?**

A: Currently, **road to vostok base building** focuses on shelter customization and interior decoration rather than "free-build" structures like those found in Rust. You utilize existing buildings and customize their interiors to suit your tactical needs.

**Q: What happens to my furniture if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: If you are playing on a difficulty with permadeath, dying in the Vostok zone will delete your save file, including all customized shelters and stored furniture. However, in standard zones, your shelters remain intact even if you die during a raid.

**Q: Can I move furniture between different shelters?**

A: Yes. By using the Furniture Catalog (accessible in Decor Mode), you can "pick up" a piece of furniture in one shelter, store it in your catalog, and then "place" it in any other shelter you have unlocked.

**Q: How many items can I store in a single piece of furniture?**

A: There is no strict "slot limit" for furniture like tables; it depends on the physical surface area. For containers like fridges or crates, the storage is limited by the Tetris-grid capacity of that specific item.
