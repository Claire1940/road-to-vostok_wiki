### Understanding Zone Transitions and Safe Houses

When you move between distinct areas on the map, the game engine refreshes both the enemy AI and the available loot. This is a crucial mechanic to exploit for farming resources or escaping dangerous situations. Similarly, utilizing safe houses acts as a soft reset for the zone they are located in.

| Action Triggering Reset | Effect on Loot | Effect on AI Bots | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entering New Area | **Resets** | **Resets** | Applies to all major map zones (e.g., Village to School). |
| Entering/Exiting Safe House | **Resets** | **Resets** | Affects the zone where the safe house is located. |
| Sleeping (Melatonin) | **No Reset** | **No Reset** | Only passes time, useful for day/night cycle changes. |

### Ammo and Weapon Scavenging

Early in your playthrough, ammo can be a scarce resource. A highly effective strategy is to consistently loot enemy weapons, unload their magazines for ammo, and then discard the empty weapon or trade it. This ensures a steady supply of ammunition without relying solely on finding loose rounds.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Engage and Neutralize">
    Successfully eliminate an enemy combatant, ensuring the area is clear before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Loot Weapon">
    Approach the downed enemy and pick up their weapon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Extract Ammunition">
    Open your inventory, right-click the enemy weapon, and select "Unload Magazine" to transfer the ammo to your inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Discard/Trade Weapon">
    Drop the now-empty weapon to save inventory space, or if near a trader, keep it for potential bartering.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success">
This method ensures you maintain a healthy ammo reserve throughout your journey, even in permadeath runs. Ammo is lightweight, so carrying extra is rarely a burden.
</Callout>

### Understanding Containers

Not all containers are created equal, and knowing which ones to prioritize can save you valuable time. For instance, school lockers come in sets, with every three lockers often constituting a single lootable container.

| Container Type | Lootable Units | Common Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Cabinets** | Every two doors is one unit | General loot, crafting items |
| **School Lockers** | Every three lockers is one unit | School supplies, low-tier weapons, ammo |
| **Backpacks (on enemies)** | Single unit | Varied, depends on enemy type |

### Task Management

While tasks are important for unlocking new safe houses and traders, they shouldn't be your sole focus. Many task items are RNG-dependent. It's often more efficient to organically collect task items as you play and turn them in when convenient. Prioritize tasks that unlock keys, such as the school key or bunker key, for crucial forward operating bases.
