## The Role of Shelters in Road to Vostok

Shelters serve as the primary hub for character progression. They are designated safe areas where you can save your simulation time, track your character's health vitals, and most importantly, store loot. In the current 2026 builds, shelters are not automatically available; many must be unlocked using specific keys acquired through trader tasks or found in rare loot caches.

Once inside, the game shifts from a tactical shooter to a management simulation. You can customize these spaces using the item placement system. This physicalized storage means that items left on the floor or on tables remain there across play sessions, provided you are in a protected zone.

| Shelter Feature | Function | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simulation Save** | Locks in your current time and world state. | **High** |
| **Item Persistence** | Dropped items remain exactly where placed. | **Critical** |
| **Customization** | Use Decor Mode to add furniture and racks. | **Medium** |
| **Trader Access** | Some shelters serve as meeting points for NPCs. | **High** |

> **💡 Tip:** Always ensure you have "saved" your simulation at the shelter bed or designated point before exiting the game to ensure your item placements are recorded.

## Managing Item Placement and Decor Mode

The physicalized nature of the inventory means that you aren't limited to boxes. You can manually arrange your gear to create a functional armory or a medical station. To interact with items for placement, you primarily use the `G` and `F1` keys.

### Essential Placement Controls

To master the layout of your shelter, you need to understand the procedural handling of objects. When you pick up an item to place it, you enter a "ghost" placement mode where you can see where the item will land.

| Action | Control (Default) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pick Up/Place** | **G** | Toggles the placement mode for the held item. |
| **Decor Mode** | **F1** | Opens the furniture and large object catalog. |
| **Rotate Item** | **Right Mouse Button** | Spins the item on its axis for horizontal/vertical alignment. |
| **Adjust Distance** | **Mouse Wheel** | Moves the item closer or further away from the character. |

When managing **road to vostok dropped items in shelter**, using the rotation tool is vital for weapons. By holding the Right Mouse Button, you can flip a rifle horizontally to fit perfectly on a shelf or rack. The scroll wheel allows for "neat stacking," which is particularly useful for organizing cans of pea soup or medical bandages in tight spaces.

## Inventory Capacity and Storage Equipment

Your ability to bring items back to the shelter is dictated by your Carry Capacity (CC). Starting with a base CC of 10, you must find or purchase equipment to expand your inventory. Items like windbreakers, tactical vests, and duffel bags significantly increase how much loot you can extract from a raid.

### CC Expansion Items

1.  **Clothing:** Basic jackets and windbreakers provide small boosts to CC.
2.  **Backpacks:** Essential for large-scale looting; ranges from small satchels to military-grade rucksacks.
3.  **Vests:** Provide quick-access slots for magazines and medical items while adding to total capacity.
4.  **Armor Plates:** While they offer protection, they occupy space within compatible carriers.

If you find yourself over-encumbered, you can drop items in the world. However, only **road to vostok dropped items in shelter** are guaranteed to persist safely. Items dropped in the "Village" or "Border" maps may disappear if the map instance resets or if you transition between zones without picking them back up.

## Persistence and the Risk of Death

The stakes in Road to Vostok are divided by the zone you are currently in. This directly affects what happens to your gear and the items you've painstakingly organized in your shelter.

### Death Consequences by Zone

*   **Area 0-5 (Starting/Border Zones):** If you die here, you lose everything currently on your person (your active inventory). However, any **road to vostok dropped items in shelter** remain untouched. Your save file persists, and you can re-arm using your stored supplies.
*   **Vostok (The Permadeath Zone):** This is the ultimate challenge. If you cross the border into Vostok, a red skull icon appears on your HUD. Dying in this zone results in the deletion of your entire save file. This includes your character progress, your keys, and every single item stored in your shelters.

| Zone Type | Death Penalty | Item Safety |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starting Zone** | Lose Inventory | **Safe in Shelter** |
| **Border Zone** | Lose Inventory | **Safe in Shelter** |
| **Vostok** | **Delete Save File** | **Total Loss** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never take your entire stash of rare ammunition or medical supplies into Vostok. Only carry what you are willing to lose, as one stray bullet can reset your entire 2026 progress.

## Advanced Organizing Strategies

To make the most of your limited shelter space, follow these expert organization tips:

### The "Kit" System
Instead of dropping items randomly, create "Ready-to-Go" kits. Place a specific weapon, two compatible magazines, and a medical kit together on a table. This allows you to quickly re-equip after a death in the border zone without searching through piles of loose loot.

### Magazine Management
The game features complex ammo compatibility. An AKM requires 7.62x39mm rounds and a specific AKM magazine. Use the item placement system to keep your 9x19mm magazines separate from your 45 ACP supplies. Dropping a magazine near the corresponding ammo boxes prevents confusion during high-pressure situations.

### Utilizing Vertical Space
Don't just use the floor. Use the `G` key and the scroll wheel to place items on top of crates, shelves, and even window sills. This keeps the floor clear for movement and prevents "clipping" issues where items might become difficult to interact with.

## FAQ

**Q: Do items disappear if I drop them outside of a shelter?**

A: In the current 2026 demo and Early Access versions, items dropped in the open world are not guaranteed to persist. If you leave a map instance or die, those items are typically lost. Only items placed within a designated shelter are saved to your profile.

**Q: How do I rotate items when placing them in my shelter?**

A: After pressing `G` to pick up an item for placement, hold the **Right Mouse Button** and move your mouse to rotate the object. This is essential for aligning weapons on racks or stacking cans neatly.

**Q: Can I lose my road to vostok dropped items in shelter if I die?**

A: It depends on where you die. If you die in the standard zones (Area 0-5), your shelter items are safe. However, if you die in the Vostok perma-death zone, your entire save file is deleted, which includes everything stored in your shelters.

**Q: What is Decor Mode and how do I use it?**

A: Decor Mode is accessed by pressing `F1` while inside a shelter. It allows you to place larger furniture items and structural decorations from a catalog. This system works alongside the standard `G` key placement for smaller loot items.
