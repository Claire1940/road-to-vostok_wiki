Navigating the transition from the beginner-friendly Village to the more hazardous School zone requires careful planning and a solid understanding of the game’s transit mechanics. ## Why You Need to Find the Doctor

While the starting trader in the Village provides the basics, the Doctor is essential for mid-game progression. His inventory focuses on higher-tier barter goods and specialized equipment that you cannot find elsewhere.

The most significant advantage of visiting the Doctor is access to **Large Weapon Lockers**. In the early stages of the game, players often struggle with limited storage space inside their cabins, frequently resorting to leaving weapons on tables or the floor. The lockers sold by the Doctor are incredibly space-efficient; they can be placed flush against your cabin walls and hold a massive number of firearms, effectively solving your organization problems.

Additionally, the Doctor serves as a primary quest-giver. Completing his tasks is the most reliable way to earn rare reputation points and unlock even better gear. Because his inventory refreshes every 10 minutes, checking back frequently is a core strategy for high-level play.

## The Safest Route to the Transit Zone

The journey begins in the Village, which serves as the game's starting area. While there are several ways to reach the edge of the map, the "Waterline Route" is generally considered the safest for beginners because it minimizes the angles from which enemies can ambush you.

1.  **Head to the Coast:** From your starting cabin, move toward the water.
2.  **Follow the Waterline:** Keep the water on your right (or left, depending on your orientation) and follow the shore all the way down.
3.  **Locate the Yellow Tape:** The boundaries of the Village map are marked by bright yellow caution tape. Follow this tape until you reach the corner of the map.
4.  **Find the Break:** Turn left at the corner and scan the perimeter. You are looking for a visible break in the yellow tape marked by wooden stakes driven into the ground.
5.  **Interact with the Transit:** Step into the area between the stakes to trigger the map transition screen.

## Navigating the School Zone

Once you complete the transit, you will find yourself in the School zone. This area is significantly more dangerous than the Village. You will notice immediately that your hunger and thirst have depleted, and the time of day has advanced.

### The Path to the School Building
The school is the largest structure in the area and is located directly ahead of the transit spawn point. Follow the main paved road. You will pass several abandoned tanks and military debris. 

> **💡 Tip:** Stay low and move between the tanks for cover. Bandits often spawn near the grocery store on the right side of the main road.

### Dealing with Bandits
Usually, two to three bandits occupy the grounds around the school. They are often armed with basic rifles or submachine guns. It is recommended to eliminate them from a distance before entering the building. If you are low on ammo, you can attempt to sneak around the back of the building, but the interior hallways are narrow, making encounters difficult to avoid.

## Locating the Doctor’s Office

The school building is a multi-story maze of classrooms and debris. To find the **road to vostok doctor**, you need to head to the upper levels.

1.  **Enter the Main Lobby:** Clear the ground floor of any immediate threats.
2.  **Take the Stairs:** Locate the central staircase and ascend to the **third floor**.
3.  **The First Door:** Once you reach the third-floor landing, look at the first room on your right. 
4.  **Identify the Trader:** The Doctor will be stationed inside this room. Unlike hostile NPCs, he will not fire on sight.

## Maximizing Your Visit

When you finally reach the **road to vostok doctor**, your first priority should be checking his "Special" tab for storage solutions. The lockers are a game-changer for base management. If he doesn't have them in stock, you can wait nearby; as long as you are safe from bandits, his inventory will cycle every 10 minutes.

While you wait, look at his quest board. The Doctor's quests often involve retrieving medical supplies from the surrounding School zone or the nearby grocery store. These quests are excellent for building "Trader Standing," which eventually lowers the prices of his most expensive items.

## Managing the Return Trip

The return trip to the Village is often more dangerous than the trip there because you will likely be carrying heavy lockers or new gear. 

*   **Weight Management:** Ensure you aren't over-encumbered. Being slow in the School zone makes you an easy target for respawned bandits.
*   **Resource Check:** Use any medical supplies you bought from the Doctor to top off your health before the return transit.
*   **Time of Day:** If it is getting dark, consider waiting in the Doctor's room until dawn. Navigating the Village woods at night is significantly harder due to limited visibility.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the road to vostok doctor move to different locations?**

A: In the current 2026 build, the Doctor remains stationary on the third floor of the school building. He does not wander or change maps, though the path to reach him may be blocked by varying bandit spawns.

**Q: What should I do if the Doctor doesn't have the weapon lockers in stock?**

A: The Doctor's inventory is randomized and refreshes every 10 minutes. You can simply wait in his room or scavenge the other classrooms in the school while you wait for the timer to reset.

**Q: Can I sell items to the Doctor for cash?**

A: Road to Vostok utilizes a barter-heavy economy. While some items can be traded for currency, the Doctor primarily deals in item-for-item barters. Ensure you bring valuable loot like electronics or rare medical components to trade for his high-tier gear.

**Q: Is the School zone a permanent death zone?**

A: The difficulty depends on your selected game settings, but generally, the School zone is a "Border" zone. It is more dangerous than the Village but less lethal than the actual Vostok crossing. Always bring a bandage and a splint when visiting the Doctor.
