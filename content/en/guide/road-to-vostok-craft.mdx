## The Fundamentals of Shelter Crafting

Unlike traditional survival games where you can craft anywhere at any time, *Road to Vostok* emphasizes realism and preparation. Crafting is primarily a shelter-based activity. To access the crafting menu for larger items, you must be within the proximity of a designated shelter, such as the initial Cabin or the mobile Tent.

When you enter a shelter and open your interface, the crafting menu expands to include a dedicated "Furniture" section. This section is governed by a proximity check; if you wander too far from your home base, these complex recipes will be grayed out. This design choice ensures that the **road to vostok craft** experience feels grounded in the world's physical space.

### Crafting Interface and Tetris Management

The game utilizes a grid-based "Tetris" inventory system for both character carry capacity and shelter storage. When you craft a piece of furniture, it doesn't simply appear in your hand. Instead, it is sent to a specialized **Furniture Catalog**. This catalog acts as a temporary storage buffer for large objects, preventing your physical inventory from becoming cluttered with sofas and refrigerators.

| Feature | Description | Keybind (Default) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Decor Mode** | Enables the manipulation of furniture and static props. | **F1** |
| **Furniture Catalog** | Stores crafted or looted furniture for later placement. | **Middle Mouse (in Decor Mode)** |
| **Grid Snapping** | Ensures furniture aligns perfectly with floors and walls. | **Automatic** |
| **Item Grouping** | Allows items placed on furniture to move with the object. | **Automatic** |

To begin crafting, you must collect raw materials found throughout the world—ranging from lumber and mattresses to pillows and sleeping bags. Below are some of the most critical recipes to prioritize in your early-game progression.

| Furniture Item | Required Materials | Function |

## Resource Gathering and Trading

Finding the materials for your **road to vostok craft** projects requires a mix of scavenging and smart trading. While you can find basic components like matches, toilet paper, and lotion in residential areas, specialized furniture components are often held by specific NPCs.

### Scavenging Tips
1.  **Industrial Zones:** Search these areas for metal scrap and lumber. These are heavy items, so ensure you have a high Carry Capacity (CC) vest equipped before a run.
2.  **Residential Houses:** These are gold mines for "soft" goods like blankets, pillows, and clothing that can be broken down for fabrics.
3.  **Medical Clinics:** Essential for finding the chemicals and bandages needed for high-end medical furniture.

### The Trading System
Traders play a vital role in the crafting economy. Each trader has a specific logic to their inventory. For instance, the trader known as **Grandma** is the primary source for fabric-related supplies, carpets, and rare upholstery. Completing tasks for these traders not only lowers their "Trader Tax" but can also unlock exclusive keys to new shelters, expanding your crafting reach across multiple maps.

| Trader Type | Primary Goods | Crafting Relevance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Generalist** | Hardware, Tools, Basic Supplies | Provides nails, scrap, and basic furniture. |
| **Grandma** | Fabrics, Carpets, Bedding | Essential for all "Comfort" category crafts. |
| **Military Surplus** | Weapon Parts, Armor Plates | Useful for crafting weapon racks and armor stands. |

## Advanced Customization: Decor Mode

Once you have utilized the **road to vostok craft** menu to create your items, you must enter **Decor Mode (F1)** to place them. This mode provides a 3D preview of the object, showing a valid (green) or invalid (red) shader based on the surface.

One of the most innovative features is **Item Grouping**. If you place a lantern or an axe on a crafted table, those items become "linked" to the table. If you decide to move the table to the other side of the room later, the items will move with it, maintaining your meticulous organization. This system allows for hundreds of individual containers and loot placements within a single shelter, solving the storage issues found in earlier versions of the game.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when moving furniture that has explosive or fragile items grouped with it. While the system is robust, clipping through walls can occasionally cause items to drop.

## Multi-Shelter Strategy

In the 2026 version of *Road to Vostok*, you are no longer limited to a single home base. The game supports **Multi-Shelter functionality**, meaning you can maintain a "Main Shelter" in the starting zone and several "Bugout Shelters" or "Forward Operating Bases" (FOBs) closer to the Vostok border.

The furniture you craft can be transferred between these shelters using the **Furniture Catalog**. If you craft a high-end fridge in your cabin but decide to move your primary operations to a tent closer to the transition zone, you can "store" the fridge in your catalog and "deploy" it at the new location. This mobility is a core pillar of the **road to vostok craft** philosophy, allowing your hard work to follow you as you progress deeper into the world.

## Modding and the Future of Crafting

The developer has introduced the **Furniture Component** tool, which is a game-changer for the modding community. This tool allows any 3D model to be converted into a dynamic, interactable piece of furniture with a single click. This means that as the game grows, the variety of items you can craft and place will likely expand exponentially through community-created mods.

For players, this signals a future where your shelter can be truly unique, filled with custom-crafted items that serve specific tactical or aesthetic purposes. The integration of these tools ensures that the **road to vostok craft** system remains a central, evolving part of the gameplay loop.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I craft weapons in Road to Vostok?**

A: While the current focus is on shelter and furniture crafting, players can modify weapons with attachments (like ACOG sights or suppressors) and manage ammunition. Full weapon "scratch-building" is not currently a primary feature, but maintaining your gear through cleaning kits is essential.

**Q: What happens to my crafted items if I die in the Vostok zone?**

A: *Road to Vostok* features a perma-death mechanic in the high-risk Vostok zone. If you die there, your save file is deleted, which includes all your shelters and crafted items. However, in the standard zones, your progress is saved when you use a bed in your shelter.

**Q: Do I need specific tools to start a road to vostok craft project?**

A: Most furniture recipes require you to be inside a shelter. While some basic items only require raw materials, more advanced structures may eventually require you to have tools like a hammer or screwdriver in your inventory or stored within the shelter's proximity.

**Q: How do I find more crafting recipes?**

A: Recipes are unlocked in several ways: by increasing your reputation with traders, finding physical blueprints in the world, or simply by having the required materials in your inventory which may "trigger" the discovery of a logical recipe.
