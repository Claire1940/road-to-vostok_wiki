## The Core Gameplay Loop: Survival and Persistence

Road to Vostok operates on a loop of exploration, scavenging, and safe return. Your journey begins in your shelter—a permanent cabin that serves as your base of operations. When you leave this shelter, you enter a persistent world where every item you carry is at risk. If you perish in the field, you lose your entire inventory and respawn back at your shelter.

The world resets every time you enter or exit your shelter. This means that enemies and loot will respawn, allowing you to "farm" specific areas for resources. If your backpack is full of valuable gear, the best strategy is to return to your shelter, deposit your findings, and then head back out. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a "starter kit" in your shelter storage. Having a spare weapon and basic meds ensures that a single death doesn't completely end your progression.

## Navigating the Transit System

The "extraction" in Road to Vostok is less about leaving a match and more about moving between zones. The world is divided into various maps, such as the Village, School, and Outpost. To move between them, you must find specific transit points, usually marked by gaps in the yellow perimeter tape that surrounds each map.

### How to Transit Between Zones
When you approach a transit point and press the interaction key (F), you will move to the adjacent zone. However, this is not a free action. Transiting consumes a portion of your hunger and thirst meters and causes time to pass in the game world. Planning your route is essential to ensure you don't arrive in a dangerous new zone while starving or in total darkness.

1.  **Village to School:** From your initial shelter, head right and follow the yellow tape until you reach a hole in the perimeter.
2.  **School to Outpost:** Follow the main highway past the school building. The transit point to the Outpost is located on the far side of the map.
3.  **Outpost to Vostok:** This is the most dangerous path, leading into the high-tier loot areas where enemies are significantly more lethal.

## Health and Injury Management

Survival in Road to Vostok is complicated by a realistic medical system. You don't just have a health bar; you have various status conditions that require specific medical interventions. Ignoring a bleed or a fracture will eventually lead to your character's death.

### Status Effects and Treatments
Understanding which medical item fixes which ailment is the difference between a successful run and a lost kit.

| Status Icon | Condition | Required Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Scratch** | Active Bleed | Bandage or Tourniquet |
| **Broken Bone** | Fracture | Splint |
| **Fire** | Burn | Balm or Lotion |
| **Skull/Target** | Head Trauma | Medkit, IFAK, or AFAK |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Head trauma (signified by the skull or target icon) causes rapid health drain. If you do not have a high-tier medkit, your only hope is to sprint back to your shelter and drop your gear before passing out.

## Trading and the Barter Economy

There is no traditional currency in the current version of Road to Vostok. Instead, all transactions are handled through a barter system. Every item has a perceived value, and you must balance the "scale" during a trade to complete the transaction.

### Meet the Traders
Currently, there are three primary traders located across the accessible zones. Each specializes in different types of goods.

| Trader | Location | Specialty |
|---|---|---|
| **The Generalist** | Village (Big Barn) | Food, basic tools, furniture, and early-game supplies. |
| **The Doctor** | School (3rd Floor) | High-quality medical supplies, IFAKs, and storage lockers. |
| **Weapon Specialist** | Outpost (Bunker) | Military-grade firearms, armor, and weapon attachments. |

To trade effectively, place the items you want to buy on one side of the scale and your trade goods on the other. Aim for the meter to sit in the center or slightly in the green. Trading high-value weapons for basic food items is a "rip off," so try to match the values appropriately.

## Combat Tactics: Dealing with Bandits

Bandits are the primary threat in the early zones. While they lack military-grade armor, they are incredibly accurate and can end your run in seconds if you are caught in the open. 

### Tactical Engagement Tips
*   **Use the Lean:** Always peak corners using the lean keys (Q and E). This minimizes your profile and allows you to scout rooms safely.
*   **Headshots are King:** Bandits do not wear helmets. Almost any weapon in the game can "one-tap" a bandit with a well-placed headshot.
*   **Break Line of Sight:** If a bandit spots you, immediately fall back behind cover. The longer they see you, the more accurate their shots become.
*   **Audio Cues:** Listen for mumbling or grumbling. Bandits often give away their positions verbally before you see them.

## Shelter Customization and Storage

Your shelter is more than just a respawn point; it is a customizable space where you can organize your progression. By purchasing furniture from traders, you can add functional storage to your home.

1.  **Enter Build Mode:** Press **F1** while inside your shelter.
2.  **Access Catalog:** Press **Tab** to see available furniture like lockers or freezers.
3.  **Functional Storage:** Use lockers (purchased from the Doctor) to store weapons and freezers (from the Generalist) for perishables.

Building a functional shelter is a core part of the **road to vostok extraction guide** philosophy. By organizing your loot, you reduce the downtime between raids and ensure you are always prepared for the next transit into dangerous territory.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I save my progress in Road to Vostok?**

A: Your progress is automatically saved when you enter your shelter. Any loot placed in your storage or furniture will remain there even if you die during your next excursion.

**Q: What happens if I die while transiting between zones?**

A: If you die anywhere in the world, you lose all items currently on your person. You will respawn at your primary shelter in the Village. This is why it is vital to follow a **road to vostok extraction guide** and only carry what you need for the specific task at hand.

**Q: Where can I find more magazines for my weapons?**

A: Magazines can be found as loose loot in POIs (Points of Interest) like houses and crates, or bartered from the Weapon Specialist in the Outpost bunker.

**Q: Is there a way to increase my carry weight?**

A: Yes. You can find or barter for backpacks, waist pouches, and fishing vests. Higher-tier gear significantly increases the amount of loot you can carry before becoming "overweight," which prevents sprinting and stamina regeneration.
