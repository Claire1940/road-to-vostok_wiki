## Understanding the Survival HUD and Status Effects

Before venturing out of your starting cabin, you must familiarize yourself with the complex status indicators located at the bottom of your screen. Unlike traditional shooters, Road to Vostok tracks multiple biological and environmental factors that directly impact your combat effectiveness.

| Status Icon | Meaning | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hydration** | Your current water levels. | Drink juice, soda, or purified water. |
| **Mental** | Your psychological well-being. | Consume "comfort" items like snus, juice, or meatballs. |
| **Energy** | Physical stamina and calorie count. | Eat canned meats, pears, or pea soup. |
| **Body Temp** | Core temperature affected by weather. | Wear insulated clothing or stand near a fire. |
| **Weight** | Carrying capacity (Base 15kg). | Equip backpacks or tactical vests to increase limit. |

Maintaining your mental health is just as critical as your physical health. A low mental state can lead to decreased focus and other negative modifiers. Items like "snus" (chewing tobacco) or high-quality meals provide a significant boost to this stat, making them high-priority loot during your runs through the village.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Bleeding and broken bones are lethal if left untreated. Always keep at least one tourniquet and one splint in your quick-access inventory slots.

## The Generalist Trader and Area 05 Tasks

The heart of the local economy is the Generalist, a jack-of-all-trades trader located in the village garage building. He specializes in common goods and is your primary source for high-tier equipment like the RK95 or heavy backpacks. In the **road to vostok pbs** version, trading is not just about currency; it is about fulfilling specific requests to unlock unique furniture and tools.

1.  **Prime Time:** Bring the Generalist toilet paper. Reward: A functional TV for your cabin.

## Combat Mechanics and AI Behavior

The AI in **road to vostok pbs** is notoriously difficult. Unlike many survival games where NPCs follow predictable paths, these bandits utilize dynamic patrolling. They can hear gunshots from a significant distance and will attempt to flank your last known position.

### Weapon Handling Tips
*   **Inspection (X):** Use this to check your magazine count and weapon condition.
*   **Fire Mode (F):** Toggle between single shot and burst/auto. In the border zone, semi-auto is usually preferred to conserve rare ammo.
*   **Side-Aiming:** You can cant your weapon for a better field of view in close-quarters combat.
*   **Attachment Cog:** When hovering over a weapon in your inventory, a cogwheel icon indicates that you can attach scopes or suppressors.

When engaging enemies, remember that the environment is your best friend. The weather in the **road to vostok pbs** build often includes heavy rain and fog, which can be used to mask your movement. However, this also makes it harder to spot snipers in the trees or second-story windows of the yellow houses.

## Base Customization: Decor Mode

One of the standout features of the current build is the "Decor Mode," accessed by pressing **F1**. This allows you to transform your starting cabin from a bare shack into a functional base of operations.

*   **Furniture Catalog:** Items you find in the world or buy from the Generalist can be added to your permanent catalog.
*   **Placement:** Use the middle mouse button to rotate items and the right mouse button to adjust the rotation axis.
*   **Storage:** Adding cabinets and fridges isn't just for looks; they provide persistent storage for your loot, allowing you to build up a stockpile of ammo and food for the journey to Vostok.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the medical cabinet to store your saline and splints. Keeping your inventory organized allows for faster "grab-and-go" sessions when the weather clears up.

## Advanced Exploration: Crossing the Border

As you move beyond the initial village of Area 05, the difficulty spikes significantly. The transition to the second and third maps requires a high level of "Insulation" to survive the dropping temperatures. The M62 jacket and winter windbreakers are essential upgrades you should seek out before attempting to cross the railroad tracks.

The **road to vostok pbs** experience is designed to be a "shield" that turns into a "sword." You start as a vulnerable survivor, but through careful looting of RK95 rifles and 7.62 magazines, you eventually become the predator. Always keep an eye on the clock; as night falls, visibility drops to near zero, and without a flashlight, you are a sitting duck for the bandits patrolling the coastline.

## FAQ

**Q: Does loot respawn in the road to vostok pbs build?**

A: Yes, loot and AI typically respawn when you exit and re-enter your home cabin or transition between major map zones. This allows you to farm the village for essential trade items like hot plates and magazines.

**Q: How do I increase my carrying capacity?**

A: You must find or purchase backpacks. A standard backpack can add up to 30kg of capacity, bringing your total to 45kg. Note that heavier loads will drain your stamina faster.

**Q: What is the best way to restore Mental health?**

A: Consuming high-quality food (like meatballs or peaches) and using "comfort" items like snus are the most effective ways. Sleeping in your cabin also provides a gradual reset to your mental state.

**Q: Where can I find the Seller Key or Attic Key?**

A: These keys are rare spawns found on bandit corpses or hidden inside residential buildings like the yellow house or the barn. They unlock high-tier loot rooms and shortcuts across the map.
