## Locating Grandma's Cat on the Outpost Map

The mission begins with a notification that Grandma's cat has gone missing after bandits intercepted a nomad caravan. The cat is currently trapped in a sealed and taped-up box somewhere within the Outpost map. Because this area is heavily patrolled by hostile NPCs, you must arrive prepared for medium-range firefights.

The box is typically located in a secondary bunker on the back right side of the Outpost map. Many players mistake the main Gunsmith bunker for the only underground structure, but this specific event guarantees the cat's presence in a mirrored bunker across the field.

### Tactical Steps for the Rescue

| Phase | Objective | Key Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Infiltration** | Reach the Outpost | Approach from the forest to maintain high-ground visibility. |
| **Clearance** | Eliminate Bandits | Target the three main guards near the artillery batteries and the barn. |
| **Discovery** | Locate the Bunker | Head to the back right rock formation and enter the concrete stairs. |
| **Extraction** | Secure the Box | Locate the cat box in the hallway, activate "Cat Vital," and exit. |

> **💡 Tip:** Arrive at the Outpost at approximately 06:00 in-game time. The early morning light provides excellent visibility for spotting bandit silhouettes against the horizon without the blinding glare of the midday sun.

## Cat Vitality and Health Mechanics

Once you pick up the box, the "Cat Vital Activated" status effect will appear on your HUD. This introduces a survival mechanic separate from your own hunger and hydration. The cat’s health starts at 99 and will slowly decrease over time. If the health reaching zero, the cat will die, a permanent consequence that many players strive to avoid.

### Monitoring Health Levels

The cat’s health level is visible in your status menu once the rescue is initiated. It is essential to have food ready before you even leave for the Outpost, as the trek back to the village can be taxing on the cat's vitals.

| Health Range | Status | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **80 - 99** | Healthy | No immediate action; continue to extraction. |
| **40 - 79** | Hungry | Feed upon arrival at the shelter or during a safe pause. |
| **1 - 39** | Critical | Immediate feeding required to prevent permanent loss. |
| **0** | Deceased | The mission fails; the cat cannot be revived. |

## Feeding and Caring for the Cat

After successfully returning to your shelter in the village, you can release the cat from its box. The cat will then inhabit your kitchen or living area. To maintain its health, you must provide specific consumables. The game allows for several different food types, ranging from common scavenged goods to rare animal products.

### Approved Food Items

| Food Type | Rarity | Effectiveness | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Canned Tuna** | Common | Medium | The most reliable food source; easy to stockpile. |
| **Cat Food** | Rare | High | Specifically designed for the cat; restores significant health. |
| **Meat** | Uncommon | Medium | Can be scavenged or traded from the Generalist. |
| **Perch (Fish)** | Rare | High | Requires the fishing mechanic and specific tools to obtain. |

When feeding the cat, a "Do Not Disturb" prompt will appear. It is vital to let the animation finish to ensure the health points are correctly applied. Interestingly, the cat in the **road to vostok 猫** event is worth approximately 2,500 units in trade value, but most players consider the companionship far more valuable than the currency.

## Essential Loot at the Outpost

While your primary goal is the **road to vostok 猫** rescue, the Outpost is rich with high-tier loot that you should collect to make your journey easier. The secondary bunker and the surrounding towers contain military-grade equipment that is often absent from the starting village.

*   **Mosin Rifle:** Often found in the sniper tower overlooking the center field.
*   **Cobra Sight:** A frequent drop on the floor of the guard towers or inside crates.
*   **PM5K:** Check the crates within the bunkers; these are excellent for close-quarters bandit encounters.
*   **Medical Supplies:** Look for painkillers and splints in the bunker where the cat is located.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The transition point to the Minefield is very close to the Outpost. Do not accidentally wander into the Minefield while carrying the cat, as a single misstep will end both your run and the cat's life.

## Shelter Management and Long-term Care

Once the cat is settled, your shelter gains a new layer of interactivity. You can move the cat by using the "Grab" key (default G), allowing you to place it on chairs, tables, or near the heater. This adds to the immersion of [Road to Vostok](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1963610/Road_to_Vostok/), a game that prides itself on realistic atmosphere and high-stakes survival.

As of the 2026 updates, the cat does not currently provide mechanical buffs to the player, but it serves as a persistent save-state tracker and a "moral" objective. Keeping the cat alive through the 10-day event cycles is a challenge in itself, especially as resources like canned tuna become scarcer in the later stages of the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the cat be killed by bandits during the rescue?**

A: No, while the cat is in the box in your inventory, it does not take damage from gunfire. However, if you are killed, you will lose the cat and must restart the mission from your last save or wait for the event to trigger again in a new run.

**Q: Where can I find more canned tuna for the road to vostok 猫?**

A: Canned tuna is most frequently found in the trash bags located behind the school building and in the kitchen cabinets of the houses in the starting village. The Generalist trader also occasionally stocks it in exchange for hardware or ammo.

**Q: Is there a way to automate feeding?**

A: Currently, in the 2026 build, there is no automated feeding system. You must manually interact with the cat in your shelter to replenish its health. It is recommended to check the cat's health every time you return from a scavenging run.

**Q: Does the cat move around the shelter on its own?**

A: The cat has limited AI movement within the room it is placed in. It will occasionally shift positions, but it generally stays within the vicinity of where you last "dropped" it. Future updates may expand its behavior to include more complex interactions.
