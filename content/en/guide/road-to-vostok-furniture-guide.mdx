## Mastering Decor Mode and Placement

The transition from the early demos to the 2026 Early Access version of Road to Vostok brought a massive overhaul to how players interact with their environment. While the original "Attic" shelter allowed for basic item placement, the current system introduces full dynamic furniture manipulation.

To begin customizing your space, you must enter **Decor Mode**. By default, this is mapped to the **F1** key while you are inside a designated shelter. Once activated, every piece of furniture—from heavy wardrobes to small stools—becomes an interactable object. 

### Placement Mechanics and Grid Snapping

When moving objects, the game utilizes a sophisticated shader system to provide visual feedback. A green "valid" shader indicates the item can be placed, while a red "invalid" shader warns of clipping or floating. To assist in creating clean, organized layouts, the system uses **grid snapping**. This is particularly useful for lining up crates against walls or centering a bed in a room without leaving awkward, unusable gaps.

| Action | Control (Default) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Enter/Exit Decor Mode** | **F1** | Toggles the customization interface and object highlights. |
| **Grab/Place Object** | **Left Mouse Button** | Picks up a highlighted piece of furniture or places it down. |
| **Rotate Object** | **R / Mouse Wheel** | Adjusts the orientation of the furniture before placement. |
| **Adjust Distance** | **Mouse Scroll** | Moves the object closer to or further from the player character. |
| **Grid Snapping** | **Automatic** | Aligns objects to surfaces and nearby furniture to prevent gaps. |

> **💡 Tip:** Wall elements like shelves or paintings have specific logic. They will only show a "valid" placement shader when hovered over a vertical surface.

## The Furniture Catalog System

One of the most innovative features in the 2026 update is the **Furniture Catalog**. Redecorating a small shelter can quickly become frustrating if the floor is cluttered with large objects. The Catalog serves as a temporary "pocket dimension" for your furniture, allowing you to clear a room entirely before deciding on a new layout.

When in Decor Mode, you can "store" furniture into the Catalog. This interface uses a **Tetris-style inventory**, similar to the main player inventory. Large items like sofas or beds take up significant blocks of space, while smaller items like stools or nightstands are much easier to slot in.

### Benefits of the Catalog:
1.  **Redecoration Ease:** Clear out an entire room to visualize a new floor plan without moving items one by one.
2.  **Furniture Transfer:** Furniture stored in the Catalog is accessible across different shelters. This means you can move your favorite bed from the starting Cabin to a more advanced shelter later in the game.
3.  **Organization:** It acts as a secondary storage for furniture you aren't currently using but want to keep for future outposts.

## Item Grouping and Storage Logic

A common complaint in early survival games is the need to empty a container before moving it. Road to Vostok solves this through **Item Grouping**. When you place lootable items—such as a lantern, a tool, or a weapon—onto a piece of furniture, those items become "parented" to that furniture.

If you pick up a shelf in Decor Mode, every item sitting on that shelf will move with it, maintaining its relative position. This allows for rapid reorganization of your storage areas without the tedious process of micro-managing every individual loot item.

### Storage Capacity and Containers
The furniture system is directly tied to the game's container logic. Almost any large piece of furniture can act as a storage vessel. By placing multiple wardrobes, crates, and shelves, you can expand your shelter's storage capacity to hold hundreds of unique items. This is essential for players who prefer to "hoard" medical supplies or ammunition for long-range excursions into the Vostok zone.

| Furniture Type | Primary Function | Interaction Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Beds/Sleeping Pads** | Save Point / Rest | Static Surface |
| **Shelves/Tables** | Item Display / Storage | Grouping Surface |
| **Wardrobes/Fridges** | High-Capacity Storage | Internal Container |
| **Carpets** | Aesthetics | Overlap-Capable |
| **Light Switches** | Mood/Visibility | Interactive Toggle |

## How to Acquire New Furniture

You aren't limited to the default items found in your starting shelter. There are three primary ways to expand your collection:

### 1. Furniture Crafting
Within your shelter, you can access the **Crafting Menu**. Under the "Furniture" tab, you will find various recipes. Note that furniture crafting has a **shelter proximity requirement**; you cannot build a bed while out in the wilderness. You must be within the bounds of your home base.

| Craftable Item | Required Materials | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Military Bed** | 2x Pillows, 2x Sleeping Pads, 1x Blanket, 1x Sleeping Bag | High-tier rest point. |
| **Single Bed** | 1x Lumber, 1x Blanket, 1x Mattress Roll, 1x Pillow | Standard rest point. |
| **Wall Shelf** | 2x Lumber, 1x Nails | Basic item grouping surface. |

### 2. Trading and Bartering
Traders in the world of Road to Vostok now carry furniture supplies. While a generalist trader might have a random fridge or nightstand, specialized traders like "Grandma" are planned to carry specific categories like carpets, fabrics, and home comforts. Trading for furniture is often expensive and may require significant inventory space to transport the "packaged" version of the item back to your shelter.

### 3. Scavenging and Looting
While less common for large pieces, smaller decorative items and certain utility furniture can be found in the world. Keep an eye out for rare paintings or specialized containers in high-tier loot zones.

> **Warning:** Always ensure your path home is clear when transporting furniture from a trader. Being encumbered by a heavy furniture item makes you a slow and easy target for hostile NPCs.

## Multi-Shelter Strategy

Road to Vostok supports **multi-shelter gameplay**, meaning you can maintain several bases simultaneously across the map. This is a game-changer for players looking to push deep into the zone.

-   **The Main Hub:** Usually the starting Cabin, where you keep the bulk of your crafted furniture and long-term storage.
-   **Bugout Shelters:** Smaller locations, like the Tent, placed near high-value loot zones or extraction points.
-   **Resource Management:** Use the Furniture Catalog to ferry essential utility items (like a crafting bench or a high-tier bed) between these locations to ensure you always have a safe place to recover.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I place furniture anywhere in the world?**

A: No, furniture placement and the Decor Mode are restricted to designated shelter zones. You can, however, place small loot items anywhere using the standard item placement feature.

**Q: What happens to my items if I delete a piece of furniture?**

A: If you move a piece of furniture to the Catalog, the items grouped with it will generally stay attached or be placed into a temporary overflow if the furniture is "packaged." It is always safer to manually clear a container before converting it back into a recipe or trading it.

**Q: Is there a limit to how much furniture I can have in one shelter?**

A: While there is no hard "count" limit, your physical space and the Tetris-style Furniture Catalog act as natural limiters. Overcrowding a shelter can make it difficult to navigate during an emergency.

**Q: How do I get more crafting recipes for this road to vostok furniture guide?**

A: Recipes are unlocked by finding blueprints in the world, increasing your reputation with specific traders, or simply by having the required base materials in your inventory while near a workbench.

**Q: Can I customize the lighting in my shelter?**

A: Yes. Most permanent shelters come with light switches that allow you to toggle different light sources. This can help set a "moody" atmosphere or provide maximum visibility for organizing your loot.
