## Finding the Outpost Bunker

The search for the cat begins at the Outpost map. Unlike some loot spawns that are randomized, the cat is tied to a specific world event that typically triggers around Day 4 or Day 5 of your survival run. You will see a notification or event log stating that "Grandma's cat has gone missing" and that bandits have acquired the box she was kept in.

The **road to vostok cat location** is situated in a secondary bunker on the back right side of the Outpost map. Many players miss this area because they focus on the primary bunker where the Gunsmith and the main shelter are located. To find it, you must navigate past the central fields and head toward the eastern boundary of the map.

| Location Feature | Description | Importance |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Bunker** | Houses the Gunsmith and initial trader. | Starting point for navigation. |
| **Back Right Bunker** | The specific **road to vostok cat location**. | Contains the cat in a taped-up box. |
| **Central Tower** | A high vantage point near the middle of the map. | Good for spotting bandit patrols. |
| **Eastern Boundary** | The edge of the map near the minefields. | The bunker is tucked against these rocks. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always clear the surrounding woods of bandits before entering the bunker. The AI can be aggressive and may bottleneck you inside the narrow hallways if you are spotted.

## Step-by-Step Rescue Guide

Once you arrive at the correct bunker on the back right side of the map, the process of rescuing the cat is straightforward but requires inventory management. The cat is found inside a sealed, taped-up box located deep within the bunker's storage rooms.

1.  **Enter the Bunker:** Locate the concrete entrance tucked into the hillside. It looks nearly identical to the Gunsmith's bunker.
2.  **Clear the Hallways:** Check the corners for any lingering hostiles. Bandits often use these bunkers as shelters.
3.  **Locate the Box:** Search the back rooms. You will find a cardboard box on the floor. Interacting with it will trigger the "Rescue Cat" prompt.
4.  **Activate Vitals:** As soon as you pick up the box, the "Cat Vital" system is activated. You will now see a health percentage for the cat in your UI.
5.  **Exfiltrate Safely:** You must carry the box back to your home shelter. Be extremely careful during the trek back, as dying with the box may result in losing the cat permanently for that run.

## Managing Cat Vitals and Health

The cat is not just a cosmetic addition to your shelter; it is a living entity that requires maintenance. Upon rescue, the cat’s health starts at 99%. This number will slowly decrease over time, representing the cat's hunger and general well-being. If the health reaches zero, the cat will die, and the "home" atmosphere of your shelter will be lost.

To maintain health, you must feed the cat using specific consumable items found throughout the world. In the current 2026 build of [Road to Vostok](https://roadtovostok.com/), there are four primary food sources for your pet.

| Food Item | Availability | Health Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| **Cat Food** | Rare (found in bags/crates) | High |
| **Canned Tuna** | Common (Generalist/Loot) | Moderate |
| **Raw Meat** | Harvested from wildlife | Low |
| **Perch (Fish)** | Obtained via fishing | Moderate |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not forget to stock up on Canned Tuna. While Cat Food provides a better boost, Tuna is much easier to find at the Generalist trader or in residential trash bags.

## Combat and Navigation Tips

The journey to the **road to vostok cat location** is fraught with danger. The Outpost is a high-traffic area for bandits, and the transition points between maps can be literal death traps if you aren't prepared. 

### Dealing with Bandits
Bandits at the Outpost often carry high-tier weaponry like the K-74U or MP5K. Before you reach the bunker, use the high ground to scout the area. There is a sniper tower in the center of the map that occasionally spawns a Mosin or other long-range optics. Clearing the tower first gives you a significant tactical advantage.

### Avoiding Minefields
The eastern edge of the Outpost, near where the cat's bunker is located, is dangerously close to the transition point for the Minefield map. Stay on the marked trails as much as possible. Wandering too far off the path in search of the bunker can lead you directly into a landmine, ending your rescue mission instantly.

### Shelter Setup
Once you return home, place the cat box in a safe area, such as the kitchen or near your bed. After a short period, the box will open, and the cat will begin to roam your shelter. You can interact with the cat to check its health status and feed it directly from your inventory.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the cat be killed by bandits?**

A: No, once the cat is safely inside your shelter, it is protected from external combat damage. However, it can still die from neglect if you fail to keep its health percentage above zero by feeding it.

**Q: Where is the best place to find food for the cat?**

A: The Generalist trader in the village often stocks Canned Tuna. Additionally, searching trash bags and kitchen cabinets in the School or Village maps is a reliable way to find Cat Food and Tuna without spending currency.

**Q: Does the cat provide any gameplay buffs?**

A: While the cat does not currently provide mechanical buffs like increased damage or stamina, it significantly boosts the "Mental Health" or "Comfort" aesthetic of the shelter, which is a core part of the game's atmospheric progression in 2026.

**Q: What happens if I lose the box before reaching home?**

A: If you drop the box or die while carrying it, you will need to return to the **road to vostok cat location** at the Outpost to see if the item has respawned. If the event timer has expired, you may have to wait for the next "Grandma's Cat" event to trigger.

**Q: Can I trade the cat to vendors?**

A: While the cat has a high trade value (approximately 2500), most players advise against selling it. The cat is a rare world event item, and the emotional value of having a pet in the wasteland far outweighs the temporary influx of cash.
