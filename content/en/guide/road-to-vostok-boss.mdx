### Weapon Stances and Tactical Reloads

Weapon stances significantly impact your combat readiness and arm fatigue. A high weapon stance keeps your firearm ready to fire, crucial for entering dangerous areas, but drains stamina. Manual reloading, especially for weapons like shotguns and Mosins, requires opening the chamber and loading each round, then racking the bolt. This realism adds a layer of challenge, making practice in the tutorial area essential.

| Weapon Mechanic    | Description                                                                 | Tactical Advantage                                                                 |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Scope Adjustment** | Change zoom level (closer/further) on your gun.                             | Adapt to varying engagement distances (long to CQB) quickly.                        |
| **Fire Selector**    | Switch between single fire and full auto modes.                             | Conserve ammo or unleash suppressive fire based on the situation.                  |
| **Weapon Stances**   | High (ready), Low (relaxed). Mouse scroll wheel to switch.                  | Quick reaction time in dangerous zones; manage arm fatigue in safe areas.          |
| **Caned Stance**     | Tilting weapon for laser aim with long-range scope.                         | Rapid target acquisition in close quarters while maintaining long-range optics.      |
| **Manual Reload**    | Load individual rounds, rack bolt for shotguns/Mosins.                      | Realistic combat; requires practice to avoid fatal delays.                         |

Finding and utilizing the right gear can make all the difference. Torches like the Arva or the more powerful Polaris head torch are indispensable in dark areas. Night vision goggles, found in military locations, offer a significant advantage in low-light combat. Crafting is also a vital survival skill, allowing you to create essential items like splints from sticks and rags, saving valuable trips back to a doctor.
