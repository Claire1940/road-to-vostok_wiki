## Core Mechanics of the Road to Vostok Health System

At its most fundamental level, your character possesses a total of **100 Health Points (HP)**. While this might sound standard for the genre, the lethality of the AI and the environment makes this pool feel incredibly shallow. Unlike arcade shooters where you can soak up dozens of rounds, the **road to vostok health system** is designed for quick, brutal engagements.

Each time you are struck by a standard caliber round from an AI opponent, you typically lose **25 Health**. This creates a "four-strike rule" where just four successful hits from an enemy will result in immediate death. Because combat often occurs in dense foliage or dilapidated structures, these four hits can happen in a matter of seconds if you are caught out of cover.

| Attribute | Value | Impact on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Total Health** | 100 HP | Maximum capacity for survival. |
| **Damage per Shot** | ~25 HP | Standard AI damage; 4 shots to kill. |
| **Bleeding** | Variable | Continuous HP loss until treated (planned feature). |
| **Limb Damage** | TBD | Affects movement and weapon sway. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** In the Vostok region, death is permanent. If your health reaches zero, your entire save file is deleted. Always prioritize health over loot when exploring high-risk zones.

## Healing and Consumables: The Power of Pea Soup

In the current 2026 builds and demos, the primary method for restoring your vitality is a staple of Finnish culture: **Pea Soup**. While the developer plans to expand the medical system with bandages, splints, and advanced medkits, the humble can of soup remains your most reliable tool for staying in the fight.

Each use of a pea soup can restores **10% of your total health** (10 HP). Because healing is incremental, you cannot simply "pop" a single item to return to full strength after a heavy firefight. You must carry multiple cans and find a secure location to consume them.

| Item | Health Restored | Rarity | Usage Tip |

| **Red Cans** | Variable | Rare | Harder to find; keep for emergency extraction runs. |

To maximize your efficiency within the **road to vostok health system**, you should perform "loot runs" specifically targeting kitchens and residential areas. Gathering a stockpile of soup allows you to test weapons and AI behavior with a safety net, ensuring you don't have to restart your run after a single mistake.

## The Stamina Connection: Body vs. Arm

A unique aspect of the **road to vostok health system** is how it links physical exhaustion to combat performance. Your character manages two distinct stamina pools: **Body Stamina** and **Arm Stamina**. If either is depleted, your ability to defend yourself—and thus maintain your health—is severely compromised.

### Body Stamina
This pool governs your movement. Road to Vostok features two running states:
1.  **Light Jog:** Drains body stamina slowly. It does not affect your arm stamina unless you reach zero body stamina while holding a weapon.
2.  **Full Tilt Run:** A high-speed sprint that drains both body and arm stamina simultaneously.

When your body stamina hits zero, your character becomes "Exhausted." In this state, your arm stamina will plummet, making it nearly impossible to aim accurately. To recover, you must stop moving or drop to a walk to let your character catch their breath.

### Arm Stamina
Arm stamina is perhaps the most critical component of the **road to vostok health system** during combat. Holding your weapon in a high-ready position or aiming down sights (ADS) drains this bar. 

*   **Hip Firing:** Drains arm stamina as you struggle to keep the weapon steady.
*   **Full Auto Fire:** Drains arm stamina rapidly. For example, emptying a full magazine from an M416 can drain nearly 30% of your arm stamina just from the recoil management and weight.
*   **Weapon Sway:** As arm stamina decreases, your weapon sway increases. At zero stamina, your aim will move in large "figure-eight" patterns, making it impossible to land shots.

> **💡 Tip:** To quickly regain arm stamina, switch to your secondary weapon or put your weapon away (holster) while sprinting. This allows your arms to rest even while your legs are working.

## Tactical Movement to Preserve Health

Because the **road to vostok health system** is so punishing, avoiding damage is always better than healing it. The game's movement mechanics offer several "hidden" ways to stay safe from AI fire.

### Exploiting AI Aiming
Currently, the AI in Road to Vostok has difficulty adjusting their aim downward. If you find yourself in a close-quarters engagement, **crouching** can often cause the AI to overshoot, giving them "Stormtrooper aim" while you remain relatively safe. This is a vital tactic when your health is low and you are trying to reach an extraction point.

### Inertia and Crouching
You can transfer your sprinting inertia into a crouch-slide. By hitting the crouch key while at a Full Tilt run, you can slide under obstacles or into cover much faster than a standard walk. Additionally, jumping and then crouching mid-air allows you to land in a low profile, which is excellent for disappearing behind low stone walls or ridges.

### Step Height and Gaps
Your movement speed dictates your "step height." When running at full speed, your character can clear larger gaps and step over higher obstacles (like rocks or debris) that would otherwise block you. Use this to navigate the verticality of the Finnish terrain and stay out of the AI's line of sight.

| Movement Action | Body Stamina Cost | Arm Stamina Cost | Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Light Jog** | Low | None | Sustainable travel. |
| **Full Tilt Run** | High | Low | Maximum speed; clears gaps. |
| **Crouch Slide** | Medium | None | Fast transition to cover. |
| **ADS Aiming** | None | Medium | High precision. |

## Weapon Handling and Its Impact on Survival

The way you handle your weapon directly influences how much damage you take. The faster you can neutralize a threat, the less likely you are to engage with the medical side of the **road to vostok health system**.

### Canted Aiming
Road to Vostok allows for **canted aiming** (tilting the firearm) by clicking the middle mouse button. This provides a clearer field of view in close quarters compared to traditional optics. Use this when clearing houses to ensure you see the AI before they see you.

### Attachment Management
Utilizing the attachment system is key to preserving stamina and health.
*   **Muzzle Devices:** Can reduce recoil, which in turn reduces the arm stamina drain during full-auto fire.
*   **Optics:** While they provide better range, they typically take longer to "swap" or aim with, potentially leaving you vulnerable.
*   **Lasers:** Essential for hip-firing. Since hip-firing drains arm stamina, having a laser allows you to land shots quickly without spending precious seconds (and stamina) aiming down sights.

## Staying Healthy: A Summary of Best Practices

To survive the Border Zone in 2026, follow these rules for managing the **road to vostok health system**:

1.  **Always Carry Soup:** Never leave your shelter without at least 3-4 cans of pea soup.
2.  **Short Bursts:** When firing full-auto, use short bursts to keep your arm stamina high and your aim steady.
3.  **Holster to Sprint:** If you need to cover a long distance, put your weapon away to preserve arm stamina for the fight at the end of the run.
4.  **Check Your Corners:** Use canted aiming to peek around doorways. The first person to shoot usually wins the encounter in this 4-shot-kill environment.
5.  **Know Your Zone:** Remember that in Area 5, you lose your inventory on death, but in Vostok, you lose everything. Adjust your aggressiveness accordingly.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I heal in Road to Vostok?**

A: Currently, the primary way to heal is by consuming Finnish pea soup. Each can restores 10% (10 HP) of your total health. Future updates are expected to add more traditional medical supplies like bandages and medkits.

**Q: What happens when my arm stamina reaches zero?**

A: When arm stamina is depleted, your weapon sway becomes extreme, making it nearly impossible to hit targets. This happens more quickly if you are also out of body stamina or if you are firing in full-auto mode.

**Q: Does weight affect the road to vostok health system?**

A: Yes, carrying a heavier load increases the rate at which your body stamina drains while running and increases the arm stamina drain when holding your weapon up. Managing your inventory weight is crucial for maintaining combat readiness.

**Q: Can I recover health automatically over time?**

A: No, there is no passive health regeneration in the **road to vostok health system**. You must manually use items like pea soup to restore any lost HP. Extraction to a transition point will also refill your health and clear exhaustion.
