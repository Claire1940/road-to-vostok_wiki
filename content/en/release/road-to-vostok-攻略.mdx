## Initial Setup and Input Optimization

Before stepping foot outside your shelter, your first priority should be fine-tuning your settings. Road to Vostok is exceptionally well-optimized for 2026 hardware, allowing most players to run the game on Ultra settings for maximum immersion. However, the default control scheme may feel unintuitive for some.

One of the most frequent recommendations from top players is to rebind the **Interact** key. By default, some functions may be mapped to the middle mouse button, but changing your primary interaction to **'F'** provides a much more fluid experience during high-pressure looting situations. Additionally, ensure your look, aim, and scope sensitivities are adjusted based on your mouse DPI to ensure consistent shot placement.

## Understanding the Shelter: The Time Capsule Mechanic

The shelter in Road to Vostok acts as your only sanctuary and your primary save point. It is helpful to view the shelter as a "time capsule." While you are inside, the outside world effectively does not exist. The moment you step out of your door, the entire game world—including AI bandits, soldiers, and loot—is procedurally spawned or reset.

This means that every time you leave your shelter, you are entering a fresh instance of the map. If you loot a building and return to your shelter to save, the loot in that same building will have respawned (and likely changed) when you head back out. This mechanic is vital for farming specific resources or equipment.

### Shelter Management and Building
Using the **F1** key allows you to enter the shelter customization mode. This system is surprisingly robust, allowing you to place furniture, containers, and workstations to organize your gear.

| Action | Keybind | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Enter Edit Mode** | **F1** | Opens the furniture and layout menu. |
| **Place Item** | **G** | Sets the highlighted piece of furniture. |
| **Remove Item** | **F** | Returns the item to your catalog. |
| **Rotate Item** | **Right Mouse** | Adjusts the orientation and distance of the object. |

## Weapon Handling and Combat Tactics

Combat in Road to Vostok is lethal and grounded in realism. One of the biggest hurdles for new players is the manual operation of specific firearms like the Kar98 or various shotguns. Unlike standard arcade shooters, these weapons often require manual cycling or individual shell loading.

If you find yourself unable to fire or reload, you likely need to use the manual interaction system. By tapping **Control**, you take your hand off the grip, allowing you to manually feed rounds into the chamber with the **Left Mouse Button**. Once loaded, you must tap **Control** again to resume your firing stance and press **R** to chamber the round.

| Weapon Type | Loading Mechanic | Critical Keybinds |
|---|---|---|
| **Automatic Rifles** | Magazine-based | **R** to reload, **V** to check fire mode. |
| **Bolt-Action / Shotguns** | Manual Single Load | **Ctrl** (Free hand) -> **LMB** (Insert) -> **R** (Cycle). |
| **Sidearms** | Magazine-based | Standard reload, but check for chambered rounds. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Death is permanent for your gear. If you die in the field, you lose every item on your person. There is no "running back to your body" in Road to Vostok.

## The Economy: Traders and the Tax System

Progression in the game is heavily tied to your relationship with the Trader. In the starting area (Area 5), you can find the Trader by following the railway tracks. However, the economy is not a simple buy-and-sell system. It is governed by a **Tax Percentage** that can significantly inflate prices.

When you first encounter a Trader, you may notice that items cost double their base value. This is due to a high initial tax. To lower this tax and unlock better equipment like armor plating, helmets, and high-tier rifles, you must complete specific tasks for the Trader.

1.  **Locate the Trader:** Follow the train tracks from your initial shelter.
2.  **Check the Tax:** View the percentage in the top corner of the trade menu.
3.  **Complete Tasks:** Accept missions to reduce the tax and earn rewards.
4.  **Barter Wisely:** Items have specific values; bring high-value loot to offset the high early-game costs.

## Advanced Survival: Fishing and Medical Care

Survival isn't just about winning gunfights; it's about managing your physical condition. In 2026, the medical system remains one of the most punishing aspects of the game. If you are shot, you must identify the type of injury. Bleeding requires bandages, while severe trauma like headshots requires a full medkit.

If you are injured and lack the proper supplies, your best bet is to sprint back to your shelter. Even if you are bleeding out, you can drop your most valuable items on the shelter floor before you die, ensuring they don't disappear when your character resets.

### Fishing for Resources
Fishing is an excellent way to generate income and food without risking a firefight. You can find fish in the water bodies near your shelter. If you are struggling to find a "bite," you can actually swim into the water to visually locate schools of fish. Once located, throw your line directly over that spot to guarantee a catch.

| Survival Activity | Benefit | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Fishing** | Safe source of income/food | Fishing Rod and specific water blocks. |
| **Looting** | Gear progression | Randomized; resets upon shelter entry. |
| **Healing** | Prevents permanent death | Specific medical items (Bandages, Medkits). |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I save my progress in Road to Vostok?**

A: The only way to save your game and your current loot is to physically enter your shelter. Once you step inside, your progress is recorded. Note that leaving the shelter resets the world outside.

**Q: What happens when I die?**

A: Death results in the total loss of all items currently equipped on your character and in your backpack. Items stored inside your shelter containers remain safe. This makes a **road to vostok 攻略** essential for minimizing risk.

**Q: Why can't I reload my shotgun?**

A: Certain weapons require manual loading. You must tap the **Control** key to free your hand, then use the **Left Mouse Button** to manually insert shells into the weapon before cycling the action with **R**.

**Q: Where is the Trader located?**

A: In the starting map, you can find the Trader by following the railway tracks (train tracks). He is usually located in a fortified position along the line. Be careful, as bandits often patrol the areas near the tracks.
