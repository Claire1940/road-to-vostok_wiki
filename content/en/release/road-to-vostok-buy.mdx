## Understanding the Hardcore Survival Loop

Road to Vostok is segmented into distinct zones, each increasing in difficulty and risk. Your journey begins in **Area 05**, a southeastern Finland evacuation zone. This area serves as your base of operations where you can learn the physics-based looting system and interact with traders like the "Generalist."

### Survival Statistics and Management
Maintaining your character's physical and mental state is just as important as having a loaded rifle. The game utilizes a "Tetris-style" inventory management system where every item's size and weight matter.

| Stat | Impact on Gameplay | Recovery Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Health** | Determines physical survival | Bandages, Splints, Saline |
| **Energy** | Affects stamina and carry weight | Food (Meatballs, Peaches) |
| **Hydration** | Impacts movement speed | Water, Juice, Soda |
| **Mental** | Affects aim stability and focus | Snacks, Cigarettes, Snus |

> **💡 Tip:** Bad weather and poor-quality food will rapidly deplete your Mental stat. Always carry "comfort items" like chocolate or cigarettes to stay focused during long raids.

## The Three Pillars of the World Map

The world is divided into three primary regions. Understanding the rules of each is essential for anyone who has just completed a **road to vostok buy** and is starting their first run.

1.  **The Safe Zone (Area 05):** Here, death is a setback but not an end. If you die, you lose the gear on your person, but your safehouse and stored loot remain intact.
2.  **The Border Zone:** This is the gateway to Russia. It is guarded by well-equipped border guards and littered with hazards like mines. You must often use specialized equipment or boats to cross.
3.  **Vostok (The Permadeath Zone):** Once you cross the border into Vostok, the stakes reach their maximum. If your character dies here, the save file is effectively wiped. You lose your character, your shelters, and all stored loot.

| Zone | Risk Level | Death Penalty | Primary Enemies |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Area 05** | Low/Medium | Lost Carried Gear | Bandits |
| **Border** | High | Lost Carried Gear | Guards / Mines |
| **Vostok** | Extreme | **Full Progress Reset** | Military / Vehicles |

## Trading, Bartering, and the Economy

One of the most unique aspects of the game is the lack of a traditional currency system. Trading is based entirely on the perceived value of items in a post-apocalyptic setting. Medical supplies, tools, and even mundane items like toilet paper hold immense bartering power.

Traders like the **Generalist** provide tasks that are essential for progression. For example, a common early-game task involves finding toilet paper in exchange for a functional TV for your shelter. These tasks are the primary way to unlock better gear and new safehouses.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Traders operate on a real-time resupply schedule. If a trader is out of ammunition or medical supplies, you may need to wait several minutes or scavenge elsewhere before they restock.

## Combat Realism and Weapon Handling

For FPS enthusiasts, the appeal of a **road to vostok buy** lies in the mechanical depth of the gunplay. The developer has prioritized "diegetic" UI, meaning most information is conveyed through character animations rather than pop-up menus.

*   **Weapon Inspection:** Pressing 'X' allows you to physically check your magazine and fire mode.
*   **Real Calibers:** You must match specific ammunition types (e.g., 9x19mm, 7.62x39mm) to the correct magazines and firearms.
*   **Physics-Based Looting:** Weapons and attachments do not just "spawn" in menus; they exist as physical objects in the world that must be manually picked up and placed in your inventory.
*   **Customization:** Using the **Decor Mode (F1)**, you can customize your shelters by moving furniture, placing weapons on racks, and organizing your loot visually.

## Scavenging and Inventory Strategy

When you first enter the world, your carry capacity is limited. Finding a backpack is a priority, as it can increase your carrying capacity from the base 15kg up to 45kg or more. Items like "Hot Plates" and "Tackle Boxes" are valuable for trade but take up significant space.

| Item Type | Example | Value/Utility |
|---|---|---|
| **Medical** | Tourniquet | Stops heavy bleeding; high trade value |
| **Tools** | Nails / Lumber | Used for shelter upgrades and tasks |
| **Electronics** | Hot Plate | High barter value (approx. 250 units) |
| **Valuables** | Religious Books | Extremely rare; worth 1300+ units |

Managing your inventory is a constant puzzle. You must balance the need for ammunition and food against the desire to bring back valuable loot for trade. Successful players often establish "loot runs" along the coastline or through village clusters, staying close to cover to avoid the surprisingly intelligent AI.
