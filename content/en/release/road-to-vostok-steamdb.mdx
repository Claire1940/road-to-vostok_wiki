## Survival Systems: Shelters and the Event Calendar

A unique aspect of Road to Vostok is the multi-shelter support. Unlike the demo, where players were confined to a single base, the Early Access world contains five different shelters scattered across the maps. These shelters serve as your primary hubs for crafting, storage, and customization.

### Shelter Customization
The "Decor Mode" allows for total freedom in arranging your living space. Players can place furniture, organize loot displays, and utilize the new tools UI to manage their tasks. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Shelters are safe zones, but the paths between them are not. Always plan your route and check your world map before moving heavy gear between different shelters.

### The Event Calendar
The world simulation progresses based on the number of days you have survived. This is tracked via the Event Calendar in the tools UI. As time passes, the game world dynamically changes:
*   **New Traders:** Specific NPCs may only appear after a certain survival threshold.
*   **Dynamic Events:** Faction-based skirmishes or environmental hazards that trigger at specific times.
*   **Post Events:** Changes to the map layout or loot tables following major world shifts.

## Advanced Survival Tactics for 2026

To succeed in the current build of Road to Vostok, players must master several advanced systems introduced in the transition from the final demo.

### Magazine and Weapon Management
The magazine system requires manual loading and management. You cannot simply press a reload button to magically refill your ammo. You must find, fill, and organize magazines in your tactical vest. Lasers and grenades have also been integrated, providing more options for clearing rooms in the School or Apartment maps.

### Fishing and Sustenance
Survival isn't just about combat. The inclusion of fishing provides a renewable food source, provided you can find a safe spot near water. Combined with the dynamic weather system, players must balance the need for calories with the risk of exposure during rain or snowstorms.
